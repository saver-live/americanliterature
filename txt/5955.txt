<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;TO JOHN GREENLEAF WHITTIER

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Three Silences there are: the first of speech,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The second of desire, the third of thought;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This is the lore a Spanish monk, distraught
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With dreams and visions, was the first to teach.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These Silences, commingling each with each,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Made up the perfect Silence, that he sought
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And prayed for, and wherein at times he caught
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mysterious sounds from realms beyond our reach.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O thou, whose daily life anticipates
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The life to come, and in whose thought and word
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The spiritual world preponderates.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hermit of Amesbury! thou too hast heard
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Voices and melodies from beyond the gates,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And speakest only when thy soul is stirred!
</pre>
