<pre>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The twilight is sad and cloudy,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The wind blows wild and free,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And like the wings of sea-birds
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flash the white caps of the sea.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But in the fisherman's cottage
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There shines a ruddier light,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And a little face at the window
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Peers out into the night.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Close, close it is pressed to the window,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As if those childish eyes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Were looking into the darkness,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To see some form arise.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And a woman's waving shadow
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is passing to and fro,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now rising to the ceiling,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now bowing and bending low.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What tale do the roaring ocean,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the night-wind, bleak and wild,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As they beat at the crazy casement,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tell to that little child?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And why do the roaring ocean,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the night-wind, wild and bleak,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As they beat at the heart of the mother,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drive the color from her cheek?
</pre>
