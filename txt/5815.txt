<pre>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is the Harvest Moon! On gilded vanes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And roofs of villages, on woodland crests
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And their aerial neighborhoods of nests
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Deserted, on the curtained window-panes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of rooms where children sleep, on country lanes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And harvest-fields, its mystic splendor rests!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gone are the birds that were our summer guests,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the last sheaves return the laboring wains!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All things are symbols: the external shows
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of Nature have their image in the mind,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As flowers and fruits and falling of the leaves;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The song-birds leave us at the summer's close,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Only the empty nests are left behind,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And pipings of the quail among the sheaves.
</pre>
