&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As a fond mother, when the day is o'er,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leads by the hand her little child to bed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Half willing, half reluctant to be led,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And leave his broken playthings on the floor,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Still gazing at them through the open door,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor wholly reassured and comforted
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By promises of others in their stead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which, though more splendid, may not please him more;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So Nature deals with us, and takes away
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Our playthings one by one, and by the hand
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leads us to rest so gently, that we go
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Scarce knowing if we wish to go or stay,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Being too full of sleep to understand
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How far the unknown transcends the what we know.
