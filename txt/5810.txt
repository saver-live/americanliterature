<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Torrent of light and river of the air,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Along whose bed the glimmering stars are seen
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like gold and silver sands in some ravine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where mountain streams have left their channels bare!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Spaniard sees in thee the pathway, where
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His patron saint descended in the sheen
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of his celestial armor, on serene
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And quiet nights, when all the heavens were fair.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not this I see, nor yet the ancient fable
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of Phaeton's wild course, that scorched the skies
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where'er the hoofs of his hot coursers trod;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the white drift of worlds o'er chasms of sable,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The star-dust that is whirled aloft and flies
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the invisible chariot-wheels of God.
</pre>
