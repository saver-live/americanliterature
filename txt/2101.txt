<p>As virtuous men pass mildly away,<br> And whisper to their souls to go,<br> Whilst some of their sad friends do say,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>"Now his breath goes," and some say, "No."<br> <br> So let us melt, and make no noise,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>No tear-floods, nor sigh-tempests move;<br> 'Twere profanation of our joys<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>To tell the laity our love.<br> <br> Moving of th' earth brings harms and fears;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Men reckon what it did, and meant;<br> But trepidation of the spheres,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Though greater far, is innocent.<br> <br> Dull sublunary lovers' love<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>—Whose soul is sense—cannot admit<br> Of absence, 'cause it doth remove<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>The thing which elemented it.<br> <br> But we by a love so much refined,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>That ourselves know not what it is,<br> Inter-assurèd of the mind,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Care less, eyes, lips and hands to miss.<br> <br> Our two souls therefore, which are one,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Though I must go, endure not yet<br> A breach, but an expansion,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Like gold to aery thinness beat.<br> <br> If they be two, they are two so<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>As stiff twin compasses are two;<br> Thy soul, the fix'd foot, makes no show<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>To move, but doth, if th' other do.<br> <br> And though it in the centre sit,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Yet, when the other far doth roam,<br> It leans, and hearkens after it,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>And grows erect, as that comes home.<br> <br> Such wilt thou be to me, who must,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Like th' other foot, obliquely run;<br> Thy firmness makes my circle just<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>And makes me end where I begun.</p>
