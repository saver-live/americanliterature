<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A gentle boy, with soft and silken locks
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A dreamy boy, with brown and tender eyes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A castle-builder, with his wooden blocks,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And towers that touch imaginary skies.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A fearless rider on his father's knee,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;An eager listener unto stories told
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At the Round Table of the nursery,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of heroes and adventures manifold.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There will be other towers for thee to build;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There will be other steeds for thee to ride;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There will be other legends, and all filled
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With greater marvels and more glorified.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Build on, and make thy castles high and fair,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rising and reaching upward to the skies;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Listen to voices in the upper air,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor lose thy simple faith in mysteries.
</pre>
