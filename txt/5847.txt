<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As one who long hath fled with panting breath
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Before his foe, bleeding and near to fall,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I turn and set my back against the wall,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And look thee in the face, triumphant Death,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I call for aid, and no one answereth;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am alone with thee, who conquerest all;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet me thy threatening form doth not appall,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For thou art but a phantom and a wraith.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wounded and weak, sword broken at the hilt,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With armor shattered, and without a shield,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I stand unmoved; do with me what thou wilt;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I can resist no more, but will not yield.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This is no tournament where cowards tilt;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The vanquished here is victor of the field.
</pre>
