<p><cite class="al-title">The Tragedy of Julius Caesar</cite> (1599) is Shakespeare's tragedy about the events leading to the murder of Julius Caesar. Two tribunes, Marullus and Flavius, break up the Roman citizens attending a victory parade for Julius Caesar. Characters include Mark Anthony (his one and only friend), Marcus Brutus, Octavia, and Calpurnia (Caesar's wife). A soothsayer forewarns Caesar's fate: <i>"Beware the Ides of March"</i>. Another favorite quote: <i>"Cry 'Havoc!' and let slip the dogs of war."</i> <i>[Painting by Edward Scriven, Brutus and the Ghost of Caesar, 1802.]</i></p><p><img alt="The Tragedy of Julius Caesar frontis" title="The Tragedy of Julius Caesar frontis" itemprop="image" src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/play/the-tragedy-of-julius-caesar-frontis.jpg" style="max-width:300px; float:right; margin-right: 12px; margin-bottom: 12px;" data-pagespeed-url-hash="2754543495" onload="pagespeed.CriticalImages.checkImageForCriticality(this);"> <img alt="Karl von Piloty, The Tragedy of Julius Caesar, murder, 1865" title="Carl von Piloty, The Murder of Julius Caesar, 1865" itemprop="image" src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/play/tragedy-of-julius-caesar-murder-1865.jpg" style="max-width:550px; float:right; margin-right: 12px; margin-bottom: 12px;" data-pagespeed-url-hash="3931623788" onload="pagespeed.CriticalImages.checkImageForCriticality(this);"> </p><p><a href="characters-personae">Characters Personae</a></p><p><a href="act-i-scene-i-rome-a-street">Act I - Scene I Rome.
