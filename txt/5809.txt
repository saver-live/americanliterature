<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sweet faces, that from pictured casements lean
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As from a castle window, looking down
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On some gay pageant passing through a town,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yourselves the fairest figures in the scene;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With what a gentle grace, with what serene
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unconsciousness ye wear the triple crown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of youth and beauty and the fair renown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of a great name, that ne'er hath tarnished been!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From your soft eyes, so innocent and sweet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Four spirits, sweet and innocent as they,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gaze on the world below, the sky above;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hark! there is some one singing in the street;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Faith, Hope, and Love! these three," he seems to say;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"These three; and greatest of the three is Love."
</pre>
