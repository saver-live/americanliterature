<pre>The White Czar is Peter the Great.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Batyushka, Father dear, and Gosudar, Sovereign, are titles the Russian people are fond of giving to the Czar in their popular songs.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dost thou see on the rampart's height
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That wreath of mist, in the light
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the midnight moon?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, hist!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is not a wreath of mist;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is the Czar, the White Czar,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Batyushka!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gosudar!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He has heard, among the dead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The artillery roll o'erhead;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The drums and the tramp of feet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of his soldiery in the street;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He is awake! the White Czar,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Batyushka!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gosudar!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He has heard in the grave the cries
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of his people: "Awake! arise!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He has rent the gold brocade
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whereof his shroud was made;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He is risen! the White Czar,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Batyushka!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gosudar!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the Volga and the Don
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He has led his armies on,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over river and morass,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over desert and mountain pass;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Czar, the Orthodox Czar,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Batyushka!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gosudar!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He looks from the mountain-chain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Toward the seas, that cleave in twain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The continents; his hand
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Points southward o'er the land
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of Roumili!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O Czar,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Batyushka!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gosudar!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the words break from his lips:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I am the builder of ships,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And my ships shall sail these seas
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the Pillars of Hercules!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I say it; the White Czar,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Batyushka!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gosudar!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"The Bosphorus shall be free;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It shall make room for me;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the gates of its water-streets
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be unbarred before my fleets.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I say it; the White Czar,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Batyushka!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gosudar!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"And the Christian shall no more
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be crushed, as heretofore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beneath thine iron rule,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O Sultan of Istamboul!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I swear it; I the Czar,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Batyushka!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gosudar!"
</pre>
