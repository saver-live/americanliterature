<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vogelweid the Minnesinger,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When he left this world of ours,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Laid his body in the cloister,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Under Wurtzburg's minster towers.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And he gave the monks his treasures,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gave them all with this behest:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They should feed the birds at noontide
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Daily on his place of rest;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Saying, "From these wandering minstrels
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have learned the art of song;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let me now repay the lessons
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They have taught so well and long."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus the bard of love departed;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, fulfilling his desire,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On his tomb the birds were feasted
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the children of the choir.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Day by day, o'er tower and turret,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In foul weather and in fair,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Day by day, in vaster numbers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flocked the poets of the air.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the tree whose heavy branches
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Overshadowed all the place,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the pavement, on the tombstone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the poet's sculptured face,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the cross-bars of each window,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the lintel of each door,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They renewed the War of Wartburg,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which the bard had fought before.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There they sang their merry carols,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sang their lauds on every side;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the name their voices uttered
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was the name of Vogelweid.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till at length the portly abbot
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Murmured, "Why this waste of food?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be it changed to loaves henceforward
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For our tasting brotherhood."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then in vain o'er tower and turret,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the walls and woodland nests,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When the minster bells rang noontide,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gathered the unwelcome guests.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then in vain, with cries discordant,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Clamorous round the Gothic spire,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Screamed the feathered Minnesingers
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For the children of the choir.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Time has long effaced the inscriptions
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the cloister's funeral stones,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And tradition only tells us
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where repose the poet's bones.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But around the vast cathedral,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By sweet echoes multiplied,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Still the birds repeat the legend,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the name of Vogelweid.
</pre>
