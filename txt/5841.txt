<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I stood on the bridge at midnight,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the clocks were striking the hour,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the moon rose o'er the city,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Behind the dark church-tower.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I saw her bright reflection
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the waters under me,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a golden goblet falling
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And sinking into the sea.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And far in the hazy distance
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of that lovely night in June,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The blaze of the flaming furnace
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gleamed redder than the moon.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Among the long, black rafters
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The wavering shadows lay,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the current that came from the ocean
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seemed to lift and bear them away;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As, sweeping and eddying through them,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rose the belated tide,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, streaming into the moonlight,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The seaweed floated wide.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And like those waters rushing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Among the wooden piers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A flood of thoughts came o'er me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That filled my eyes with tears.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How often, oh, how often,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the days that had gone by,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I had stood on that bridge at midnight
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And gazed on that wave and sky!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How often, oh, how often,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I had wished that the ebbing tide
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Would bear me away on its bosom
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er the ocean wild and wide!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For my heart was hot and restless,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And my life was full of care,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the burden laid upon me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seemed greater than I could bear.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But now it has fallen from me,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is buried in the sea;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And only the sorrow of others
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Throws its shadow over me.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet whenever I cross the river
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On its bridge with wooden piers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the odor of brine from the ocean
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Comes the thought of other years.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I think how many thousands
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of care-encumbered men,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Each bearing his burden of sorrow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Have crossed the bridge since then.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I see the long procession
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Still passing to and fro,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The young heart hot and restless,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the old subdued and slow!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And forever and forever,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As long as the river flows,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As long as the heart has passions,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As long as life has woes;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The moon and its broken reflection
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And its shadows shall appear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the symbol of love in heaven,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And its wavering image here.
</pre>
