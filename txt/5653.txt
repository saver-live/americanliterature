<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am poor and old and blind;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sun burns me, and the wind
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blows through the city gate
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And covers me with dust
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the wheels of the august
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Justinian the Great.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was for him I chased
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Persians o'er wild and waste,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As General of the East;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Night after night I lay
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In their camps of yesterday;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their forage was my feast.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For him, with sails of red,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And torches at mast-head,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Piloting the great fleet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I swept the Afric coasts
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And scattered the Vandal hosts,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like dust in a windy street.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For him I won again
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Ausonian realm and reign,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rome and Parthenope;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And all the land was mine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the summits of Apennine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the shores of either sea.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For him, in my feeble age,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I dared the battle's rage,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To save Byzantium's state,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When the tents of Zabergan,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like snow-drifts overran
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The road to the Golden Gate.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And for this, for this, behold!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Infirm and blind and old,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With gray, uncovered head,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beneath the very arch
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of my triumphal march,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I stand and beg my bread!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Methinks I still can hear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sounding distinct and near,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Vandal monarch's cry,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As, captive and disgraced,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With majestic step he paced,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"All, all is Vanity!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah! vainest of all things
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is the gratitude of kings;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The plaudits of the crowd
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are but the clatter of feet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At midnight in the street,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hollow and restless and loud.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the bitterest disgrace
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is to see forever the face
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the Monk of Ephesus!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The unconquerable will
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This, too, can bear;--I still
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Am Belisarius!
</pre>
