<p>Brunett created an inspirational tale of transformation and empowerment. Mary Lennox, a sickly and contrary little girl, is orphaned to dim prospects in a gloomy English manor - her only friend is a bed-ridden boy named Colin whose prospects may be dimmer than hers. But when Mary finds the key to a Secret Garden, the magical powers of transformation fall within her reach. </p><p> <img alt="The Secret Garden" title="Entry to the Secret Garden" style="max-width:400px;" itemprop="image" src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/book/the-secret-garden-kew.jpg" data-pagespeed-url-hash="1448120437" onload="pagespeed.CriticalImages.checkImageForCriticality(this);"> </p><p>Featured in our collection of <a href="/books-for-young-readers">Books for Young Readers</a> and <a href="/twenty-five-great-american-novels">25 Great American Novels</a>.</p><p><a href="chapter-i-there-is-no-one-left">Chapter I.
