<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As from the darkening gloom a silver dove
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Upsoars, and darts into the eastern light,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On pinions that nought moves but pure delight,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So fled thy soul into the realms above,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Regions of peace and everlasting love;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where happy spirits, crown'd with circlets bright
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of starry beam, and gloriously bedight,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Taste the high joy none but the blest can prove.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There thou or joinest the immortal quire
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In melodies that even heaven fair
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fill with superior bliss, or, at desire,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the omnipotent Father, cleav'st the air
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On holy message sent, What pleasure's higher?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wherefore does any grief our joy impair?
</pre>
