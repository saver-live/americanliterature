<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"What sound awakened me, I wonder,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For now tis dumb."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Wheels on the road most like, or thunder:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lie down; twas not the drum.:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Toil at sea and two in haven
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And trouble far:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fly, crow, away, and follow, raven,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And all that croaks for war."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Hark, I heard the bugle crying,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And where am I?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My friends are up and dressed and dying,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I will dress and die."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh love is rare and trouble plenty
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And carrion cheap,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And daylight dear at four-and-twenty:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lie down again and sleep."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Reach me my belt and leave your prattle:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Your hour is gone;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But my day is the day of battle,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And that comes dawning on.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"They mow the field of man in season:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Farewell, my fair,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, call it truth or call it treason,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Farewell the vows that were."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Ay, false heart, forsake me lightly:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tis like the brave.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They find no bed to joy in rightly
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Before they find the grave.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Their love is for their own undoing.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And east and west
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They scour about the world a-wooing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The bullet in their breast.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Sail away the ocean over,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Oh sail away,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And lie there with your leaden lover
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For ever and a day."
</pre>
