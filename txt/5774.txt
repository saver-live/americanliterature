<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The young Endymion sleeps Endymion's sleep;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The shepherd-boy whose tale was left half told!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The solemn grove uplifts its shield of gold
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the red rising moon, and loud and deep
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The nightingale is singing from the steep;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is midsummer, but the air is cold;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Can it be death?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alas, beside the fold
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A shepherd's pipe lies shattered near his sheep.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lo! in the moonlight gleams a marble white,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On which I read: "Here lieth one whose name
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was writ in water."&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And was this the meed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of his sweet singing?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rather let me write:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"The smoking flax before it burst to flame
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was quenched by death, and broken the bruised reed."
</pre>
