<p>Edward Bellamy's <span class="al-title">Looking Backward from 2000 to 1887</span> is <a href="/author/washington-irving/short-story/rip-van-winkle">Rip Van Winkle</a> in reverse, with a Utopian twist. Bellamy, who had socialist political inclinations, wrote the book because he was disillusioned by his view of an increasingly competitive industrialized society. The book became a sensational best seller in its time, selling over one million copies, and was the second most purchased novel at the end of the nineteenth century (behind <a href="/author/harriet-beecher-stowe/bio-books-stories">Harriet Beecher Stowe's</a> <a href="/author/harriet-beecher-stowe/book/uncle-toms-cabin-or-life-among-the-lowly/summary">Uncle Tom's Cabin</a>).
