<p>I stated it as definitely as I could. I was in a room with them.</p><p>They had tongues like me, and hair and eyes.</p><p>I got up out of my chair and said it as definitely as I could.</p><p>Their eyes wavered. Something slipped out of their grasp. Had I been white and strong and young enough I might have plunged through walls, gone outward into nights and days, gone into prairies, into distances-- gone outward to the doorstep of the house of God, gone to God's throne room with their hands in mine.</p><p>What I am trying to say is this--</p><p>By God I made their minds flee out of them.</p><p>Their minds came out of them as clear and straight as anything could be.</p><p>I said they might build temples to their lives.</p><p>I threw my words at faces floating in a street.</p><p>I threw my words like stones, like building stones.</p><p>I scattered words in alleyways like seeds.</p><p>I crept at night and threw my words in empty rooms of houses in a street.</p><p>I said that life was life, that men in streets and cities might build temples to their souls.</p><p>I whispered words at night into a telephone.</p><p>I told my people life was sweet, that men might live.</p><p>I said a million temples might be built, that doorsteps might be cleansed.</p><p>At their fleeing harried minds I hurled a stone.</p><p>I said they might build temples to themselves.</p><p> </p><p><br> </p><p> </p>
