<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beautiful lily, dwelling by still rivers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or solitary mere,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or where the sluggish meadow-brook delivers
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Its waters to the weir!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou laughest at the mill, the whir and worry
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of spindle and of loom,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the great wheel that toils amid the hurry
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And rushing of the flame.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Born in the purple, born to joy and pleasance,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou dost not toil nor spin,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But makest glad and radiant with thy presence
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The meadow and the lin.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The wind blows, and uplifts thy drooping banner,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And round thee throng and run
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The rushes, the green yeomen of thy manor,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The outlaws of the sun.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The burnished dragon-fly is thine attendant,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And tilts against the field,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And down the listed sunbeam rides resplendent
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With steel-blue mail and shield.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou art the Iris, fair among the fairest,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who, armed with golden rod
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And winged with the celestial azure, bearest
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The message of some God.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou art the Muse, who far from crowded cities
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hauntest the sylvan streams,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Playing on pipes of reed the artless ditties
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That come to us as dreams.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O flower-de-luce, bloom on, and let the river
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Linger to kiss thy feet!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O flower of song, bloom on, and make forever
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The world more fair and sweet.
</pre>
