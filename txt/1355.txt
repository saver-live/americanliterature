<p><cite class="al-title">Childe Harold's Pilgrimage</cite> (1812) launched the young poet into instant stardom, leading to his reputation for centuries as the world best poet. In Byron’s words, <i>“I awoke one morning and found myself famous.”</i> It consists of four cantos. John Murray was Byron's publisher, raising their reputation to publish renowned works by <a href="/author/jane-austen">Jane Austen</a> and <a href="/author/washington-irving">Washington Irving</a>. That same firm is responsible for burning the personal memoirs of Byron the month after his death in 1824, against the directives of Byron's literary executor, Irish author <cite class="al-title">Thomas Moore. The John Murray firm burned the contents of these diaries, whose contents the world will never know.</cite></p><p><a href="to-ianthe">To Ianthe</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-first">Canto the First</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-second">Canto the Second</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-third">Canto the Third</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-fourth">Canto the Fourth</a></p><p style="margin-top: 26px;"> Return to the <a href="/author/lord-byron">Lord Byron</a> library. </p>
