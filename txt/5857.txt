<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here in a little rustic hermitage
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alfred the Saxon King, Alfred the Great,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Postponed the cares of king-craft to translate
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Consolations of the Roman sage.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here Geoffrey Chaucer in his ripe old age
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wrote the unrivalled Tales, which soon or late
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The venturous hand that strives to imitate
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vanquished must fall on the unfinished page.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Two kings were they, who ruled by right divine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And both supreme; one in the realm of Truth,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One in the realm of Fiction and of Song.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What prince hereditary of their line,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Uprising in the strength and flush of youth,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their glory shall inherit and prolong?
</pre>
