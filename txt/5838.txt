<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O ye dead Poets, who are living still
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Immortal in your verse, though life be fled,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And ye, O living Poets, who are dead
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Though ye are living, if neglect can kill,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tell me if in the darkest hours of ill,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With drops of anguish falling fast and red
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the sharp crown of thorns upon your head,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ye were not glad your errand to fulfil?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes; for the gift and ministry of Song
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Have something in them so divinely sweet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It can assuage the bitterness of wrong;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not in the clamor of the crowded street,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not in the shouts and plaudits of the throng,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But in ourselves, are triumph and defeat.
</pre>
