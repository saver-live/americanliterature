<pre>[Greek poem here--Euripides.]

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pleasant it was, when woods were green,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And winds were soft and low,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To lie amid some sylvan scene.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where, the long drooping boughs between,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shadows dark and sunlight sheen
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alternate come and go;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or where the denser grove receives
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No sunlight from above,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the dark foliage interweaves
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In one unbroken roof of leaves,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Underneath whose sloping eaves
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The shadows hardly move.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beneath some patriarchal tree
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I lay upon the ground;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His hoary arms uplifted he,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And all the broad leaves over me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Clapped their little hands in glee,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With one continuous sound;--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A slumberous sound, a sound that brings
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The feelings of a dream,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As of innumerable wings,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As, when a bell no longer swings,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Faint the hollow murmur rings
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er meadow, lake, and stream.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And dreams of that which cannot die,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bright visions, came to me,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As lapped in thought I used to lie,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And gaze into the summer sky,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where the sailing clouds went by,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like ships upon the sea;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dreams that the soul of youth engage
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ere Fancy has been quelled;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Old legends of the monkish page,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Traditions of the saint and sage,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tales that have the rime of age,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And chronicles of Eld.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, loving still these quaint old themes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Even in the city's throng
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I feel the freshness of the streams,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That, crossed by shades and sunny gleams,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Water the green land of dreams,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The holy land of song.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Therefore, at Pentecost, which brings
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Spring, clothed like a bride,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When nestling buds unfold their wings,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And bishop's-caps have golden rings,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Musing upon many things,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I sought the woodlands wide.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The green trees whispered low and mild;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was a sound of joy!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They were my playmates when a child,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And rocked me in their arms so wild!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Still they looked at me and smiled,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As if I were a boy;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And ever whispered, mild and low,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Come, be a child once more!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And waved their long arms to and fro,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And beckoned solemnly and slow;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, I could not choose but go
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into the woodlands hoar,--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into the blithe and breathing air,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into the solemn wood,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Solemn and silent everywhere
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nature with folded hands seemed there
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kneeling at her evening prayer!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like one in prayer I stood.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Before me rose an avenue
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of tall and sombrous pines;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Abroad their fan-like branches grew,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, where the sunshine darted through,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Spread a vapor soft and blue,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In long and sloping lines.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, falling on my weary brain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a fast-falling shower,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The dreams of youth came back again,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Low lispings of the summer rain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dropping on the ripened grain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As once upon the flower.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Visions of childhood!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stay, O stay!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ye were so sweet and wild!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And distant voices seemed to say,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"It cannot be!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They pass away!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Other themes demand thy lay;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou art no more a child!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"The land of Song within thee lies,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Watered by living springs;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The lids of Fancy's sleepless eyes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are gates unto that Paradise,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Holy thoughts, like stars, arise,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Its clouds are angels' wings.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Learn, that henceforth thy song shall be,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not mountains capped with snow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor forests sounding like the sea,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor rivers flowing ceaselessly,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where the woodlands bend to see
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The bending heavens below.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"There is a forest where the din
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of iron branches sounds!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A mighty river roars between,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And whosoever looks therein
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sees the heavens all black with sin,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sees not its depths, nor bounds.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Athwart the swinging branches cast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Soft rays of sunshine pour;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then comes the fearful wintry blast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Our hopes, like withered leaves, fail fast;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pallid lips say, 'It is past!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We can return no more!,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Look, then, into thine heart, and write!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes, into Life's deep stream!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All forms of sorrow and delight,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All solemn Voices of the Night,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That can soothe thee, or affright,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be these henceforth thy theme."
</pre>
