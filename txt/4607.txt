<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now praise the Gods of Time and Chance
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That bring a hearts desire,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And lay the joyous roads of France
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Once more beneath the tyre,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So numbered by Napoleon,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The veriest ass can spy
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How Twenty takes to Bourg-Madame
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Ten is for Hendaye.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sixteen hath fed our fighting-line
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From Dunkirk to Péronne,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Thirty-nine and Twenty-nine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Can show where it has gone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which slant through Arras and Bapaume,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And join outside Cambrai,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While Twenty takes to Bourg-Madame,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Ten is for Hendaye.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The crops and houses spring once more
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where Thirty-seven ran,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And even ghostly Forty-four
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is all restored to man.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Oh, swift as shell-hole poppies pass
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The blurring years go by,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Twenty takes to Bourg-Madame,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Ten is for Hendaye!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And you desire that sheeted snow
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where chill Mont Louis stands?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And we the rounder gales that blow
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Full-lunged across the Landes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So you will use the Orleans Gate,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While we slip through Versailles;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Since Twenty takes to Bourg-Madame,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Ten is for Hendaye.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sou-West by South, and South by West,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On every vine appear
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Those four first cautious leaves that test
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The temper of the year;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The dust is white at Angoulême,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sun is warm at Blaye;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Twenty takes to Bourg-Madame,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Ten is for Hendaye.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Broad and unbridled, mile on mile,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The highway drops her line
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Past Langon down that grey-walled aisle
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of resin-scented pine;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And ninety to the lawless hour
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The kilometres fly,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What was your pace to Bourg-Madame?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We sauntered to Hendaye.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now Fontarabia marks our goal,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Bidassoa shows,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At issue with each whispering shoal
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In violet, pearl and rose,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ere crimson over oceans edge
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sunset banners die . . .
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes, Twenty takes to Bourg-Madame,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But Ten is for Hendaye!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Oh, praise the Gods of Time and Chance
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That ease the long control,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And bring the glorious soul of France
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Once more to cheer our soul
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With beauty, change and valiancy
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of sun and soil and sky,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where Twenty takes to Bourg-Madame,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Ten is for Hendaye!
</pre>
