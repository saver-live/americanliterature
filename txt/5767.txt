<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Out of the bosom of the Air,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Out of the cloud-folds of her garments shaken,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over the woodlands brown and bare,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over the harvest-fields forsaken,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Silent, and soft, and slow
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Descends the snow.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Even as our cloudy fancies take
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suddenly shape in some divine expression,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Even as the troubled heart doth make
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the white countenance confession,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The troubled sky reveals
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The grief it feels.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This is the poem of the air,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Slowly in silent syllables recorded;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This is the secret of despair,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Long in its cloudy bosom hoarded,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now whispered and revealed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To wood and field.
</pre>
