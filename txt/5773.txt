<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The summer sun is sinking low;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Only the tree-tops redden and glow:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Only the weathercock on the spire
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the neighboring church is a flame of fire;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; All is in shadow below.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O beautiful, awful summer day,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What hast thou given, what taken away?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Life and death, and love and hate,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Homes made happy or desolate,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Hearts made sad or gay!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the road of life one mile-stone more!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the book of life one leaf turned o'er!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a red seal is the setting sun
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the good and the evil men have done,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Naught can to-day restore!
</pre>
