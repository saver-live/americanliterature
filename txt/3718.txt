<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When the eye of day is shut,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the stars deny their beams,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And about the forest hut
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blows the roaring wood of dreams,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From deep clay, from desert rock,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the sunk sands of the main,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come not at my door to knock,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hearts that loved me not again.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sleep, be still, turn to your rest
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the lands where you are laid;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In far lodgings east and west
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lie down on the beds you made.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In gross marl, in blowing dust,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the drowned ooze of the sea,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where you would not, lie you must,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lie you must, and not with me.
</pre>
