<p>I'll tell thee now (dear love) what thou shalt do<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>To anger destiny, as she doth us;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>How I shall stay, though she eloign me thus,<br> And how posterity shall know it too;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>How thine may out-endure<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Sibyl's glory, and obscure<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Her who from Pindar could allure,<br> And her, through whose help Lucan is not lame,<br> And her, whose book (they say) Homer did find, and name.<br> <br> Study our manuscripts, those myriads<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Of letters, which have past 'twixt thee and me;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Thence write our annals, and in them will be<br> To all whom love's subliming fire invades,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Rule and example found;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>There the faith of any ground<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>No schismatic will dare to wound,<br> That sees, how Love this grace to us affords,<br> To make, to keep, to use, to be these his records.<br> <br> This book, as long-lived as the elements,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Or as the world's form, this all-gravèd tome<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>In cypher writ, or new made idiom;<br> We for Love's clergy only are instruments;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>When this book is made thus,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Should again the ravenous<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Vandals and Goths invade us,<br> Learning were safe; in this our universe,<br> Schools might learn sciences, spheres music, angels verse.<br> <br> Here Love's divines—since all divinity<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Is love or wonder—may find all they seek,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Whether abstract spiritual love they like,<br> Their souls exhaled with what they do not see;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Or, loth so to amuse<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Faith's infirmity, they choose<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Something which they may see and use;<br> For, though mind be the heaven, where love doth sit,<br> Beauty a convenient type may be to figure it.<br> <br> Here more than in their books may lawyers find,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Both by what titles mistresses are ours,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>And how prerogative these states devours,<br> Transferr'd from Love himself, to womankind;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Who, though from heart and eyes,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>They exact great subsidies,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Forsake him who on them relies;<br> And for the cause, honour, or conscience give;<br> Chimeras vain as they or their prerogative.<br> <br> Here statesmen—or of them, they which can read—<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>May of their occupation find the grounds;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Love, and their art, alike it deadly wounds,<br> If to consider what 'tis, one proceed.<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>In both they do excel<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Who the present govern well,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Whose weakness none doth, or dares tell;<br> In this thy book, such will there something see,<br> As in the Bible some can find out alchemy.<br> <br> Thus vent thy thoughts; abroad I'll study thee,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>As he removes far off, that great heights takes;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>How great love is, presence best trial makes,<br> But absence tries how long this love will be;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>To take a latitude<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Sun, or stars, are fitliest view'd<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>At their brightest, but to conclude<br> Of longitudes, what other way have we,<br> But to mark when and where the dark eclipses be?<br></p>
