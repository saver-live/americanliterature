<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When the summer fields are mown,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When the birds are fledged and flown,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the dry leaves strew the path;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the falling of the snow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the cawing of the crow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Once again the fields we mow
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And gather in the aftermath.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not the sweet, new grass with flowers
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is this harvesting of ours;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not the upland clover bloom;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the rowen mired with weeds,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tangled tufts from marsh and meads,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where the poppy drops its seeds
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the silence and the gloom.
</pre>
