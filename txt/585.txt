<p><cite class="al-title">Dom Casmurro</cite> (1899) is considered Assis's masterpiece of literary Realism, in which he expertly employs the technique of <a href="/the-unreliable-narrator">The Unreliable Narrator</a>. It is a fictional memoir of a jealous, vengeful husband with many references to <a href="/author/william-shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>'s plays, rich in themes of betrayal and deception, such as <a href="/author/william-shakespeare/play/the-merry-wives-of-windsor/summary">The Merry Wives of Windsor</a> Assis's novel was published in Portuguese in 1899; we used Google translate to offer it, so please excuse some awkwardness in the text, as the English translation (1953) is not yet in the public domain.</p><p><a href="i-about-the-title">I - About the Title</a></p><p><a href="ii-about-the-book">II - About the Book</a></p><p><a href="iii-the-complaint">III - The Complaint</a></p><p><a href="iv-a-most-loving-duty">IV - A Most Loving Duty!</a></p><p><a href="v-the-aggregate">V - The Aggregate</a></p><p><a href="vi-uncle-cosme">VI - Uncle Cosme</a></p><p><a href="vii-d-gloria">VII - D.
