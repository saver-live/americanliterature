<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Christ to the young man said: "Yet one thing more;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If thou wouldst perfect be,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sell all thou hast and give it to the poor,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And come and follow me!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Within this temple Christ again, unseen,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Those sacred words hath said,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And his invisible hands to-day have been
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Laid on a young man's head.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And evermore beside him on his way
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The unseen Christ shall move,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That he may lean upon his arm and say,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Dost thou, dear Lord, approve?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beside him at the marriage feast shall be,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To make the scene more fair;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beside him in the dark Gethsemane
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of pain and midnight prayer.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O holy trust!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O endless sense of rest!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the beloved John
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To lay his head upon the Saviour's breast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And thus to journey on!
</pre>
