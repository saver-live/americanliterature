<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Slowly the hour-hand of the clock moves round;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So slowly that no human eye hath power
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To see it move!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Slowly in shine or shower
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The painted ship above it, homeward bound,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sails, but seems motionless, as if aground;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet both arrive at last; and in his tower
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The slumberous watchman wakes and strikes the hour,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; A mellow, measured, melancholy sound.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Midnight! the outpost of advancing day!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The frontier town and citadel of night!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The watershed of Time, from which the streams
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of Yesterday and To-morrow take their way,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One to the land of promise and of light,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One to the land of darkness and of dreams!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;II

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O River of Yesterday, with current swift
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through chasms descending, and soon lost to sight,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I do not care to follow in their flight
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The faded leaves, that on thy bosom drift!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O River of To-morrow, I uplift
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mine eyes, and thee I follow, as the night
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wanes into morning, and the dawning light
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Broadens, and all the shadows fade and shift!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I follow, follow, where thy waters run
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through unfrequented, unfamiliar fields,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fragrant with flowers and musical with song;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Still follow, follow; sure to meet the sun,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And confident, that what the future yields
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Will be the right, unless myself be wrong.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;III

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet not in vain, O River of Yesterday,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through chasms of darkness to the deep descending,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I heard thee sobbing in the rain, and blending
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy voice with other voices far away.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I called to thee, and yet thou wouldst not stay,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But turbulent, and with thyself contending,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And torrent-like thy force on pebbles spending,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou wouldst not listen to a poet's lay.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thoughts, like a loud and sudden rush of wings,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Regrets and recollections of things past,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With hints and prophecies of things to be,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And inspirations, which, could they be things,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And stay with us, and we could hold them fast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Were our good angels,--these I owe to thee.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;IV

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And thou, O River of To-morrow, flowing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Between thy narrow adamantine walls,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But beautiful, and white with waterfalls,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And wreaths of mist, like hands the pathway showing;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hear the trumpets of the morning blowing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hear thy mighty voice, that calls and calls,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And see, as Ossian saw in Morven's halls,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mysterious phantoms, coming, beckoning, going!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is the mystery of the unknown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That fascinates us; we are children still,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wayward and wistful; with one hand we cling
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the familiar things we call our own,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And with the other, resolute of will,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Grope in the dark for what the day will bring.
</pre>
