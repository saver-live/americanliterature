<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In that building, long and low,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With its windows all a-row,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the port-holes of a hulk,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Human spiders spin and spin,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Backward down their threads so thin
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dropping, each a hempen bulk.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At the end, an open door;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Squares of sunshine on the floor
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Light the long and dusky lane;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the whirring of a wheel,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dull and drowsy, makes me feel
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All its spokes are in my brain.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the spinners to the end
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Downward go and reascend,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gleam the long threads in the sun;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While within this brain of mine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cobwebs brighter and more fine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the busy wheel are spun.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Two fair maidens in a swing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like white doves upon the wing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;First before my vision pass;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Laughing, as their gentle hands
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Closely clasp the twisted strands,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At their shadow on the grass.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then a booth of mountebanks,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With its smell of tan and planks,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And a girl poised high in air
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On a cord, in spangled dress,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a faded loveliness,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And a weary look of care.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then a homestead among farms,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And a woman with bare arms
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drawing water from a well;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the bucket mounts apace,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With it mounts her own fair face,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As at some magician's spell.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then an old man in a tower,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ringing loud the noontide hour,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While the rope coils round and round
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a serpent at his feet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And again, in swift retreat,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nearly lifts him from the ground.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then within a prison-yard,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Faces fixed, and stern, and hard,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Laughter and indecent mirth;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah! it is the gallows-tree!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Breath of Christian charity,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blow, and sweep it from the earth!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then a school-boy, with his kite
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gleaming in a sky of light,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And an eager, upward look;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Steeds pursued through lane and field;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fowlers with their snares concealed;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And an angler by a brook.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ships rejoicing in the breeze,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wrecks that float o'er unknown seas,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Anchors dragged through faithless sand;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sea-fog drifting overhead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, with lessening line and lead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sailors feeling for the land.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All these scenes do I behold,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These, and many left untold,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In that building long and low;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While the wheel goes round and round,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a drowsy, dreamy sound,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the spinners backward go.
</pre>
