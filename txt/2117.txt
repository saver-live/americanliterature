<p>I NEVER stoop'd so low, as they<br> Which on an eye, cheek, lip, can prey;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Seldom to them which soar no higher<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Than virtue, or the mind to admire.<br> For sense and understanding may<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Know what gives fuel to their fire;<br> My love, though silly, is more brave;<br> For may I miss, whene'er I crave,<br> If I know yet what I would have.<br> <br> If that be simply perfectest,<br> Which can by no way be express'd<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>But negatives, my love is so.<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>To all, which all love, I say no.<br> If any who deciphers best,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>What we know not—ourselves—can know,<br> Let him teach me that nothing. This<br> As yet my ease and comfort is,<br> Though I speed not, I cannot miss.</p>
