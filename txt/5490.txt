<p><cite class="al-title">The Iron Heel</cite> (1908) is considered one of the earliest novels of modern <a href="/dystopian-stories">Dystopian Fiction</a>, which would lead to works later known as <a href="/science-fiction-study-guide">Soft Science Fiction</a>. Jack London, a socialist himself, portrays America under tyrannical rule. It is based on <cite class="al-author">Avis Everhard</cite>'s fictional "Everhard Manuscript" and was written by London as a woman's first person narrative, not a common writing style at the time. <i>[Note that <i>"philomath"</i> (chapter V) is a Greek word meaning lover of learning.]</i> The book's epigraph: </p><p>“At first, this Earth, a stage so gloomed with woe <br>You almost sicken at the shifting of the scenes. <br>And yet be patient.
