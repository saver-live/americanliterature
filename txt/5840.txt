<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What say the Bells of San Blas
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the ships that southward pass
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the harbor of Mazatlan?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To them it is nothing more
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Than the sound of surf on the shore,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nothing more to master or man.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But to me, a dreamer of dreams,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To whom what is and what seems
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are often one and the same,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Bells of San Blas to me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Have a strange, wild melody,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And are something more than a name.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For bells are the voice of the church;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They have tones that touch and search
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The hearts of young and old;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One sound to all, yet each
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lends a meaning to their speech,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the meaning is manifold.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They are a voice of the Past,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of an age that is fading fast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of a power austere and grand,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When the flag of Spain unfurled
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Its folds o'er this western world,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the Priest was lord of the land.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The chapel that once looked down
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the little seaport town
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Has crumbled into the dust;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And on oaken beams below
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The bells swing to and fro,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And are green with mould and rust.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Is, then, the old faith dead,"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They say, "and in its stead
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is some new faith proclaimed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That we are forced to remain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Naked to sun and rain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unsheltered and ashamed?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Once, in our tower aloof,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We rang over wall and roof
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Our warnings and our complaints;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And round about us there
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The white doves filled the air,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the white souls of the saints.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"The saints!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah, have they grown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Forgetful of their own?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are they asleep, or dead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That open to the sky
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their ruined Missions lie,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No longer tenanted?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh, bring us back once more
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The vanished days of yore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When the world with faith was filled;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bring back the fervid zeal,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The hearts of fire and steel,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The hands that believe and build.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Then from our tower again
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We will send over land and main
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Our voices of command,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like exiled kings who return
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To their thrones, and the people learn
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That the Priest is lord of the land!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O Bells of San Blas in vain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ye call back the Past again;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Past is deaf to your prayer!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Out of the shadows of night
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The world rolls into light;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is daybreak everywhere.
</pre>
