<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At anchor in Hampton Roads we lay,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On board of the cumberland, sloop-of-war;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And at times from the fortress across the bay
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The alarum of drums swept past,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or a bugle blast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the camp on the shore.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then far away to the south uprose
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A little feather of snow-white smoke,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And we knew that the iron ship of our foes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was steadily steering its course
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To try the force
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of our ribs of oak.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Down upon us heavily runs,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Silent and sullen, the floating fort;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then comes a puff of smoke from her guns,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And leaps the terrible death,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With fiery breath,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From each open port.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We are not idle, but send her straight
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Defiance back in a full broadside!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As hail rebounds from a roof of slate,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rebounds our heavier hail
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From each iron scale
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the monster's hide.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Strike your flag!" the rebel cries,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In his arrogant old plantation strain.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Never!" our gallant Morris replies;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"It is better to sink than to yield!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the whole air pealed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the cheers of our men.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then, like a kraken huge and black,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She crushed our ribs in her iron grasp!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Down went the Cumberland all a wrack,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a sudden shudder of death,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the cannon's breath
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For her dying gasp.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Next morn, as the sun rose over the bay,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Still floated our flag at the mainmast head.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lord, how beautiful was Thy day!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Every waft of the air
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was a whisper of prayer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or a dirge for the dead.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ho! brave hearts that went down in the seas
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ye are at peace in the troubled stream;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ho! brave land! with hearts like these,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy flag, that is rent in twain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shall be one again,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And without a seam!
</pre>
