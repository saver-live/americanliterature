<p><cite class="al-title">Exiles: A Play in Three Acts</cite> was first published in 1918. It takes place in the summer of 1912 in Merrion and Ranelagh, both suburbs of Dublin, to which a famous writer returns after nine years of exile. Joyce described the structure of the play as <i>"three cat and mouse acts."</i> The inspiration for the play drew from his own feelings of exile when he and and his lover, <cite class="al-person">Nora Barnacle</cite> lived, unmarried, in Trieste, Italy for almost a decade, a city described as Italy's <i>"most beautifully haunting city.'</i></p><p><a href="list-of-characters">List of Characters</a></p><p><a href="act-one">Act One</a></p><p><a href="act-two">Act Two</a></p><p><a href="act-three">Act Three</a></p><p style="margin-top: 26px;"> Return to the <a href="/author/james-joyce">James Joyce</a> library. </p>
