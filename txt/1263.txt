<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which is the weakest thing of all
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mine heart can ponder?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sun, a little cloud can pall
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With darkness yonder?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The cloud, a little wind can move
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where'er it listeth?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The wind, a little leaf above,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Though sere, resisteth?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What time that yellow leaf was green,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My days were gladder;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But now, whatever Spring may mean,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I must grow sadder.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah me! a leaf with sighs can wring
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My lips asunder
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then is mine heart the weakest thing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Itself can ponder.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet, Heart, when sun and cloud are pined
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And drop together,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And at a blast, which is not wind,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The forests wither,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou, from the darkening deathly curse
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To glory breakest,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Strongest of the universe
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Guarding the weakest!
</pre>
