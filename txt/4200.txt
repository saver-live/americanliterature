&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chief of organic Numbers!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Old Scholar of the Spheres!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy spirit never slumbers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But rolls about our ears
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For ever and for ever.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, what a mad endeavour
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Worketh he
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who, to thy sacred and ennobled hearse,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Would offer a burnt sacrifice of verse
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Melody!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How heavenward thou soundedst
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Live Temple of sweet noise;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And discord unconfoundedst:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Giving delight new joys,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Pleasure nobler pinions
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O where are thy Dominions!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lend thine ear
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To a young delian oath aye, by thy soul,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By all that from thy mortal Lips did roll;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And by the Kernel of thine earthly Love,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beauty, in things on earth and things above,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When every childish fashion
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Has vanish'd from my rhyme
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Will I grey-gone in passion
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Give to an after-time
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hymning and harmony
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of thee, and of thy Words and of thy Life:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But vain is now the bruning and the strife
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pangs are in vain until I grow high-rife
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With Old Philosophy
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And mad with glimpses at futurity!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For many years my offerings must be hush'd:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When I do speak I'll think upon this hour,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Because I feel my forehead hot and flush'd,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Even at the simplest vassal of thy Power,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A Lock of thy bright hair!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sudden it came,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I was startled when I heard thy name
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Coupled so unaware
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet, at the moment, temperate was my blood:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Methought I had beheld it from the flood.
</pre>
