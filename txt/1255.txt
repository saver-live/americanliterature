<pre>IX

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My lips do need thy breath,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My lips do need thy smile,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And my pallid eyne, that light in thine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which met the stars erewhile:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet go with light and life
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If that thou lovest one
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In all the earth who loveth thee
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As truly as the sun.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Margret, Margret.


XIV

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But better loveth he
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy chaliced wine than thy chanted song,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And better both than thee,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Margret, Margret.



XVII

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But better loveth she
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy golden comb than thy gathered flowers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And better both than thee,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Margret, Margret.


XXII

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We brake no gold, a sign
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of stronger faith to be,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But I wear his last look in my soul,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which said, I love but thee!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Margret, Margret.


XXVI

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A knights bloodhound and he
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The funeral watch did keep;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a thought o the chase he stroked its face
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As it howled to see him weep.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A fair child kissed the dead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But shrank before its cold.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And alone yet proudly in his hall
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Did stand a baron of old.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Margret, Margret.
