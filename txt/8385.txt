<p>Henry David Thoreau's account of <cite class="al-title">Sir Walter Raleigh</cite> was published in 1905, having been "lately discovered among his unpublished journals and manuscripts." Thoreau features many of Raleigh's romantic poems, with helpful notes. Originally published exclusively for members of the Bibliophile Society which holds the copyright, it was edited by Henry Aiken Metcalf.</p><p><a href="preface">Preface</a></p><p><a href="introduction-by-franklin-benjamin-sanborn">Introduction by Franklin Benjamin Sanborn</a></p><p><a href="sir-walter-raleigh-part-i">Sir Walter Raleigh - Part I</a></p><p><a href="sir-walter-raleigh-part-ii">Sir Walter Raleigh - Part II</a></p><p><a href="footnotes">Footnotes</a></p><p style="margin-top: 26px;"> Return to the <a href="/author/henry-david-thoreau">Henry David Thoreau</a> library. </p>
