<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Othere, the old sea-captain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who dwelt in Helgoland,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To King Alfred, the Lover of Truth,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Brought a snow-white walrus-tooth,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which he held in his brown right hand.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His figure was tall and stately,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a boy's his eye appeared;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His hair was yellow as hay,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But threads of a silvery gray
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gleamed in his tawny beard.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hearty and hale was Othere,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His cheek had the color of oak;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a kind of laugh in his speech,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the sea-tide on a beach,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As unto the King he spoke.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Alfred, King of the Saxons,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Had a book upon his knees,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And wrote down the wondrous tale
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of him who was first to sail
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Into the Arctic seas.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"So far I live to the northward,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No man lives north of me;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the east are wild mountain-chains;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And beyond them meres and plains;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the westward all is sea.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"So far I live to the northward,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the harbor of Skeringes-hale,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If you only sailed by day,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a fair wind all the way,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;More than a month would you sail.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I own six hundred reindeer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With sheep and swine beside;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have tribute from the Finns,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whalebone and reindeer-skins,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And ropes of walrus-hide.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I ploughed the land with horses,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But my heart was ill at ease,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For the old seafaring men
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Came to me now and then,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With their sagas of the seas;--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Of Iceland and of Greenland,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the stormy Hebrides,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the undiscovered deep;--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I could not eat nor sleep
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For thinking of those seas.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"To the northward stretched the desert,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How far I fain would know;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So at last I sallied forth,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And three days sailed due north,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As far as the whale-ships go.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"To the west of me was the ocean,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the right the desolate shore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But I did not slacken sail
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For the walrus or the whale,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till after three days more.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"The days grew longer and longer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till they became as one,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And southward through the haze
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I saw the sullen blaze
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the red midnight sun.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"And then uprose before me,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Upon the water's edge,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The huge and haggard shape
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of that unknown North Cape,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose form is like a wedge.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"The sea was rough and stormy,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The tempest howled and wailed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the sea-fog, like a ghost,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Haunted that dreary coast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But onward still I sailed.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Four days I steered to eastward,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Four days without a night:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Round in a fiery ring
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Went the great sun, O King,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With red and lurid light."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here Alfred, King of the Saxons,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ceased writing for a while;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And raised his eyes from his book,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a strange and puzzled look,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And an incredulous smile.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But Othere, the old sea-captain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He neither paused nor stirred,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till the King listened, and then
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Once more took up his pen,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And wrote down every word.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"And now the land," said Othere,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Bent southward suddenly,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I followed the curving shore
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And ever southward bore
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into a nameless sea.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"And there we hunted the walrus,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The narwhale, and the seal;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ha! 't was a noble game!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And like the lightning's flame
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flew our harpoons of steel.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"There were six of us all together,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Norsemen of Helgoland;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In two days and no more
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We killed of them threescore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And dragged them to the strand!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here Alfred the Truth-Teller
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suddenly closed his book,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And lifted his blue eyes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With doubt and strange surmise
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Depicted in their look.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Othere the old sea-captain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stared at him wild and weird,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then smiled, till his shining teeth
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gleamed white from underneath
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His tawny, quivering beard.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And to the King of the Saxons,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In witness of the truth,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Raising his noble head,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He stretched his brown hand, and said,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Behold this walrus-tooth!"
</pre>
