<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the Valley of the Vire
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Still is seen an ancient mill,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With its gables quaint and queer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And beneath the window-sill,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the stone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These words alone:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oliver Basselin lived here."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Far above it, on the steep,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ruined stands the old Chateau;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nothing but the donjon-keep
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Left for shelter or for show.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Its vacant eyes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stare at the skies,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stare at the valley green and deep.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Once a convent, old and brown,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Looked, but ah! it looks no more,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the neighboring hillside down
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the rushing and the roar
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the stream
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose sunny gleam
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cheers the little Norman town.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In that darksome mill of stone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the water's dash and din,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Careless, humble, and unknown,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sang the poet Basselin
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Songs that fill
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That ancient mill
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a splendor of its own.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never feeling of unrest
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Broke the pleasant dream he dreamed;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Only made to be his nest,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All the lovely valley seemed;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No desire
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of soaring higher
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stirred or fluttered in his breast.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;True, his songs were not divine;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Were not songs of that high art,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which, as winds do in the pine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Find an answer in each heart;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the mirth
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of this green earth
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Laughed and revelled in his line.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the alehouse and the inn,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Opening on the narrow street,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Came the loud, convivial din,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Singing and applause of feet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The laughing lays
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That in those days
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sang the poet Basselin.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the castle, cased in steel,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Knights, who fought at Agincourt,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Watched and waited, spur on heel;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the poet sang for sport
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Songs that rang
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Another clang,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Songs that lowlier hearts could feel.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the convent, clad in gray,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sat the monks in lonely cells,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Paced the cloisters, knelt to pray,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the poet heard their bells;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But his rhymes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Found other chimes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nearer to the earth than they.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gone are all the barons bold,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gone are all the knights and squires,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gone the abbot stern and cold,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the brotherhood of friars;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not a name
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Remains to fame,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From those mouldering days of old!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the poet's memory here
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the landscape makes a part;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the river, swift and clear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flows his song through many a heart;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Haunting still
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That ancient mill,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the Valley of the Vire.
</pre>
