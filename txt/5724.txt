<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the birds come in the Spring,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We know not from where;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the stars come at evening
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From depths of the air;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the rain comes from the cloud,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the brook from the ground;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As suddenly, low or loud,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Out of silence a sound;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the grape comes to the vine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The fruit to the tree;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the wind comes to the pine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the tide to the sea;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As come the white sails of ships
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er the ocean's verge;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As comes the smile to the lips,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The foam to the surge;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So come to the Poet his songs,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All hitherward blown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the misty realm, that belongs
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the vast unknown.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His, and not his, are the lays
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He sings; and their fame
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is his, and not his; and the praise
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the pride of a name.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For voices pursue him by day,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And haunt him by night,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And he listens, and needs must obey,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When the Angel says: "Write!"
</pre>
