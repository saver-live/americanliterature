<p>Originally serialized in 1851, <span class="al-title">Cranford</span>, appeared in <a href="/author/charles-dickens/bio-books-stories">Charles Dickens'</a> literary magazine <span class="al-periodical">Household Words</span>. It was published in book from in 1853 and is probably <span class="al-person">Glaskell's</span> most popular novel. The title refers to the town of Cranford where <span class="al-char">Mary Smith</span> lives in friendship with her friends <span class="al-char">Miss Matty</span> and <span class="al-char">Miss Deborah</span> who are sisters.
