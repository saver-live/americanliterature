<p>No lover saith, I love, nor any other<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Can judge a perfect lover;<br> He thinks that else none can or will agree,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>That any loves but he;<br> I cannot say I loved, for who can say<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>He was kill'd yesterday.<br> Love with excess of heat, more young than old,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Death kills with too much cold;<br> We die but once, and who loved last did die,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>He that saith, twice, doth lie;<br> For though he seem to move, and stir a while,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>It doth the sense beguile.<br> Such life is like the light which bideth yet<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>When the life's light is set,<br> Or like the heat which fire in solid matter<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Leaves behind, two hours after.<br> Once I loved and died; and am now become<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Mine epitaph and tomb;<br> Here dead men speak their last, and so do I;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Love-slain, lo! here I die.</p>
