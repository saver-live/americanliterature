<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<i>"Under the flag
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of each his faction, they to battle bring
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their embryo atoms."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;- Milton.</i>


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Welcome joy, and welcome sorrow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lethe's weed and Hermes' feather;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come to-day, and come to-morrow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I do love you both together!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I love to mark sad faces in fair weather;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And hear a merry laugh amid the thunder;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fair and foul I love together.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Meadows sweet where flames are under,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And a giggle at a wonder;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Visage sage at pantomine;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Funeral, and steeple-chime;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Infant playing with a skull;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Morning fair, and shipwreck'd hull;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nightshade with the woodbine kissing;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Serpents in red roses hissing;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cleopatra regal-dress'd
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the aspic at her breast;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dancing music, music sad,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Both together, sane and mad;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Muses bright and muses pale;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sombre Saturn, Momus hale;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Laugh and sigh, and laugh again;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Oh the sweetness of the pain!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Muses bright, and muses pale,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bare your faces of the veil;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let me see; and let me write
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the day, and of the night
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Both together: let me slake
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All my thirst for sweet heart-ache!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let my bower be of yew,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Interwreath'd with myrtles new;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pines and lime-trees full in bloom,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And my couch a low grass-tomb.
</pre>
