<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O lovely river of Yvette!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O darling river! like a bride,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some dimpled, bashful, fair Lisette,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou goest to wed the Orge's tide.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Maincourt, and lordly Dampierre,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;See and salute thee on thy way,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, with a blessing and a prayer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ring the sweet bells of St. Forget.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The valley of Chevreuse in vain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Would hold thee in its fond embrace;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou glidest from its arms again
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And hurriest on with swifter pace.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou wilt not stay; with restless feet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pursuing still thine onward flight,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou goest as one in haste to meet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Her sole desire, her head's delight.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O lovely river of Yvette!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O darling stream! on balanced wings
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The wood-birds sang the chansonnette
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That here a wandering poet sings.
</pre>
