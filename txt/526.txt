<p><cite class="al-title">A Treatise on Government</cite> (1776) was translated by William Ellis, serving as an essential reference philosophy text for those interested in comparing various forms of government, and finding out more about Aristotle's social contract theories. They diverged from Plato's and would serve particularly useful for American revolutionaries in the 18th century.
