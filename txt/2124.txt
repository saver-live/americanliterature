<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;LITTLE think'st thou, poor flower,<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whom I've watch'd six or seven days,<br> And seen thy birth, and seen what every hour<br> Gave to thy growth, thee to this height to raise,<br> And now dost laugh and triumph on this bough,<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Little think'st thou,<br> That it will freeze anon, and that I shall<br> To-morrow find thee fallen, or not at all.<br> <br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Little think'st thou, poor heart,<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That labourest yet to nestle thee,<br> And think'st by hovering here to get a part<br> In a forbidden or forbidding tree,<br> And hopest her stiffness by long siege to bow,<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Little think'st thou<br> That thou to-morrow, ere the sun doth wake,<br> Must with the sun and me a journey take.<br> <br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But thou, which lovest to be<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Subtle to plague thyself, wilt say,<br> Alas! if you must go, what's that to me?<br> Here lies my business, and here I will stay<br> You go to friends, whose love and means present<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Various content<br> To your eyes, ears, and taste, and every part;<br> If then your body go, what need your heart?<br> <br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Well then, stay here; but know,<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When thou hast stay'd and done thy most,<br> A naked thinking heart, that makes no show,<br> Is to a woman but a kind of ghost.<br> How shall she know my heart; or having none,<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Know thee for one?<br> Practice may make her know some other part;<br> But take my word, she doth not know a heart.<br> <br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Meet me in London, then,<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Twenty days hence, and thou shalt see<br> Me fresher and more fat, by being with men,<br> Than if I had stay'd still with her and thee.<br> For God's sake, if you can, be you so too;<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will give you<br> There to another friend, whom we shall find<br> As glad to have my body as my mind.</p>
