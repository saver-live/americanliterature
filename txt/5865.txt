<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In St. Luke's Gospel we are told
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How Peter in the days of old
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was sifted;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And now, though ages intervene,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sin is the same, while time and scene
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are shifted.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Satan desires us, great and small,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As wheat to sift us, and we all
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are tempted;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not one, however rich or great,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is by his station or estate
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Exempted.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No house so safely guarded is
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But he, by some device of his,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Can enter;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No heart hath armor so complete
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But he can pierce with arrows fleet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Its centre.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For all at last the cock will crow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who hear the warning voice, but go
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unheeding,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till thrice and more they have denied
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Man of Sorrows, crucified
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And bleeding.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One look of that pale suffering face
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Will make us feel the deep disgrace
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of weakness;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We shall be sifted till the strength
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of self-conceit be changed at length
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To meekness.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wounds of the soul, though healed will ache;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The reddening scars remain, and make
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Confession;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lost innocence returns no more;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We are not what we were before
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Transgression.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But noble souls, through dust and heat,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rise from disaster and defeat
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The stronger,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And conscious still of the divine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Within them, lie on earth supine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No longer.
</pre>
