<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She dwells by Great Kenhawa's side,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In valleys green and cool;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And all her hope and all her pride
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are in the village school.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Her soul, like the transparent air
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That robes the hills above,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Though not of earth, encircles there
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All things with arms of love.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And thus she walks among her girls
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With praise and mild rebukes;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Subduing e'en rude village churls
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By her angelic looks.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She reads to them at eventide
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of One who came to save;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To cast the captive's chains aside
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And liberate the slave.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And oft the blessed time foretells
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When all men shall be free;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And musical, as silver bells,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their falling chains shall be.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And following her beloved Lord,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In decent poverty,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She makes her life one sweet record
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And deed of charity.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For she was rich, and gave up all
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To break the iron bands
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of those who waited in her hall,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And labored in her lands.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Long since beyond the Southern Sea
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their outbound sails have sped,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While she, in meek humility,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now earns her daily bread.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is their prayers, which never cease,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That clothe her with such grace;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their blessing is the light of peace
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That shines upon her face.
</pre>
