<pre><i>To A Friend</i>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No! those days are gone away,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And their hours are old and gray,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And their minutes buried all
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Under the down-trodden pall
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the leaves of many years:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Many times have winters shears,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Frozen North, and chilling East,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sounded tempests to the feast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the forests whispering fleeces,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Since men knew nor rent nor leases.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No, the bugle sounds no more,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the twanging bow no more;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Silent is the ivory shrill
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Past the heath and up the hill;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is no mid-forest laugh,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where lone Echo gives the half
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To some wight, amazd to hear
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Jesting, deep in forest drear.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the fairest time of June
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You may go, with sun or moon,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or the seven stars to light you,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or the polar ray to right you;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But you never may behold
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Little John, or Robin bold;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never one, of all the clan,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thrumming on an empty can
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some old hunting ditty, while
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He doth his green way beguile
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To fair hostess Merriment,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Down beside the pasture Trent;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For he left the merry tale
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Messenger for spicy ale.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gone, the merry morris din;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gone, the song of Gamelyn;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gone, the tough-belted outlaw
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Idling in the grenè shawe;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All are gone away and past!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And if Robin should be cast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sudden from his turfed grave,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And if Marian should have
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Once again her forest days,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She would weep, and he would craze:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He would swear, for all his oaks,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Falln beneath the dockyard strokes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Have rotted on the briny seas;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She would weep that her wild bees
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sang not to her strange! that honey
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cant be got without hard money!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So it is: yet let us sing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Honour to the old bow-string!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Honour to the bugle-horn!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Honour to the woods unshorn!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Honour to the Lincoln green!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Honour to the archer keen!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Honour to tight Little John,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the horse he rode upon!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Honour to bold Robin Hood,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sleeping in the underwood!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Honour to Maid Marian,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And to all the Sherwood-clan!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Though their days have hurried by,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let us two a burden try.
</pre>
