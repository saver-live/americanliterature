<pre>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O faithful, indefatigable tides,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That evermore upon God's errands go,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now seaward bearing tidings of the land,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now landward bearing tidings of the sea,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And filling every frith and estuary,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Each arm of the great sea, each little creek,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Each thread and filament of water-courses,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Full with your ministration of delight!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Under the rafters of this wooden bridge
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I see you come and go; sometimes in haste
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To reach your journey's end, which being done
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With feet unrested ye return again
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And recommence the never-ending task;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Patient, whatever burdens ye may bear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And fretted only by the impeding rocks.
</pre>
