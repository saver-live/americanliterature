<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A mist was driving down the British Channel,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The day was just begun,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And through the window-panes, on floor and panel,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Streamed the red autumn sun.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It glanced on flowing flag and rippling pennon,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the white sails of ships;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, from the frowning rampart, the black cannon
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hailed it with feverish lips.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sandwich and Romney, Hastings, Hithe, and Dover
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Were all alert that day,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To see the French war-steamers speeding over,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When the fog cleared away.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sullen and silent, and like couchant lions,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their cannon, through the night,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Holding their breath, had watched, in grim defiance,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sea-coast opposite.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And now they roared at drum-beat from their stations
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On every citadel;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Each answering each, with morning salutations,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That all was well.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And down the coast, all taking up the burden,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Replied the distant forts,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As if to summon from his sleep the Warden
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Lord of the Cinque Ports.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Him shall no sunshine from the fields of azure,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No drum-beat from the wall,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No morning gun from the black fort's embrasure,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Awaken with its call!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No more, surveying with an eye impartial
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The long line of the coast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shall the gaunt figure of the old Field Marshal
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be seen upon his post!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For in the night, unseen, a single warrior,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In sombre harness mailed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dreaded of man, and surnamed the Destroyer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The rampart wall has scaled.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He passed into the chamber of the sleeper,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The dark and silent room,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And as he entered, darker grew, and deeper,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The silence and the gloom.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He did not pause to parley or dissemble,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But smote the Warden hoar;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah! what a blow! that made all England tremble
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And groan from shore to shore.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Meanwhile, without, the surly cannon waited,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sun rose bright o'erhead;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nothing in Nature's aspect intimated
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That a great man was dead.
</pre>
