<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Once the Emperor Charles of Spain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With his swarthy, grave commanders,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I forget in what campaign,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Long besieged, in mud and rain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some old frontier town of Flanders.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Up and down the dreary camp,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In great boots of Spanish leather,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Striding with a measured tramp,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These Hidalgos, dull and damp,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cursed the Frenchmen, cursed the weather.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus as to and fro they went,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over upland and through hollow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Giving their impatience vent,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Perched upon the Emperor's tent,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In her nest, they spied a swallow.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes, it was a swallow's nest,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Built of clay and hair of horses,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mane, or tail, or dragoon's crest,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Found on hedge-rows east and west,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;After skirmish of the forces.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then an old Hidalgo said,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As he twirled his gray mustachio,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Sure this swallow overhead
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thinks the Emperor's tent a shed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the Emperor but a Macho!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hearing his imperial name
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Coupled with those words of malice,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Half in anger, half in shame,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Forth the great campaigner came
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Slowly from his canvas palace.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Let no hand the bird molest,"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Said he solemnly, "nor hurt her!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adding then, by way of jest,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Golondrina is my guest,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'Tis the wife of some deserter!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Swift as bowstring speeds a shaft,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through the camp was spread the rumor,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the soldiers, as they quaffed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flemish beer at dinner, laughed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At the Emperor's pleasant humor.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So unharmed and unafraid
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sat the swallow still and brooded,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till the constant cannonade
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through the walls a breach had made,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the siege was thus concluded.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then the army, elsewhere bent,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Struck its tents as if disbanding,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Only not the Emperor's tent,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For he ordered, ere he went,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Very curtly, "Leave it standing!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So it stood there all alone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Loosely flapping, torn and tattered,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till the brood was fledged and flown,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Singing o'er those walls of stone
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which the cannon-shot had shattered.
</pre>
