<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mine is a wayward lay;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, if its echoing rhymes I try to string,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Proveth a truant thing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whenso some names I love, send it away!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For then, eyes swimming o'er,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And clasped hands, and smiles in fondness meant,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are much more eloquent,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So it had fain begone, and speak no more!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet shall it come again,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah, friend belov'd! if so thy wishes be,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, with wild melody,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will, upon thine ear, cadence my strain.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cadence my simple line,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unfashion'd by the cunning hand of Art,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But coming from my heart,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To tell the message of its love to thine!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As ocean shells, when taken
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From Ocean's bed, will faithfully repeat
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Her ancient music sweet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ev'n so these words, true to my heart, shall waken!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Oh! while our bark is seen,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Our little bark of kindly, social love,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Down life's clear stream to move
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Toward the summer shores, where all is green.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So long thy name shall bring,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Echoes of joy unto the grateful gales,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And thousand tender tales,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To freshen the fond hearts that round thee cling!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hast thou not look'd upon
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The flowerets of the field in lowly dress?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blame not my simpleness,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Think only of my love! my song is gone.
</pre>
