<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On your midnight pallet lying
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Listen, and undo the door:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lads that waste the light in sighing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the dark should sigh no more;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Night should ease a lover's sorrow;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Therefore, since I go to-morrow;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pity me before.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the land to which I travel,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The far dwelling, let me say-
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Once, if here the couch is gravel,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In a kinder bed I lay,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the breast the darnel smothers
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rested once upon another's
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When it was not clay.
</pre>
