<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Labor with what zeal we will,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Something still remains undone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Something uncompleted still
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Waits the rising of the sun.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the bedside, on the stair,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At the threshold, near the gates,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With its menace or its prayer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a mendicant it waits;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Waits, and will not go away;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Waits, and will not be gainsaid;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the cares of yesterday
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Each to-day is heavier made;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till at length the burden seems
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Greater than our strength can bear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Heavy as the weight of dreams,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pressing on us everywhere.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And we stand from day to day,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the dwarfs of times gone by,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who, as Northern legends say,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On their shoulders held the sky.
</pre>
