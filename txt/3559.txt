&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leave your home behind, lad,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And reach your friends your hand,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And go, and luck go with you
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While Ludlow tower shall stand.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Oh, come you home of Sunday
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When Ludlow streets are still
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Ludlow bells are calling
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To farm and lane and mill,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or come you home of Monday
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When Ludlow market hums
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Ludlow chimes are playing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"The conquering hero comes,"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come you home a hero,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or come not home at all,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The lads you leave will mind you
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till Ludlow tower shall fall.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And you will list the bugle
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That blows in lands of morn,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And make the foes of England
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be sorry you were born.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And you till trump of doomsday
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On lands of morn may lie,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And make the hearts of comrades
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be heavy where you die.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leave your home behind you,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Your friends by field and town
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Oh, town and field will mind you
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till Ludlow tower is down.
