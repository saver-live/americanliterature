<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O Solitude! if I must with thee dwell,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let it not be among the jumbled heap
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of murky buildings: climb with me the steep,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nature's observatory, whence the dell,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In flowery slopes, its river's crystal swell,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;May seem a span; let me thy vigils keep
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'Mongst boughs pavilioned, where the deer's swift leap
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Startles the wild bee from the foxglove bell.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But though I'll gladly trace these scenes with thee,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet the sweet converse of an innocent mind,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose words are images of thoughts refined,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is my soul's pleasure; and it sure must be
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Almost the highest bliss of human-kind,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When to thy haunts two kindred spirits flee.
</pre>
