<pre>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I saw the long line of the vacant shore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sea-weed and the shells upon the sand,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the brown rocks left bare on every hand,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As if the ebbing tide would flow no more.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then heard I, more distinctly than before,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The ocean breathe and its great breast expand,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And hurrying came on the defenceless land
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The insurgent waters with tumultuous roar.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All thought and feeling and desire, I said,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love, laughter, and the exultant joy of song
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Have ebbed from me forever!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suddenly o'er me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They swept again from their deep ocean bed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And in a tumult of delight, and strong
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As youth, and beautiful as youth, upbore me.
</pre>
