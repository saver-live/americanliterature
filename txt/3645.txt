&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Once in the wind of morning
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I ranged the thymy wold;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The world-wide air was azure
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And all the brooks ran gold.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There through the dews beside me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Behold a youth that trod,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With feathered cap on forehead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And poised a golden rod.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With mien to match the morning
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And gay delightful guise
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And friendly brows and laughter
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He looked me in the eyes.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Oh whence, I asked, and whither?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He smiled and would not say,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And looked at me and beckoned
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And laughed and led the way.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And with kind looks and laughter
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And nought to say beside
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We two went on together,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I and my happy guide.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Across the glittering pastures
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And empty upland still
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And solitude of shepherds
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;High in the folded hill,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By hanging woods and hamlets
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That gaze through orchards down
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On many a windmill turning
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And far-discovered town,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With gay regards of promise
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And sure unslackened stride
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And smiles and nothing spoken
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Led on my merry guide.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By blowing realms of woodland
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With sunstruck vanes afield
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And cloud-led shadows sailing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;About the windy weald,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By valley-guarded granges
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And silver waters wide,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Content at heart I followed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With my delightful guide.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And like the cloudy shadows
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Across the country blown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We two face on for ever,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But not we two alone.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the great gale we journey
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That breathes from gardens thinned,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Borne in the drift of blossoms
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose petals throng the wind;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Buoyed on the heaven-heard whisper
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of dancing leaflets whirled
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From all the woods that autumn
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bereaves in all the world.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And midst the fluttering legion
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of all that ever died
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I follow, and before us
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Goes the delightful guide,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With lips that brim with laughter
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But never once respond,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And feet that fly on feathers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And serpent-circled wand.
</pre>
