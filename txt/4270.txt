<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O Goddess! hear these tuneless numbers, wrung
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By sweet enforcement and remembrance dear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And pardon that thy secrets should be sung
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Even into thine own soft-conched ear:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Surely I dreamt to-day, or did I see
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The winged Psyche with awakend eyes?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I wanderd in a forest thoughtlessly,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, on the sudden, fainting with surprise,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Saw two fair creatures, couched side by side
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In deepest grass, beneath the whispring roof
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of leaves and trembled blossoms, where there ran
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A brooklet, scarce espied:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mid hushd, cool-rooted flowers, fragrant-eyed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blue, silver-white, and budded Tyrian,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They lay calm-breathing on the bedded grass;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their arms embraced, and their pinions too;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their lips touchd not, but had not bade adieu,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As if disjoined by soft-handed slumber,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And ready still past kisses to outnumber
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At tender eye-dawn of aurorean love:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The winged boy I knew;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But who wast thou, O happy, happy dove?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His Psyche true!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O latest born and loveliest vision far
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of all Olympus faded hierarchy!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fairer than Phoebes sapphire-regiond star,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or Vesper, amorous glow-worm of the sky;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fairer than these, though temple thou hast none,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor altar heapd with flowers;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor virgin-choir to make delicious moan
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Upon the midnight hours;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No voice, no lute, no pipe, no incense sweet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From chain-swung censer teeming;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No shrine, no grove, no oracle, no heat
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of pale-mouthd prophet dreaming.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O brightest! though too late for antique vows,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Too, too late for the fond believing lyre,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When holy were the haunted forest boughs,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Holy the air, the water, and the fire;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet even in these days so far retird
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From happy pieties, thy lucent fans,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fluttering among the faint Olympians,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I see, and sing, by my own eyes inspired.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So let me be thy choir, and make a moan
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Upon the midnight hours;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy voice, thy lute, thy pipe, thy incense sweet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From swinged censer teeming;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy shrine, thy grove, thy oracle, thy heat
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of pale-mouthd prophet dreaming.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes, I will be thy priest, and build a fane
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In some untrodden region of my mind,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where branched thoughts, new grown with pleasant pain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Instead of pines shall murmur in the wind:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Far, far around shall those dark-clusterd trees
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fledge the wild-ridged mountains steep by steep;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And there by zephyrs, streams, and birds, and bees,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The moss-lain Dryads shall be lulld to sleep;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And in the midst of this wide quietness
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A rosy sanctuary will I dress
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the wreathd trellis of a working brain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With buds, and bells, and stars without a name,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With all the gardener Fancy eer could feign,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who breeding flowers, will never breed the same:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And there shall be for thee all soft delight
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That shadowy thought can win,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A bright torch, and a casement open at night,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To let the warm Love in!
</pre>
