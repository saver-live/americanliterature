<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A handful of red sand, from the hot clime
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of Arab deserts brought,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Within this glass becomes the spy of Time,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The minister of Thought.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How many weary centuries has it been
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;About those deserts blown!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How many strange vicissitudes has seen,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How many histories known!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Perhaps the camels of the Ishmaelite
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Trampled and passed it o'er,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When into Egypt from the patriarch's sight
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His favorite son they bore.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Perhaps the feet of Moses, burnt and bare,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crushed it beneath their tread;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or Pharaoh's flashing wheels into the air
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Scattered it as they sped;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or Mary, with the Christ of Nazareth
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Held close in her caress,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose pilgrimage of hope and love and faith
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Illumed the wilderness;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or anchorites beneath Engaddi's palms
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pacing the Dead Sea beach,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And singing slow their old Armenian psalms
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In half-articulate speech;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or caravans, that from Bassora's gate
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With westward steps depart;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or Mecca's pilgrims, confident of Fate,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And resolute in heart!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These have passed over it, or may have passed!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now in this crystal tower
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Imprisoned by some curious hand at last,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It counts the passing hour,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And as I gaze, these narrow walls expand;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Before my dreamy eye
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stretches the desert with its shifting sand,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Its unimpeded sky.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And borne aloft by the sustaining blast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This little golden thread
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dilates into a column high and vast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A form of fear and dread.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And onward, and across the setting sun,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Across the boundless plain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The column and its broader shadow run,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till thought pursues in vain.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The vision vanishes!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These walls again
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shut out the lurid sun,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shut out the hot, immeasurable plain;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The half-hour's sand is run!
</pre>
