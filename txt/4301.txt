<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Byron! how sweetly sad thy melody!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Attuning still the soul to tenderness,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As if soft Pity, with unusual stress,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Had touch'd her plaintive lute, and thou, being by,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hadst caught the tones, nor suffer'd them to die.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'ershadowing sorrow doth not make thee less
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Delightful: thou thy griefs dost dress
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a bright halo, shining beamily,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As when a cloud the golden moon doth veil,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Its sides are ting'd with a resplendent glow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through the dark robe oft amber rays prevail,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And like fair veins in sable marble flow;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Still warble, dying swan! still tell the tale,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The enchanting tale, the tale of pleasing woe.
</pre>
