<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He is here, Uranias son,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hymen come from Helicon;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;God that glads the lovers heart,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He is here to join and part.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So the groomsman quits your side
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the bridegroom seeks the bride:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Friend and comrade yield you oer
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To her that hardly loves you more.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now the sun his skyward beam
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Has tilted from the Ocean stream.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Light the Indies, laggard sun:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Happy bridegroom, day is done,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the star from tas steep
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Calls to bed but not to sleep.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Happy bridegroom, Hesper brings
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All desired and timely things.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All whom morning sends to roam,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hesper loves to lead them home.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Home return who him behold,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Child to mother, sheep to fold,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bird to nest from wandering wide:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Happy bridegroom, seek your bride.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pour it out, the golden cup
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Given and guarded, brimming up,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Safe through jostling markets borne
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the thicket of the thorn;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Folly spurned and danger past,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pour it to the god at last.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now, to smother noise and light,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is stolen abroad the wildering night,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the blotting shades confuse
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Path and meadow full of dews;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the high heavens, that all control,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Turn in silence round the pole.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Catch the starry beams they shed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prospering the marriage bed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And breed the land that reared your prime
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sons to stay the rot of time.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All is quiet, no alarms;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nothing fear of nightly harms.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Safe you sleep on guarded ground,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And in silent circle round
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The thoughts of friends keep watch and ward,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Harnessed angels, hand on sword.
</pre>
