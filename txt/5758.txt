<pre>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All houses wherein men have lived and died
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are haunted houses.&nbsp;Through the open doors
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The harmless phantoms on their errands glide,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With feet that make no sound upon the floors.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We meet them at the door-way, on the stair,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Along the passages they come and go,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Impalpable impressions on the air,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A sense of something moving to and fro.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There are more guests at table, than the hosts
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Invited; the illuminated hall
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is thronged with quiet, inoffensive ghosts,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As silent as the pictures on the wall.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The stranger at my fireside cannot see
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The forms I see, nor hear the sounds I hear;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He but perceives what is; while unto me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All that has been is visible and clear.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We have no title-deeds to house or lands;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Owners and occupants of earlier dates
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From graves forgotten stretch their dusty hands,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And hold in mortmain still their old estates.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The spirit-world around this world of sense
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Floats like an atmosphere, and everywhere
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wafts through these earthly mists and vapors dense
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A vital breath of more ethereal air.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Our little lives are kept in equipoise
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By opposite attractions and desires;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The struggle of the instinct that enjoys,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the more noble instinct that aspires.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These perturbations, this perpetual jar
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of earthly wants and aspirations high,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come from the influence of an unseen star,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;An undiscovered planet in our sky.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And as the moon from some dark gate of cloud
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Throws o'er the sea a floating bridge of light,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Across whose trembling planks our fancies crowd
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into the realm of mystery and night,--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So from the world of spirits there descends
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A bridge of light, connecting it with this,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er whose unsteady floor, that sways and bends,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wander our thoughts above the dark abyss.
</pre>
