<pre>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How beautiful is the rain!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;After the dust and heat,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the broad and fiery street,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the narrow lane,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How beautiful is the rain!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How it clatters along the roofs,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the tramp of hoofs
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How it gushes and struggles out
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the throat of the overflowing spout!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Across the window-pane
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It pours and pours;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And swift and wide,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a muddy tide,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a river down the gutter roars
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The rain, the welcome rain!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sick man from his chamber looks
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At the twisted brooks;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He can feel the cool
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Breath of each little pool;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His fevered brain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Grows calm again,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And he breathes a blessing on the rain.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the neighboring school
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come the boys,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With more than their wonted noise
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And commotion;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And down the wet streets
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sail their mimic fleets,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till the treacherous pool
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ingulfs them in its whirling
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And turbulent ocean.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the country, on every side,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where far and wide,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a leopard's tawny and spotted hide,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stretches the plain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the dry grass and the drier grain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How welcome is the rain!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the furrowed land
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The toilsome and patient oxen stand;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lifting the yoke encumbered head,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With their dilated nostrils spread,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They silently inhale
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The clover-scented gale,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the vapors that arise
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the well-watered and smoking soil.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For this rest in the furrow after toil
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their large and lustrous eyes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seem to thank the Lord,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;More than man's spoken word.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Near at hand,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From under the sheltering trees,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The farmer sees
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His pastures, and his fields of grain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As they bend their tops
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the numberless beating drops
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the incessant rain.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He counts it as no sin
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That he sees therein
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Only his own thrift and gain.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These, and far more than these,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Poet sees!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He can behold
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aquarius old
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Walking the fenceless fields of air;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And from each ample fold
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the clouds about him rolled
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Scattering everywhere
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The showery rain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the farmer scatters his grain.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He can behold
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Things manifold
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That have not yet been wholly told,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Have not been wholly sung nor said.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For his thought, that never stops,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Follows the water-drops
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Down to the graves of the dead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Down through chasms and gulfs profound,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the dreary fountain-head
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of lakes and rivers under ground;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And sees them, when the rain is done,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the bridge of colors seven
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Climbing up once more to heaven,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Opposite the setting sun.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus the Seer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With vision clear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sees forms appear and disappear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the perpetual round of strange,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mysterious change
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From birth to death, from death to birth,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From earth to heaven, from heaven to earth;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till glimpses more sublime
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of things, unseen before,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unto his wondering eyes reveal
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Universe, as an immeasurable wheel
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Turning forevermore
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the rapid and rushing river of Time.
</pre>
