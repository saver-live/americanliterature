<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tuscan, that wanderest through the realms of gloom,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With thoughtful pace, and sad, majestic eyes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stern thoughts and awful from thy soul arise,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like Farinata from his fiery tomb.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy sacred song is like the trump of doom;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet in thy heart what human sympathies,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What soft compassion glows, as in the skies
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The tender stars their clouded lamps relume!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Methinks I see thee stand, with pallid cheeks,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By Fra Hilario in his diocese,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As up the convent-walls, in golden streaks,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The ascending sunbeams mark the day's decrease;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, as he asks what there the stranger seeks,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy voice along the cloister whispers, "Peace!"
</pre>
