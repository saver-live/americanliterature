<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This song of mine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is a Song of the Vine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To be sung by the glowing embers
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of wayside inns,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When the rain begins
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To darken the drear Novembers.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is not a song
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the Scuppernong,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From warm Carolinian valleys,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor the Isabel
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the Muscadel
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That bask in our garden alleys.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor the red Mustang,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose clusters hang
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er the waves of the Colorado,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the fiery flood
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of whose purple blood
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Has a dash of Spanish bravado.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For richest and best
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is the wine of the West,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That grows by the Beautiful River;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose sweet perfume
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fills all the room
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a benison on the giver.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And as hollow trees
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are the haunts of bees,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For ever going and coming;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So this crystal hive
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is all alive
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a swarming and buzzing and humming.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Very good in its way
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is the Verzenay,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or the Sillery soft and creamy;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But Catawba wine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Has a taste more divine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;More dulcet, delicious, and dreamy.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There grows no vine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the haunted Rhine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By Danube or Guadalquivir,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor on island or cape,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That bears such a grape
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As grows by the Beautiful River.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drugged is their juice
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For foreign use,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When shipped o'er the reeling Atlantic,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To rack our brains
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the fever pains,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That have driven the Old World frantic.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the sewers and sinks
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With all such drinks,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And after them tumble the mixer;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For a poison malign
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is such Borgia wine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or at best but a Devil's Elixir.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While pure as a spring
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is the wine I sing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And to praise it, one needs but name it;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For Catawba wine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Has need of no sign,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No tavern-bush to proclaim it.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And this Song of the Vine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This greeting of mine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The winds and the birds shall deliver
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the Queen of the West,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In her garlands dressed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the banks of the Beautiful River.
</pre>
