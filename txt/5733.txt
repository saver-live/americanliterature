<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Oft have I seen at some cathedral door
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A laborer, pausing in the dust and heat,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lay down his burden, and with reverent feet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enter, and cross himself, and on the floor
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kneel to repeat his paternoster o'er;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Far off the noises of the world retreat;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The loud vociferations of the street
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Become an undistinguishable roar.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So, as I enter here from day to day,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And leave my burden at this minster gate,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kneeling in prayer, and not ashamed to pray,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The tumult of the time disconsolate
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To inarticulate murmurs dies away,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While the eternal ages watch and wait.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;II

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How strange the sculptures that adorn these towers!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This crowd of statues, in whose folded sleeves
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Birds build their nests; while canopied with leaves
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Parvis and portal bloom like trellised bowers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the vast minster seems a cross of flowers!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But fiends and dragons on the gargoyled eaves
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Watch the dead Christ between the living thieves,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, underneath, the traitor Judas lowers!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah! from what agonies of heart and brain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What exultations trampling on despair,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What tenderness, what tears, what hate of wrong,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What passionate outcry of a soul in pain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Uprose this poem of the earth and air,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This medieval miracle of song!


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;III

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I enter, and I see thee in the gloom
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the long aisles, O poet saturnine!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And strive to make my steps keep pace with thine.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The air is filled with some unknown perfume;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The congregation of the dead make room
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For thee to pass; the votive tapers shine;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like rooks that haunt Ravenna's groves of pine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The hovering echoes fly from tomb to tomb.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the confessionals I hear arise
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rehearsals of forgotten tragedies,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And lamentations from the crypts below;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And then a voice celestial, that begins
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the pathetic words, "Although your sins
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As scarlet be," and ends with "as the snow."


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;IV

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With snow-white veil and garments as of flame,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She stands before thee, who so long ago
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Filled thy young heart with passion and the woe
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From which thy song and all its splendors came;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And while with stern rebuke she speaks thy name,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The ice about thy heart melts as the snow
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On mountain height; and in swift overflow
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Comes gushing from thy lips in sobs of shame.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou makest full confession; and a gleam,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As of the dawn on some dark forest cast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seems on thy lifted forehead to increase;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lethe and Eunoe--the remembered dream
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the forgotten sorrow--bring at last
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That perfect pardon which is perfect peace.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;V

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I lift mine eyes, and all the windows blaze
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With forms of saints and holy men who died,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here martyred and hereafter glorified;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the great Rose upon its leaves displays
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Christ's Triumph, and the angelic roundelays,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With splendor upon splendor multiplied;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Beatrice again at Dante's side
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No more rebukes, but smiles her words of praise.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And then the organ sounds, and unseen choirs
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sing the old Latin hymns of peace and love,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And benedictions of the Holy Ghost;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the melodious bells among the spires
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er all the house-tops and through heaven above
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Proclaim the elevation of the Host!


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;VI

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O star of morning and of liberty!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O bringer of the light, whose splendor shines
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Above the darkness of the Apennines,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Forerunner of the day that is to be!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The voices of the city and the sea,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The voices of the mountains and the pines,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Repeat thy song, till the familiar lines
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are footpaths for the thought of Italy!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy fame is blown abroad from all the heights,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through all the nations, and a sound is heard,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As of a mighty wind, and men devout,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Strangers of Rome, and the new proselytes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In their own language hear thy wondrous word,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And many are amazed and many doubt.
</pre>
