<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Oh, long had we paltered
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With bridle and girth
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ere those horses were haltered
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That gave us the Earth,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ere the Flame and the Fountain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Spark and the Wheel,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sank Ocean and Mountain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alike neath our keel.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the Wind in her blowing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The bird on the wind,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Made naught of our going,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And left us behind.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till the gale was outdriven,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The gull overflown,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And there matched us in Heaven
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Sun-God alone.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He only the master
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We leagued to oerthrow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He only the faster
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, therefore, our foe!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; .&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; .&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; .&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; .
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Light steals to uncurtain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The dim-shaping skies
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That arch and make certain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where he shall arise.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We lift to the onset.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We challenge anew.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From sunrise to sunset,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Apollo, pursue!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; .&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; .&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; .&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; .
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What ails thee, O Golden?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy Chariot is still?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What Power has withholden
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Way from the Will?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lo, Hesper hath paled not,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor darkness withdrawn.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Hours have availed not
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To lead forth the Dawn!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Do they flinch from full trial,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Coursers of Day?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The shade on our dial
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Moves swifter than they!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We fleet, but thou stayest
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A God unreleased;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And still thou delayest
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Low down in the East,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A beacon faint-burning,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A glare that decays
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the blasts of our spurning
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blow backward its blaze.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The mid-noon grows colder,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Night rushes to meet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the curve of Earths shoulder
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Heaves up thy defeat.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Storm on at that portal,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We have thee in prison!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Apollo, immortal,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou hast not arisen!
