<pre>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fire, Air, Earth, and Water,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Salamander, Zephyr, Dusketha, and Breama.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Salamander.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Happy, happy glowing fire!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Zephyr.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fragrant air! delicious light!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dusketha.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let me to my glooms retire!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Breama.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I to the green-wood rivers bright!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Salamander.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Happy, happy glowing fire!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dazzling bowers of soft retire,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ever let my nourish'd wing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a bat's, still wandering,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Faintly fan your fiery spaces,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Spirit sole in deadly places.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In unhaunted roar and blaze,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Open eyes that never daze,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let me see the myriad shapes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of men, and beasts, and fish, and apes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Portray'd in many a fiery den,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And wrought by spumy bitumen.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the deep intenser roof,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Arched every way aloof,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let me breathe upon their skies,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And anger their live tapestries;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Free from cold, and every care,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of chilly rain, and shivering air.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Zephyr.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Spirit of Fire! away! away!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or your very roundelay
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Will sear my plumage newly budded
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From its quilled sheath, all studded
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the self-same news that fell
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the May-grown Asphodel.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Spirit of Fire, away! away!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Breama.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Spirit of Fire, away! away!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Zephyr, blue-ey'd Faery turn,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And see my cool sedge-bury'd urn,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where it rests its mossy brim
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'Mid water-mint and cresses dim;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the flowers, in sweet troubles,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lift their eyes above the bubbles,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like our Queen, when she would please
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To sleep, and Oberon will teaze.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love me, blue-ey'd Faery, true!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Soothly I am sick for you.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Zephyr.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gentle Breama! by the first
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Violet young nature nurst,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will bathe myself with thee,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So you sometimes follow me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To my home, far, far, in west,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beyond the nimble-wheeled quest
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the golden-browed sun:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come with me, o'er tops of trees,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To my fragrant palaces,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where they ever floating are
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beneath the cherish of a star
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Call'd Vesper, who with silver veil
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ever hides his brilliance pale,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ever gently-drows'd doth keep
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Twilight for the Fayes to sleep.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fear not that your watery hair
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Will thirst in drouthy ringlets there;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Clouds of stored summer rains
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou shalt taste, before the stains
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the mountain soil they take,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And too unlucent for thee make.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I love thee, crystal Faery, true!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sooth I am as sick for you!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Salamander.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Out, ye aguish Faeries, out!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chilly lovers, what a rout
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Keep ye with your frozen breath,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Colder than the mortal death.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adder-eye'd Dusketha, speak,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shall we leave these, and go seek
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the earth's wide entrails old
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Couches warm as their's are cold?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O for a fiery gloom and thee,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dusketha, so enchantingly
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Freckle-wing'd and lizard-sided!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dusketha.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By thee, Sprite, will I be guided!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I care not for cold or heat;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Frost and flame, or sparks, or sleet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To my essence are the same; 
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But I honour more the flame.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Spirit of Fire, I follow thee
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wheresoever it may be,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the torrid spouts and fountains,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Underneath earth-quaked mountains;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or, at thy supreme desire,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Touch the very pulse of fire
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With my bare unlidded eyes.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Salamander.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sweet Dusketha! paradise!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Off, ye icy Spirits, fly!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Frosty creatures of the sky!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dusketha.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Breathe upon them, fiery sprite!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Zephyr and Breama.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Away! away to our delight!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Salamander.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Go, feed on icicles, while we
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bedded in tongue-flames will be.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dusketha.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lead me to those feverous glooms,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sprite of Fire!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Breama.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Me to the blooms,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blue-ey'd Zephyr, of those flowers
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Far in the west where the May-cloud lowers;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the beams of still Vesper, when winds are all wist,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are shed thro' the rain and the milder mist,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And twilight your floating bowers.
</pre>
