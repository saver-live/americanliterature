<pre>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When I meet the morning beam,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or lay me down at night to dream,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hear my bones within me say,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Another night, another day."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"When shall this slough of sense be cast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This dust of thoughts be laid at last,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The man of flesh and soul be slain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the man of bone remain?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"This tongue that talks, these lungs that shout,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These thews that hustle us about,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This brain that fills the skull with schemes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And its humming hive of dreams,-"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"These to-day are proud in power
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And lord it in their little hour:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The immortal bones obey control
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of dying flesh and dying soul."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;" 'Tis long till eve and morn are gone:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Slow the endless night comes on,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And late to fulness grows the birth
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That shall last as long as earth."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Wanderers eastward, wanderers west,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Know you why you cannot rest?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'Tis that every mother's son
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Travails with a skeleton."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Lie down in the bed of dust;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bear the fruit that bear you must;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bring the eternal seed to light,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And morn is all the same as night."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Rest you so from trouble sore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fear the heat o' the sun no more,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor the snowing winter wild,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now you labour not with child."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Empty vessel, garment cast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We that wore you long shall last.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;-Another night, another day."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So my bones within me say.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Therefore they shall do my will
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To-day while I am master still,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And flesh and soul, now both are strong,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shall hale the sullen slaves along,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Before this fire of sense decay,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This smoke of thought blow clean away,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And leave with ancient night alone
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The stedfast and enduring bone.

</pre>
