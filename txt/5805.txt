<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lo! in the painted oriel of the West,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose panes the sunken sun incarnadines,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a fair lady at her casement, shines
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The evening star, the star of love and rest!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And then anon she doth herself divest
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of all her radiant garments, and reclines
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Behind the sombre screen of yonder pines,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With slumber and soft dreams of love oppressed.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O my beloved, my sweet Hesperus!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My morning and my evening star of love!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My best and gentlest lady! even thus,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As that fair planet in the sky above,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dost thou retire unto thy rest at night,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And from thy darkened window fades the light.
</pre>
