<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mounted on Kyrat strong and fleet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His chestnut steed with four white feet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Roushan Beg, called Kurroglou,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Son of the road and bandit chief,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seeking refuge and relief,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Up the mountain pathway flew.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Such was Kyrat's wondrous speed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never yet could any steed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Reach the dust-cloud in his course.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;More than maiden, more than wife,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;More than gold and next to life
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Roushan the Robber loved his horse.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the land that lies beyond
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Erzeroum and Trebizond,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Garden-girt his fortress stood;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Plundered khan, or caravan
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Journeying north from Koordistan,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gave him wealth and wine and food.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seven hundred and fourscore
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Men at arms his livery wore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Did his bidding night and day.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now, through regions all unknown,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He was wandering, lost, alone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seeking without guide his way.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suddenly the pathway ends,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sheer the precipice descends,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Loud the torrent roars unseen;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thirty feet from side to side
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yawns the chasm; on air must ride
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He who crosses this ravine.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Following close in his pursuit,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At the precipice's foot,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Reyhan the Arab of Orfah
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Halted with his hundred men,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shouting upward from the glen,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"La Illah illa Allah!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gently Roushan Beg caressed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kyrat's forehead, neck, and breast;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kissed him upon both his eyes;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sang to him in his wild way,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As upon the topmost spray
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sings a bird before it flies.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"O my Kyrat, O my steed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Round and slender as a reed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Carry me this peril through!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Satin housings shall be thine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shoes of gold, O Kyrat mine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O thou soul of Kurroglou!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Soft thy skin as silken skein,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Soft as woman's hair thy mane,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tender are thine eyes and true;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All thy hoofs like ivory shine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Polished bright; O, life of mine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leap, and rescue Kurroglou!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kyrat, then, the strong and fleet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drew together his four white feet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Paused a moment on the verge,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Measured with his eye the space,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And into the air's embrace
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leaped as leaps the ocean surge.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the ocean surge o'er sand
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bears a swimmer safe to land,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kyrat safe his rider bore;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rattling down the deep abyss
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fragments of the precipice
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rolled like pebbles on a shore.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Roushan's tasselled cap of red
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Trembled not upon his head,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Careless sat he and upright;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Neither hand nor bridle shook,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor his head he turned to look,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As he galloped out of sight.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flash of harness in the air,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seen a moment like the glare
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of a sword drawn from its sheath;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus the phantom horseman passed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the shadow that he cast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leaped the cataract underneath.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Reyhan the Arab held his breath
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While this vision of life and death
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Passed above him.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Allahu!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cried he.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"In all Koordistan
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lives there not so brave a man
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As this Robber Kurroglou!"
</pre>
