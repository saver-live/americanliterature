<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;TRAVELLER
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why dost thou wildly rush and roar,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Mad River, O Mad River?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wilt thou not pause and cease to pour
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy hurrying, headlong waters o'er
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; This rocky shelf forever?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What secret trouble stirs thy breast?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Why all this fret and flurry?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dost thou not know that what is best
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In this too restless world is rest
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; From over-work and worry?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;THE RIVER
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What wouldst thou in these mountains seek,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; O stranger from the city?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is it perhaps some foolish freak
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of thine, to put the words I speak
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Into a plaintive ditty?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;TRAVELLER
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes; I would learn of thee thy song,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; With all its flowing number;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And in a voice as fresh and strong
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As thine is, sing it all day long,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; And hear it in my slumbers.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;THE RIVER
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A brooklet nameless and unknown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Was I at first, resembling
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A little child, that all alone
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Comes venturing down the stairs of stone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Irresolute and trembling.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Later, by wayward fancies led,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; For the wide world I panted;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Out of the forest dark and dread
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Across the open fields I fled,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Like one pursued and haunted.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I tossed my arms, I sang aloud,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; My voice exultant blending
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With thunder from the passing cloud,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The wind, the forest bent and bowed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The rush of rain descending.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I heard the distant ocean call,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Imploring and entreating;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drawn onward, o'er this rocky wall
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I plunged, and the loud waterfall
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Made answer to the greeting.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And now, beset with many ills,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; A toilsome life I follow;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Compelled to carry from the hills
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These logs to the impatient mills
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Below there in the hollow.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet something ever cheers and charms
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The rudeness of my labors;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Daily I water with these arms
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The cattle of a hundred farms,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; And have the birds for neighbors.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Men call me Mad, and well they may,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; When, full of rage and trouble,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I burst my banks of sand and clay,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And sweep their wooden bridge away,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Like withered reeds or stubble.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now go and write thy little rhyme,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; As of thine own creating.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou seest the day is past its prime;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I can no longer waste my time;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The mills are tired of waiting.
</pre>
