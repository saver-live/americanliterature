<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Burn, O evening hearth, and waken
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pleasant visions, as of old!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Though the house by winds be shaken,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Safe I keep this room of gold!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah, no longer wizard Fancy
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Builds her castles in the air,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Luring me by necromancy
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Up the never-ending stair!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But, instead, she builds me bridges
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over many a dark ravine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where beneath the gusty ridges
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cataracts dash and roar unseen.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I cross them, little heeding
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blast of wind or torrent's roar,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As I follow the receding
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Footsteps that have gone before.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Naught avails the imploring gesture,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Naught avails the cry of pain!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When I touch the flying vesture,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T is the gray robe of the rain.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Baffled I return, and, leaning
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er the parapets of cloud,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Watch the mist that intervening
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wraps the valley in its shroud.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the sounds of life ascending
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Faintly, vaguely, meet the ear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Murmur of bells and voices blending
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the rush of waters near.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Well I know what there lies hidden,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Every tower and town and farm,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And again the land forbidden
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Reassumes its vanished charm.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Well I know the secret places,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the nests in hedge and tree;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At what doors are friendly faces,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In what hearts are thoughts of me.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through the mist and darkness sinking,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blown by wind and beaten by shower,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Down I fling the thought I'm thinking,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Down I toss this Alpine flower.
</pre>
