<p><cite class="al-title">The Purple Cloud</cite> is a <a href="/dystopian-stories">Dystopian</a> "man-alone" story about a highly sought-after adventure to be the first to the North Pole, published as a serial in 1901. It was praised by both <a href="/author/hg-wells">H.G. Wells</a> and <a href="/author/hp-lovecraft">H.P. Lovecraft</a>. The <a href="http://biblehub.com/library/deane/pseudepigrapha/the_sibylline_oracles.htm">Sibyllian Oracles</a> referenced in the book's epigram are apocalyptic predictions, with themes similar to the Book of Revelation and other apocalyptic literature. <br><i>"estai kai Samos ammos, eseitai Daelos adaelos"</i></p><p><img alt="The Purple Cloud, Famous Fantastic Mysteries" title="The Purple Cloud, Famous Fantastic Mysteries cover" itemprop="image" src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/book/the-purple-cloud.jpg" style="max-width:400px; float:right; margin-right: 12px; margin-bottom: 12px;" data-pagespeed-url-hash="1411264931" onload="pagespeed.CriticalImages.checkImageForCriticality(this);"> </p><p><a href="introduction">Introduction</a></p><p><a href="the-purple-cloud">The Purple Cloud</a></p><p style="margin-top: 26px;"> Return to the <a href="/author/mp-shiel">M.P.
