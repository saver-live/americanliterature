<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Just above yon sandy bar,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the day grows fainter and dimmer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lonely and lovely, a single star
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lights the air with a dusky glimmer

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into the ocean faint and far
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Falls the trail of its golden splendor,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the gleam of that single star
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is ever refulgent, soft, and tender.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chrysaor, rising out of the sea,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Showed thus glorious and thus emulous,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leaving the arms of Callirrhoe,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Forever tender, soft, and tremulous.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus o'er the ocean faint and far
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Trailed the gleam of his falchion brightly;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is it a God, or is it a star
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That, entranced, I gaze on nightly!
</pre>
