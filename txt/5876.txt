<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Saint Augustine! well hast thou said,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That of our vices we can frame
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A ladder, if we will but tread
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beneath our feet each deed of shame!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All common things, each day's events,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That with the hour begin and end,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Our pleasures and our discontents,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are rounds by which we may ascend.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The low desire, the base design,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That makes another's virtues less;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The revel of the ruddy wine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And all occasions of excess;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The longing for ignoble things;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The strife for triumph more than truth;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The hardening of the heart, that brings
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Irreverence for the dreams of youth;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All thoughts of ill; all evil deeds,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That have their root in thoughts of ill;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whatever hinders or impedes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The action of the nobler will;--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All these must first be trampled down
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beneath our feet, if we would gain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the bright fields of fair renown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The right of eminent domain.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We have not wings, we cannot soar;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But we have feet to scale and climb
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By slow degrees, by more and more,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The cloudy summits of our time.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The mighty pyramids of stone
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That wedge-like cleave the desert airs,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When nearer seen, and better known,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are but gigantic flights of stairs.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The distant mountains, that uprear
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their solid bastions to the skies,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are crossed by pathways, that appear
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As we to higher levels rise.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The heights by great men reached and kept
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Were not attained by sudden flight,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But they, while their companions slept,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Were toiling upward in the night.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Standing on what too long we bore
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With shoulders bent and downcast eyes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We may discern--unseen before--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A path to higher destinies.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor deem the irrevocable Past,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As wholly wasted, wholly vain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If, rising on its wrecks, at last
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To something nobler we attain.
</pre>
