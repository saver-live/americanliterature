<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like two cathedral towers these stately pines
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Uplift their fretted summits tipped with cones;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The arch beneath them is not built with stones,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not Art but Nature traced these lovely lines,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And carved this graceful arabesque of vines;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No organ but the wind here sighs and moans,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No sepulchre conceals a martyr's bones.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No marble bishop on his tomb reclines.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enter! the pavement, carpeted with leaves,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gives back a softened echo to thy tread!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Listen! the choir is singing; all the birds,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In leafy galleries beneath the eaves,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are singing! listen, ere the sound be fled,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And learn there may be worship with out words.
</pre>
