<p><span style="display:inline-block; width:4em;">&nbsp;</span>WHILST yet to prove<br> I thought there was some deity in love,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>So did I reverence, and gave<br> Worship; as atheists at their dying hour<br> Call, what they cannot name, an unknown power,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>As ignorantly did I crave.<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:4em;">&nbsp;</span><span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Thus when<br> Things not yet known are coveted by men,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Our desires give them fashion, and so<br> As they wax lesser, fall, as they size, grow.<br> <br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:4em;">&nbsp;</span>But, from late fair,<br> His highness sitting in a golden chair,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Is not less cared for after three days<br> By children, than the thing which lovers so<br> Blindly admire, and with such worship woo;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Being had, enjoying it decays;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:4em;">&nbsp;</span><span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>And thence,<br> What before pleased them all, takes but one sense,<br> And that so lamely, as it leaves behind<br> A kind of sorrowing dulness to the mind.<br> <br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:4em;">&nbsp;</span>Ah cannot we,<br> As well as cocks and lions, jocund be<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>After such pleasures, unless wise<br> Nature decreed—since each such act, they say,<br> Diminisheth the length of life a day—<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>This; as she would man should despise<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:4em;">&nbsp;</span><span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>The sport,<br> Because that other curse of being short,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>And only for a minute made to be<br> Eager, desires to raise posterity.<br> <br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:4em;">&nbsp;</span>Since so, my mind<br> Shall not desire what no man else can find;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>I'll no more dote and run<br> To pursue things which had endamaged me;<br> And when I come where moving beauties be,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>As men do when the summer's sun<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:4em;">&nbsp;</span><span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Grows great,<br> Though I admire their greatness, shun their heat.<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Each place can afford shadows; if all fail,<br> 'Tis but applying worm-seed to the tail.</p>
