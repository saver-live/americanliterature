<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sweet the memory is to me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of a land beyond the sea,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where the waves and mountains meet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where, amid her mulberry-trees
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sits Amalfi in the heat,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bathing ever her white feet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the tideless summer seas.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the middle of the town,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From its fountains in the hills,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tumbling through the narrow gorge,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Canneto rushes down,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Turns the great wheels of the mills,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lifts the hammers of the forge.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T is a stairway, not a street,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That ascends the deep ravine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where the torrent leaps between
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rocky walls that almost meet.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Toiling up from stair to stair
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Peasant girls their burdens bear;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sunburnt daughters of the soil,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stately figures tall and straight,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What inexorable fate
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dooms them to this life of toil?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lord of vineyards and of lands,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Far above the convent stands.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On its terraced walk aloof
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leans a monk with folded hands,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Placid, satisfied, serene,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Looking down upon the scene
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over wall and red-tiled roof;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wondering unto what good end
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All this toil and traffic tend,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And why all men cannot be
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Free from care and free from pain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the sordid love of gain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And as indolent as he.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where are now the freighted barks
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the marts of east and west?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where the knights in iron sarks
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Journeying to the Holy Land,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Glove of steel upon the hand,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cross of crimson on the breast?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where the pomp of camp and court?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where the pilgrims with their prayers?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where the merchants with their wares,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And their gallant brigantines
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sailing safely into port
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chased by corsair Algerines?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vanished like a fleet of cloud,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a passing trumpet-blast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are those splendors of the past,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the commerce and the crowd!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fathoms deep beneath the seas
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lie the ancient wharves and quays,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Swallowed by the engulfing waves;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Silent streets and vacant halls,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ruined roofs and towers and walls;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hidden from all mortal eyes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Deep the sunken city lies:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Even cities have their graves!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This is an enchanted land!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Round the headlands far away
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sweeps the blue Salernian bay
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With its sickle of white sand:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Further still and furthermost
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the dim discovered coast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Paestum with its ruins lies,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And its roses all in bloom
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seem to tinge the fatal skies
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of that lonely land of doom.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On his terrace, high in air,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nothing doth the good monk care
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For such worldly themes as these,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the garden just below
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Little puffs of perfume blow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And a sound is in his ears
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the murmur of the bees
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the shining chestnut-trees;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nothing else he heeds or hears.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All the landscape seems to swoon
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the happy afternoon;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Slowly o'er his senses creep
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The encroaching waves of sleep,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And he sinks as sank the town,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unresisting, fathoms down,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into caverns cool and deep!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Walled about with drifts of snow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hearing the fierce north-wind blow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seeing all the landscape white,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the river cased in ice,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Comes this memory of delight,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Comes this vision unto me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of a long-lost Paradise
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the land beyond the sea.
</pre>
