<p>Wharton's novel <span class="al-title">Summer</span> (1917) is one of only two she sets in New England, this one following <span class="al-char">Charity Royall's</span> journey of sexual awakening.</p><p><span class="al-char">Charity</span> comes from poverty and is taken in by the wealthy and successful <span class="al-char">Lawyer Royall</span> when she is five years old. <span class="al-char">Lawyer</span> develops a romantic interest in <span class="al-char">Charity</span> after his wife dies, but things go awry. First when <span class="al-char">Lawyer</span> loses drunken control and enters her bedroom when she is seventeen and then again in response to the apparent love between her and the already betrothed young architect, <span class="al-char">Lucius Harney</span>.
