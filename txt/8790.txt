<p><cite class="al-title">The Food of the Gods and How It Came to Earth</cite> is Wells' lesser known work of science fiction (1904) wreaking in satire, in which so-called "scientists" invent a superfood that grows children into giants (as if parents need BIGGER problems with their teenagers!). It was made into several B-movies, including <cite class="al-title">Village of the Giants</cite> (1965), featuring an 11-year old <cite class="al-person">Ron Howard</cite> who creates the "goo." His novel is featured in <a href="/foodie-stories#foodie-books">Foodie Books</a> and <a href="/dystopian-stories">Dystopian Stories</a>.</p><p><i>"'Jab!' a fresh supply of the Food of the Gods was let loose to wreak its powers of giantry upon the world."</i></p><p><img alt="The Food of the Gods, Village of the Giants poster, 1965" title="The Food of the Gods, Village of the Giants poster, 1965" itemprop="image" src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/book/the-food-of-the-gods-village-of-the-giants-1965.jpg" style="max-width:350px;
