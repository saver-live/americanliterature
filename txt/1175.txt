<p><cite class="al-author">Charlotte Bronte</cite> published <cite class="al-title">Jane Eyre</cite> on October 16, 1847 under the pseudonym, <cite class="al-author">Currer Bell</cite>. The American edition appeared a year later. The novel follows the classic <em>Bildungsroman</em> genre; structured as a coming-of-age story where <cite class="al-title">Jane Eyre</cite> progresses from a young orphan into a woman; the reader follows the heroine's character development, emotional travails, and moral development. </p><p> <cite class="al-title">Jane Eyre</cite>'s setting is an unspecified location in northern England, presented as five distinct stages: 1) Jane's childhood where, as an orphan at <cite>Gateshead Hall</cite> she is tormented by her cousin John; 2) She is then sent away to a charity school, <cite>Lowood School</cite>; 3) Her governorship at <cite>Thornfield Hall</cite> where she falls in love with <cite class="al-char">Edward Rochester</cite>; 4) Her time with the <cite class="al-char">Rivers family</cite> where she receives her first marriage proposal; and finally; 5) Her reunion and marriage to her beloved, <cite class="al-char">Rochester</cite>. </p><p><i>"I am no bird; and no net ensnares me: I am a free human being with an independent will.‚Äù</i> </p><p> <cite class="al-author">Charlotte Bronte</cite> dedicated the novel to <cite><a href="/author/william-makepeace-thackeray/bio-books-stories">William Makepeace Thackeray</a></cite>.
