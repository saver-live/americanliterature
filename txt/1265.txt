&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Loving friend, the gift of one,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who, her own true faith, hath run,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through thy lower nature;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be my benediction said
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With my hand upon thy head,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gentle fellow-creature!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a lady's ringlets brown,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flow thy silken ears adown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Either side demurely,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of thy silver-suited breast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shining out from all the rest
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of thy body purely.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Darkly brown thy body is,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till the sunshine, striking this,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alchemize its dulness,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When the sleek curls manifold
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flash all over into gold,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a burnished fulness.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Underneath my stroking hand,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Startled eyes of hazel bland
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kindling, growing larger,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Up thou leapest with a spring,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Full of prank and curvetting,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leaping like a charger.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leap! thy broad tail waves a light;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leap! thy slender feet are bright,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Canopied in fringes.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leap those tasselled ears of thine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flicker strangely, fair and fine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Down their golden inches

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet, my pretty sportive friend,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Little is 't to such an end
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That I praise thy rareness!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Other dogs may be thy peers
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Haply in these drooping ears,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And this glossy fairness.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But of thee it shall be said,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This dog watched beside a bed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Day and night unweary,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Watched within a curtained room,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where no sunbeam brake the gloom
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Round the sick and dreary.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Roses, gathered for a vase,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In that chamber died apace,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beam and breeze resigning
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This dog only, waited on,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Knowing that when light is gone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love remains for shining.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Other dogs in thymy dew
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tracked the hares and followed through
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sunny moor or meadow
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This dog only, crept and crept
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Next a languid cheek that slept,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sharing in the shadow.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Other dogs of loyal cheer
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bounded at the whistle clear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Up the woodside hieing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This dog only, watched in reach
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of a faintly uttered speech,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or a louder sighing.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And if one or two quick tears
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dropped upon his glossy ears,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or a sigh came double,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Up he sprang in eager haste,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fawning, fondling, breathing fast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In a tender trouble.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And this dog was satisfied,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If a pale thin hand would glide,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Down his dewlaps sloping,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which he pushed his nose within,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;After, platforming his chin
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the palm left open.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This dog, if a friendly voice
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Call him now to blyther choice
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Than such chamber-keeping,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come out! 'praying from the door,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Presseth backward as before,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Up against me leaping.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Therefore to this dog will I,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tenderly not scornfully,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Render praise and favour!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With my hand upon his head,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is my benediction said
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Therefore, and for ever.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And because he loves me so,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Better than his kind will do
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Often, man or woman,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Give I back more love again
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Than dogs often take of men,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leaning from my Human.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blessings on thee, dog of mine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pretty collars make thee fine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sugared milk make fat thee!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pleasures wag on in thy tail
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hands of gentle motion fail
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nevermore, to pat thee!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Downy pillow take thy head,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Silken coverlid bestead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sunshine help thy sleeping!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No fly 's buzzing wake thee up
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No man break thy purple cup,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Set for drinking deep in.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whiskered cats arointed flee
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sturdy stoppers keep from thee
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cologne distillations;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nuts lie in thy path for stones,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And thy feast-day macaroons
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Turn to daily rations!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mock I thee, in wishing weal ?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tears are in my eyes to feel
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou art made so straightly,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blessing needs must straighten too,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Little canst thou joy or do,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou who lovest greatly.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet be blessed to the height
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of all good and all delight
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pervious to thy nature,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Only loved beyond that line,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a love that answers thine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Loving fellow-creature!
