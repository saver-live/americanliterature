<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dear child! how radiant on thy mother's knee,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With merry-making eyes and jocund smiles,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou gazest at the painted tiles,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose figures grace,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With many a grotesque form and face.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The ancient chimney of thy nursery!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The lady with the gay macaw,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The dancing girl, the grave bashaw
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With bearded lip and chin;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, leaning idly o'er his gate,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beneath the imperial fan of state,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Chinese mandarin.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With what a look of proud command
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou shakest in thy little hand
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The coral rattle with its silver bells,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Making a merry tune!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thousands of years in Indian seas
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That coral grew, by slow degrees,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Until some deadly and wild monsoon
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dashed it on Coromandel's sand!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Those silver bells
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Reposed of yore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As shapeless ore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Far down in the deep-sunken wells
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of darksome mines,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In some obscure and sunless place,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beneath huge Chimborazo's base,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or Potosi's o'erhanging pines
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And thus for thee, O little child,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through many a danger and escape,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The tall ships passed the stormy cape;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For thee in foreign lands remote,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beneath a burning, tropic clime,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Indian peasant, chasing the wild goat,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Himself as swift and wild,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In falling, clutched the frail arbute,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The fibres of whose shallow root,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Uplifted from the soil, betrayed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The silver veins beneath it laid,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The buried treasures of the miser, Time.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But, lo! thy door is left ajar!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou hearest footsteps from afar!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, at the sound,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou turnest round
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With quick and questioning eyes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like one, who, in a foreign land,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beholds on every hand
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some source of wonder and surprise!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, restlessly, impatiently,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou strivest, strugglest, to be free,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The four walls of thy nursery
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are now like prison walls to thee.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No more thy mother's smiles,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No more the painted tiles,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Delight thee, nor the playthings on the floor,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That won thy little, beating heart before;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou strugglest for the open door.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through these once solitary halls
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy pattering footstep falls.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sound of thy merry voice
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Makes the old walls
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Jubilant, and they rejoice
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the joy of thy young heart,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er the light of whose gladness
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No shadows of sadness
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the sombre background of memory start.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Once, ah, once, within these walls,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One whom memory oft recalls,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Father of his Country, dwelt.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And yonder meadows broad and damp
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The fires of the besieging camp
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Encircled with a burning belt.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Up and down these echoing stairs,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Heavy with the weight of cares,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sounded his majestic tread;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes, within this very room
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sat he in those hours of gloom,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Weary both in heart and head.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But what are these grave thoughts to thee?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Out, out! into the open air!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy only dream is liberty,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou carest little how or where.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I see thee eager at thy play,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now shouting to the apples on the tree,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With cheeks as round and red as they;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And now among the yellow stalks,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Among the flowering shrubs and plants,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As restless as the bee.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Along the garden walks,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The tracks of thy small carriage-wheels I trace;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And see at every turn how they efface
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whole villages of sand-roofed tents,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That rise like golden domes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Above the cavernous and secret homes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of wandering and nomadic tribes of ants.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah, cruel little Tamerlane,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who, with thy dreadful reign,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dost persecute and overwhelm
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These hapless Troglodytes of thy realm!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What! tired already! with those suppliant looks,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And voice more beautiful than a poet's books,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or murmuring sound of water as it flows.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou comest back to parley with repose;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This rustic seat in the old apple-tree,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With its o'erhanging golden canopy
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of leaves illuminate with autumnal hues,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And shining with the argent light of dews,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shall for a season be our place of rest.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beneath us, like an oriole's pendent nest,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From which the laughing birds have taken wing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By thee abandoned, hangs thy vacant swing.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dream-like the waters of the river gleam;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A sailless vessel drops adown the stream,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And like it, to a sea as wide and deep,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou driftest gently down the tides of sleep.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O child! O new-born denizen
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of life's great city! on thy head
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The glory of the morn is shed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a celestial benison!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here at the portal thou dost stand,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And with thy little hand
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou openest the mysterious gate
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into the future's undiscovered land.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I see its valves expand,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As at the touch of Fate!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into those realms of love and hate,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into that darkness blank and drear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By some prophetic feeling taught,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I launch the bold, adventurous thought,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Freighted with hope and fear;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As upon subterranean streams,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In caverns unexplored and dark,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Men sometimes launch a fragile bark,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Laden with flickering fire,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And watch its swift-receding beams,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Until at length they disappear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And in the distant dark expire.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By what astrology of fear or hope
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dare I to cast thy horoscope!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the new moon thy life appears;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A little strip of silver light,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And widening outward into night
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The shadowy disk of future years;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And yet upon its outer rim,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A luminous circle, faint and dim,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And scarcely visible to us here,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rounds and completes the perfect sphere;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A prophecy and intimation,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A pale and feeble adumbration,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the great world of light, that lies
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Behind all human destinies.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah! if thy fate, with anguish fraught,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Should be to wet the dusty soil
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the hot tears and sweat of toil,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To struggle with imperious thought,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Until the overburdened brain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Weary with labor, faint with pain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a jarred pendulum, retain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Only its motion, not its power,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Remember, in that perilous hour,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When most afflicted and oppressed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From labor there shall come forth rest.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And if a more auspicious fate
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On thy advancing steps await
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Still let it ever be thy pride
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To linger by the laborer's side;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With words of sympathy or song
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To cheer the dreary march along
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the great army of the poor,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er desert sand, o'er dangerous moor.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor to thyself the task shall be
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Without reward; for thou shalt learn
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The wisdom early to discern
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;True beauty in utility;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As great Pythagoras of yore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Standing beside the blacksmith's door,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And hearing the hammers, as they smote
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The anvils with a different note,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stole from the varying tones, that hung
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vibrant on every iron tongue,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The secret of the sounding wire.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And formed the seven-chorded lyre.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enough! I will not play the Seer;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will no longer strive to ope
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The mystic volume, where appear
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The herald Hope, forerunning Fear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Fear, the pursuivant of Hope.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy destiny remains untold;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For, like Acestes' shaft of old,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The swift thought kindles as it flies,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And burns to ashes in the skies.
</pre>
