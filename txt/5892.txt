<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The old house by the lindens
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stood silent in the shade,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And on the gravelled pathway
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The light and shadow played.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I saw the nursery windows
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wide open to the air;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the faces of the children,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They were no longer there.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The large Newfoundland house-dog
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was standing by the door;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He looked for his little playmates,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who would return no more.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They walked not under the lindens,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They played not in the hall;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But shadow, and silence, and sadness
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Were hanging over all.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The birds sang in the branches,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With sweet, familiar tone;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the voices of the children
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Will be heard in dreams alone!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the boy that walked beside me,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He could not understand
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why closer in mine, ah! closer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I pressed his warm, soft hand!
</pre>
