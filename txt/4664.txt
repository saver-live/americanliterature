<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rain on the face of the sea,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rain on the sodden land,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the window-pane is blurred with rain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As I watch it, pen in hand.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mist on the face of the sea,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mist on the sodden land,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Filling the vales as daylight fails,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And blotting the desolate sand.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Voices from out of the mist,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Calling to one another:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Hath love an end, thou more than friend,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou dearer than ever brother?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Voices from out of the mist,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Calling and passing away;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But I cannot speak, for my voice is weak,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And ... this is the end of my lay.
</pre>
