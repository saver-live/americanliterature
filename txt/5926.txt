<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Welcome, my old friend,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Welcome to a foreign fireside,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While the sullen gales of autumn
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shake the windows.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The ungrateful world
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Has, it seems, dealt harshly with thee,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Since, beneath the skies of Denmark,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;First I met thee.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There are marks of age,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There are thumb-marks on thy margin,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Made by hands that clasped thee rudely,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At the alehouse.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Soiled and dull thou art;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yellow are thy time-worn pages,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the russet, rain-molested
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leaves of autumn.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou art stained with wine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Scattered from hilarious goblets,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the leaves with the libations
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of Olympus.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet dost thou recall
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Days departed, half-forgotten,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When in dreamy youth I wandered
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the Baltic,--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When I paused to hear
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The old ballad of King Christian
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shouted from suburban taverns
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the twilight.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou recallest bards,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who in solitary chambers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And with hearts by passion wasted,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wrote thy pages.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou recallest homes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where thy songs of love and friendship
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Made the gloomy Northern winter
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bright as summer.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Once some ancient Scald,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In his bleak, ancestral Iceland,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chanted staves of these old ballads
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the Vikings.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Once in Elsinore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At the court of old King Hamlet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yorick and his boon companions
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sang these ditties.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Once Prince Frederick's Guard
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sang them in their smoky barracks;--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suddenly the English cannon
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Joined the chorus!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Peasants in the field,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sailors on the roaring ocean,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Students, tradesmen, pale mechanics,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All have sung them.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou hast been their friend;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They, alas! have left thee friendless!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet at least by one warm fireside
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Art thou welcome.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, as swallows build
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In these wide, old-fashioned chimneys,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So thy twittering songs shall nestle
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In my bosom,--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Quiet, close, and warm,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sheltered from all molestation,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And recalling by their voices
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Youth and travel.
</pre>
