<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;JANUARY

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Janus am I; oldest of potentates;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Forward I look, and backward, and below
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I count, as god of avenues and gates,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The years that through my portals come and go.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I block the roads, and drift the fields with snow;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I chase the wild-fowl from the frozen fen;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My frosts congeal the rivers in their flow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My fires light up the hearths and hearts of men.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;FEBRUARY

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am lustration, and the sea is mine.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I wash the sands and headlands with my tide;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My brow is crowned with branches of the pine;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Before my chariot-wheels the fishes glide.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By me all things unclean are purified,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By me the souls of men washed white again;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;E'en the unlovely tombs of those who died
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Without a dirge, I cleanse from every stain.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;MARCH

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I Martius am!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Once first, and now the third!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To lead the Year was my appointed place;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A mortal dispossessed me by a word,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And set there Janus with the double face.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hence I make war on all the human race;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I shake the cities with my hurricanes;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I flood the rivers and their banks efface,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And drown the farms and hamlets with my rains.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;APRIL

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I open wide the portals of the Spring
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To welcome the procession of the flowers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With their gay banners, and the birds that sing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their song of songs from their aerial towers.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I soften with my sunshine and my showers
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The heart of earth; with thoughts of love I glide
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into the hearts of men; and with the Hours
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Upon the Bull with wreathed horns I ride.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;MAY

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hark!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sea-faring wild-fowl loud proclaim
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My coming, and the swarming of the bees.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These are my heralds, and behold! my name
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is written in blossoms on the hawthorn-trees.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I tell the mariner when to sail the seas;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I waft o'er all the land from far away
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The breath and bloom of the Hesperides,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My birthplace.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am Maia.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am May.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;JUNE

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mine is the Month of Roses; yes, and mine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Month of Marriages!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All pleasant sights
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And scents, the fragrance of the blossoming vine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The foliage of the valleys and the heights.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mine are the longest days, the loveliest nights;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The mower's scythe makes music to my ear;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am the mother of all dear delights;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am the fairest daughter of the year.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;JULY

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My emblem is the Lion, and I breathe
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The breath of Libyan deserts o'er the land;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My sickle as a sabre I unsheathe,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And bent before me the pale harvests stand.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The lakes and rivers shrink at my command,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And there is thirst and fever in the air;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sky is changed to brass, the earth to sand;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am the Emperor whose name I bear.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;AUGUST

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Emperor Octavian, called the August,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I being his favorite, bestowed his name
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Upon me, and I hold it still in trust,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In memory of him and of his fame.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am the Virgin, and my vestal flame
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Burns less intensely than the Lion's rage;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sheaves are my only garlands, and I claim
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The golden Harvests as my heritage.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SEPTEMBER

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I bear the Scales, where hang in equipoise
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The night and day; and when unto my lips
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I put my trumpet, with its stress and noise
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fly the white clouds like tattered sails of ships;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The tree-tops lash the air with sounding whips;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Southward the clamorous sea-fowl wing their flight;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The hedges are all red with haws and hips,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Hunter's Moon reigns empress of the night.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;OCTOBER

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My ornaments are fruits; my garments leaves,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Woven like cloth of gold, and crimson dyed;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I do not boast the harvesting of sheaves,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er orchards and o'er vineyards I preside.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Though on the frigid Scorpion I ride,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The dreamy air is full, and overflows
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With tender memories of the summer-tide,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And mingled voices of the doves and crows.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;NOVEMBER

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Centaur, Sagittarius, am I,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Born of Ixion's and the cloud's embrace;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With sounding hoofs across the earth I fly,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A steed Thessalian with a human face.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sharp winds the arrows are with which I chase
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The leaves, half dead already with affright;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I shroud myself in gloom; and to the race
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of mortals bring nor comfort nor delight.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;DECEMBER

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Riding upon the Goat, with snow-white hair,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I come, the last of all.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This crown of mine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is of the holly; in my hand I bear
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The thyrsus, tipped with fragrant cones of pine.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I celebrate the birth of the Divine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the return of the Saturnian reign;--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My songs are carols sung at every shrine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Proclaiming "Peace on earth, good will to men."
</pre>
