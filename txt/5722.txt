<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come to me, O ye children!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For I hear you at your play,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the questions that perplexed me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Have vanished quite away.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ye open the eastern windows,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That look towards the sun,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where thoughts are singing swallows
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the brooks of morning run.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In your hearts are the birds and the sunshine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In your thoughts the brooklet's flow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But in mine is the wind of Autumn
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the first fall of the snow.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah! what would the world be to us
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If the children were no more?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We should dread the desert behind us
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Worse than the dark before.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What the leaves are to the forest,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With light and air for food,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ere their sweet and tender juices
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Have been hardened into wood,--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That to the world are children;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through them it feels the glow
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of a brighter and sunnier climate
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Than reaches the trunks below.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come to me, O ye children!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And whisper in my ear
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What the birds and the winds are singing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In your sunny atmosphere.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For what are all our contrivings,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the wisdom of our books,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When compared with your caresses,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the gladness of your looks?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ye are better than all the ballads
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That ever were sung or said;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For ye are living poems,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And all the rest are dead.
</pre>
