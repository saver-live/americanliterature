<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O golden-tongued Romance with serene lute!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fair plumed Syren! Queen of far away!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leave melodizing on this wintry day,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shut up thine olden pages, and be mute:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adieu! for once again the fierce dispute,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Betwixt damnation and impassion'd clay
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Must I burn through; once more humbly assay
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The bitter-sweet of this Shakespearian fruit.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chief Poet! and ye clouds of Albion,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Begetters of our deep eternal theme,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When through the old oak forest I am gone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let me not wander in a barren dream,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But when I am consumed in the fire,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Give me new Phoenix wings to fly at my desire.
</pre>
