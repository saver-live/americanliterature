<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How much of my young heart, O Spain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Went out to thee in days of yore!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What dreams romantic filled my brain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And summoned back to life again
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Paladins of Charlemagne
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The Cid Campeador!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And shapes more shadowy than these,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the dim twilight half revealed;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Phoenician galleys on the seas,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Roman camps like hives of bees,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Goth uplifting from his knees
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pelayo on his shield.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was these memories perchance,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From annals of remotest eld,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That lent the colors of romance
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To every trivial circumstance,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And changed the form and countenance
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of all that I beheld.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Old towns, whose history lies hid
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In monkish chronicle or rhyme,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Burgos, the birthplace of the Cid,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Zamora and Valladolid,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Toledo, built and walled amid
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The wars of Wamba's time;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The long, straight line of the high-way,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The distant town that seems so near,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The peasants in the fields, that stay
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their toil to cross themselves and pray,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When from the belfry at midday
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Angelus they hear;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;White crosses in the mountain pass,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mules gay with tassels, the loud din
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of muleteers, the tethered ass
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That crops the dusty wayside grass,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And cavaliers with spurs of brass
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alighting at the inn;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;White hamlets hidden in fields of wheat,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; White cities slumbering by the sea,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;White sunshine flooding square and street,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dark mountain-ranges, at whose feet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The river-beds are dry with heat,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All was a dream to me.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet something sombre and severe
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er the enchanted landscape reigned;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A terror in the atmosphere
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As if King Philip listened near,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or Torquemada, the austere,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His ghostly sway maintained.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The softer Andalusian skies
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dispelled the sadness and the gloom;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There Cadiz by the seaside lies,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Seville's orange-orchards rise,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Making the land a paradise
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of beauty and of bloom.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There Cordova is hidden among
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The palm, the olive, and the vine;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gem of the South, by poets sung,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And in whose Mosque Ahmanzor hung
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As lamps the bells that once had rung
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At Compostella's shrine.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But over all the rest supreme,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The star of stars, the cynosure,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The artist's and the poet's theme,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The young man's vision, the old man's dream,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Granada by its winding stream,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The city of the Moor!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And there the Alhambra still recalls
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aladdin's palace of delight;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Allah il Allah! through its halls
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whispers the fountain as it falls,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Darro darts beneath its walls,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The hills with snow are white.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah yes, the hills are white with snow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And cold with blasts that bite and freeze;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But in the happy vale below
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The orange and pomegranate grow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And wafts of air toss to and fro
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The blossoming almond-trees.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Vega cleft by the Xenil,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The fascination and allure
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the sweet landscape chains the will;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The traveller lingers on the hill,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His parted lips are breathing still
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The last sigh of the Moor.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How like a ruin overgrown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With flower's that hide the rents of time,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stands now the Past that I have known,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Castles in Spain, not built of stone
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But of white summer clouds, and blown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into this little mist of rhyme!
</pre>
