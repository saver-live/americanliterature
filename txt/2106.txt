<p><span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Some man unworthy to be possessor<br> Of old or new love, himself being false or weak,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Thought his pain and shame would be lesser,<br> If on womankind he might his anger wreak;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>And thence a law did grow,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>One might but one man know;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>But are other creatures so?<br> <br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Are sun, moon, or stars by law forbidden<br> To smile where they list, or lend away their light?<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Are birds divorced or are they chidden<br> If they leave their mate, or lie abroad a night?<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Beasts do no jointures lose<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Though they new lovers choose;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>But we are made worse than those.<br> <br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Who e'er rigg'd fair ships to lie in harbours,<br> And not to seek lands, or not to deal with all?<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Or built fair houses, set trees, and arbours,<br> Only to lock up, or else to let them fall?<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Good is not good, unless<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>A thousand it possess,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>But doth waste with greediness.</p>
