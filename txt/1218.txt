<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hearken, oh hearken! let your souls behind you
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Turn, gently moved!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Our voices feel along the Dread to find you,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O lost, beloved!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through the thick-shielded and strong-marshalled angels,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They press and pierce:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Our requiems follow fast on our evangels,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Voice throbs in verse.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We are but orphaned spirits left in Eden
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A time ago:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;God gave us golden cups, and we were bidden
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To feed you so.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But now our right hand hath no cup remaining,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No work to do,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The mystic hydromel is spilt, and staining
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The whole earth through.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Most ineradicable stains, for showing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Not interfused!)
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That brighter colours were the worlds foregoing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Than shall be used.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hearken, oh hearken! ye shall hearken surely
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For years and years,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The noise beside you, dripping coldly, purely,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of spirits tears.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The yearning to a beautiful denied you,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shall strain your powers.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ideal sweetnesses shall over-glide you,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Resumed from ours.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In all your music, our pathetic minor
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Your ears shall cross;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And all good gifts shall mind you of diviner,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With sense of loss.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We shall be near you in your poet-languors
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And wild extremes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What time ye vex the desert with vain angers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or mock with dreams.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And when upon you, weary after roaming,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Deaths seal is put,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the foregone ye shall discern the coming,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through eyelids shut.
</pre>
