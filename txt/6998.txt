<p><cite class="al-title">Anthem</cite> was published in the United Kingdom in 1938, but the U.S. edition was delayed until 1946, following the success of <cite class="al-title">The Fountainhead</cite>. <cite class="al-title">Anthem</cite> is Rand's <a href="/dystopian-stories">Dystopian story</a> about a new Dark Age in which technological advances are carefully planned, and individualism is suppressed. "I" has been replaced by "we." A young man known as "Equality 7-2521" is caught conducting secret science research, he and his girlfriend escape to the forest where they hatch a plan to create a new society based on individualism. Rand expanded the work into a novella; her original intention was as a screenplay.</p><p><a href="part-one">Part One</a></p><p><a href="part-two-liberty-5-3000">Part Two - Liberty 5-3000</a></p><p><a href="part-three">Part Three </a></p><p><a href="part-four">Part Four</a></p><p><a href="part-five">Part Five</a></p><p><a href="part-six">Part Six</a></p><p><a href="part-seven">Part Seven</a></p><p><a href="part-eight-it-has-been-a-day-of-wonder-this-our-first-day-in-the-forest">Part Eight - It has been a day of wonder, this, our first day in the forest</a></p><p><a href="part-nine">Part Nine</a></p><p><a href="part-ten">Part Ten</a></p><p><a href="part-eleven-i-am-i-think-i-will">Part Eleven - I am.
