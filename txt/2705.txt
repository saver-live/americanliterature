<p><cite class="al-title">The Grey Woman</cite> (1865) is a fine example of Gaskell's <a href="/gothic-literature-study-guide">Gothic</a> ghost stories, this one with a <a href="/author/charles-perrault/fairy-tale/the-story-of-blue-beard">Blue Beard</a> twist (French folktale character who murdered all his wives, except his last who outsmarted him). Gaskell's protagonist is a lady you wouldn't want to mess with named Amante. The novella is dished out in three "portions."</p><p><img alt="Elizabeth Gaskell: The Grey Woman: Anna" itemprop="image" src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/book/the-grey-woman-anna.jpg" style="float:right; margin-right: 12px; margin-bottom: 10px;" data-pagespeed-url-hash="3874079096" onload="pagespeed.CriticalImages.checkImageForCriticality(this);"></p><p><a href="portion-1">Portion 1</a></p><p><a href="portion-2">Portion 2</a></p><p><a href="portion-3">Portion 3</a></p><p style="margin-top: 26px;"> Return to the <a href="/author/elizabeth-gaskell">Elizabeth Gaskell</a> library. </p>
