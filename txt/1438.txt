<p> Published in two volumes, the first in 1605 and the second in 1615, the novel <span class="al-title">Don Quixote</span> is a cornerstone of Spanish and Western literature. It regularly appears on the short list of the most influential works ever written. </p><p> This story follows a Spanish gentleman that has read so many chivalric romances that he begins to blur the lines of fiction and reality and loses his mind. He brandishes the name <span class="al-char">Don Quixote</span> and sets of to revive chivalry, correct injustices and restore order to the world. He recruits the farmer <span class="al-char">Sancho Panza</span> as his side-kick to join him on his quest. </p><p> Trivia Points: The full title of the book is <span class="al-title">The Ingenious Gentleman Don Quixote of La Mancha</span>, in Spanish <span class="al-title">El ingenioso hidalgo don Quijote de la Mancha</span>. The phrase "Tilting at windmills", used to describe a fool-hardy task or quest was inspired by this book (by <a href="part-i-chapter-viii">Chapter VIII</a> to be specific). </p><p><img alt="Don Quixote frontis" title="Don Quixote frontis" itemprop="image" src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/book/don-quixote-1.jpg" style="max-width:500px; float:left; margin-right: 12px; margin-bottom: 10px;" data-pagespeed-url-hash="2385998110" onload="pagespeed.CriticalImages.checkImageForCriticality(this);"> </p><p><a href="translators-preface">TRANSLATOR'S PREFACE</a></p><p><a href="the-authors-preface">THE AUTHOR'S PREFACE</a></p><p><a href="dedication-of-volume-i">DEDICATION OF VOLUME I</a></p><p><a href="volume-i">Volume I.</a></p><p><a href="part-i-chapter-i">PART I -
