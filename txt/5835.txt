<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This is the Arsenal.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From floor to ceiling,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a huge organ, rise the burnished arms;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But front their silent pipes no anthem pealing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Startles the villages with strange alarms.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah! what a sound will rise, how wild and dreary,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When the death-angel touches those swift keys
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What loud lament and dismal Miserere
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Will mingle with their awful symphonies

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hear even now the infinite fierce chorus,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The cries of agony, the endless groan,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which, through the ages that have gone before us,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In long reverberations reach our own.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On helm and harness rings the Saxon hammer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through Cimbric forest roars the Norseman's song,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And loud, amid the universal clamor,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er distant deserts sounds the Tartar gong.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hear the Florentine, who from his palace
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wheels out his battle-bell with dreadful din,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Aztec priests upon their teocallis
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beat the wild war-drums made of serpent's skin;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The tumult of each sacked and burning village;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The shout that every prayer for mercy drowns;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The soldiers' revels in the midst of pillage;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The wail of famine in beleaguered towns;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The bursting shell, the gateway wrenched asunder,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The rattling musketry, the clashing blade;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And ever and anon, in tones of thunder,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The diapason of the cannonade.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is it, O man, with such discordant noises,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With such accursed instruments as these,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou drownest Nature's sweet and kindly voices,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And jarrest the celestial harmonies?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Were half the power, that fills the world with terror,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Were half the wealth, bestowed on camps and courts,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Given to redeem the human mind from error,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There were no need of arsenals or forts:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The warrior's name would be a name abhorred!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And every nation, that should lift again
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Its hand against a brother, on its forehead
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Would wear forevermore the curse of Cain!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Down the dark future, through long generations,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The echoing sounds grow fainter and then cease;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And like a bell, with solemn, sweet vibrations,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hear once more the voice of Christ say, "Peace!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Peace! and no longer from its brazen portals
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The blast of War's great organ shakes the skies!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But beautiful as songs of the immortals,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The holy melodies of love arise.

</pre>
