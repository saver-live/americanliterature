L'eternite est une pendule, dont le balancier 
dit et redit sans cesse ces deux mots seulement
dans le silence des tombeaux:
"Toujours! jamais! 
Jamais! toujours!"
                --JACQUES BRIDAINE.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Somewhat back from the village street
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stands the old-fashioned country-seat.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Across its antique portico
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tall poplar-trees their shadows throw;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And from its station in the hall
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;An ancient timepiece says to all,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Forever--never!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never--forever!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Half-way up the stairs it stands,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And points and beckons with its hands
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From its case of massive oak,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a monk, who, under his cloak,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crosses himself, and sighs, alas!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With sorrowful voice to all who pass,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Forever--never!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never--forever!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By day its voice is low and light;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But in the silent dead of night,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Distinct as a passing footstep's fall,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It echoes along the vacant hall,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Along the ceiling, along the floor,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And seems to say, at each chamber-door,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Forever--never!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never--forever!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through days of sorrow and of mirth,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through days of death and days of birth,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through every swift vicissitude
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of changeful time, unchanged it has stood,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And as if, like God, it all things saw,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It calmly repeats those words of awe,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Forever--never!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never--forever!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In that mansion used to be
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Free-hearted Hospitality;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His great fires up the chimney roared;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The stranger feasted at his board;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But, like the skeleton at the feast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That warning timepiece never ceased,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Forever--never!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never--forever!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There groups of merry children played,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There youths and maidens dreaming strayed;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O precious hours! O golden prime,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And affluence of love and time!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Even as a Miser counts his gold,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Those hours the ancient timepiece told,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Forever--never!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never--forever!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From that chamber, clothed in white,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The bride came forth on her wedding night;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There, in that silent room below,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The dead lay in his shroud of snow;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And in the hush that followed the prayer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was heard the old clock on the stair,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Forever--never!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never--forever!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All are scattered now and fled,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some are married, some are dead;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And when I ask, with throbs of pain.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Ah! when shall they all meet again?"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As in the days long since gone by,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The ancient timepiece makes reply,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Forever--never!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never--forever!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never here, forever there,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where all parting, pain, and care,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And death, and time shall disappear,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Forever there, but never here!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The horologe of Eternity
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sayeth this incessantly,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Forever--never!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never--forever!"
