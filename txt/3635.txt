&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In valleys green and still
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where lovers wander maying
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They hear from over hill
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A music playing.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Behind the drum and fife,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Past hawthornwood and hollow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through earth and out of life
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The soldiers follow.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The soldier's is the trade:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In any wind or weather
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He steals the heart of maid
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And man together.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The lover and his lass
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beneath the hawthorn lying
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Have heard the soldiers pass,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And both are sighing.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And down the distance they
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With dying note and swelling
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Walk the resounding way
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the still dwelling.
</pre>
