<pre>I.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Gothic looks solemn,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The plain Doric column
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Supports an old Bishop and Crosier;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The mouldering arch,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shaded o'er by a larch
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stands next door to Wilson the Hosier.

II.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vice that is, by turns,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er pale faces mourns
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The black tassell'd trencher and common hat;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Chantry boy sings,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Steeple-bell rings,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And as for the Chancellor dominat.

III.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There are plenty of trees,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And plenty of ease,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And plenty of fat deer for Parsons;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And when it is venison,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Short is the benison,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then each on a leg or thigh fastens.
</pre>
