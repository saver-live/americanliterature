<pre>RESIGNATION

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is no flock, however watched and tended,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But one dead lamb is there!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is no fireside, howsoe'er defended,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But has one vacant chair!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The air is full of farewells to the dying,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And mournings for the dead;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The heart of Rachel, for her children crying,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Will not be comforted!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let us be patient!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These severe afflictions
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not from the ground arise,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But oftentimes celestial benedictions
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Assume this dark disguise.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We see but dimly through the mists and vapors;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Amid these earthly damps
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What seem to us but sad, funereal tapers
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;May be heaven's distant lamps.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is no Death!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What seems so is transition;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This life of mortal breath
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is but a suburb of the life elysian,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose portal we call Death.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She is not dead,--the child of our affection,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But gone unto that school
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where she no longer needs our poor protection,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Christ himself doth rule.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In that great cloister's stillness and seclusion,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By guardian angels led,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Safe from temptation, safe from sin's pollution,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She lives, whom we call dead.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Day after day we think what she is doing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In those bright realms of air;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Year after year, her tender steps pursuing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Behold her grown more fair.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus do we walk with her, and keep unbroken
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The bond which nature gives,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thinking that our remembrance, though unspoken,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;May reach her where she lives.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not as a child shall we again behold her;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For when with raptures wild
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In our embraces we again enfold her,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She will not be a child;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But a fair maiden, in her Father's mansion,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Clothed with celestial grace;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And beautiful with all the soul's expansion
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shall we behold her face.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And though at times impetuous with emotion
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And anguish long suppressed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The swelling heart heaves moaning like the ocean,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That cannot be at rest,--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We will be patient, and assuage the feeling
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We may not wholly stay;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By silence sanctifying, not concealing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The grief that must have way.
</pre>
