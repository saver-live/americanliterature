<p><cite class="al-title">The Brothers Karmazov</cite> was Dostoevsky's last novel, published as a serial in <cite class="al-periodical">The Russian Messenger</cite> (1879-1880). It took more than two years to write this deeply philosophical work involving free will, morality, and existence of God, set in 19th century Russia. Dostoevsky died four months after its publication. Dostoevsky's novel was a major influence in <a href="/author/franz-kafka">Franz Kafka</a>'s Dystopian novel about a man accused and prosecuted by a remote authority for unknown crimes, titled <a href="/author/franz-kafka/book/the-trial/summary">The Trial</a>. <br><i>[Pictured are Dostoevsky's notes for Chapter 5.]</i></p><p><img alt="The Brothers Karamazov, Priest Maxim Massalitin, Optina Monestary, Vvedensky Temple" title="The Brothers Karamazov, Priest Maxim Massalitin, Optina Monestary, Vvedensky Temple" itemprop="image" src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/book/the-brothers-karamazov-optina-monestary.jpg" style="max-width:450px;
