<p> Life, like a marble block, is given to all,<br> A blank, inchoate mass of years and days,<br> Whence one with ardent chisel swift essays<br> Some shape of strength or symmetry to call;<br> One shatters it in bits to mend a wall;<br> One in a craftier hand the chisel lays,<br> And one, to wake the mirth in Lesbia's gaze,<br> Carves it apace in toys fantastical.<br></p>
<p>But least is he who, with enchanted eyes<br> Filled with high visions of fair shapes to be,<br> Muses which god he shall immortalize<br> In the proud Parian's perpetuity,<br> Till twilight warns him from the punctual skies<br> That the night cometh wherein none shall see.<br></p>
