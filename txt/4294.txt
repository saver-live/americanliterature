<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When by my solitary hearth I sit,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And hateful thoughts enwrap my soul in gloom;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When no fair dreams before my "mind's eye" flit,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the bare heath of life presents no bloom;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sweet Hope, ethereal balm upon me shed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And wave thy silver pinions o'er my head!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whene'er I wander, at the fall of night,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where woven boughs shut out the moon's bright ray,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Should sad Despondency my musings fright,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And frown, to drive fair Cheerfulness away,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Peep with the moonbeams through the leafy roof,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And keep that fiend Despondence far aloof!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Should Disappointment, parent of Despair,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Strive for her son to seize my careless heart;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When, like a cloud, he sits upon the air,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Preparing on his spell-bound prey to dart:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chase him away, sweet Hope, with visage bright,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And fright him as the morning frightens night!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whene'er the fate of those I hold most dear
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tells to my fearful breast a tale of sorrow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O bright-eyed Hope, my morbidfancy cheer;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let me awhile thy sweetest comforts borrow:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy heaven-born radiance around me shed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And wave thy silver pinions o'er my head!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Should e'er unhappy love my bosom pain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From cruel parents, or relentless fair;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O let me think it is not quite in vain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To sigh out sonnets to the midnight air!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sweet Hope, ethereal balm upon me shed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And wave thy silver pinions o'er my head!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the long vista of the years to roll,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let me not see our country's honour fade:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O let me see our land retain her soul,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Her pride, her freedom; and not freedom's shade.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From thy bright eyes unusual brightness shed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beneath thy pinions canopy my head!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let me not see the patriot's high bequest,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Great Liberty! how great in plain attire!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the base purple of a court oppress'd,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bowing her head, and ready to expire:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But let me see thee stoop from heaven on wings
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That fill the skies with silver glitterings!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And as, in sparkling majesty, a star
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gilds the bright summit of some gloomy cloud;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Brightening the half veil'd face of heaven afar:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So, when dark thoughts my boding spirit shroud,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sweet Hope, celestial influence round me shed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Waving thy silver pinions o'er my head!
</pre>
