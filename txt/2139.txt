<p>SEND home my long stray'd eyes to me,<br> Which, O! too long have dwelt on thee;<br> Yet since there they have learn'd such ill,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Such forced fashions,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>And false passions,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>That they be<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Made by thee<br> Fit for no good sight, keep them still.<br> <br> Send home my harmless heart again,<br> Which no unworthy thought could stain;<br> Which if it be taught by thine<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>To make jestings<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Of protestings,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>And break both<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Word and oath,<br> Keep it, for then 'tis none of mine.<br> <br> Yet send me back my heart and eyes,<br> That I may know, and see thy lies,<br> And may laugh and joy, when thou<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Art in anguish<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>And dost languish<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>For some one<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>That will none,<br> Or prove as false as thou art now.<br> <br></p>
