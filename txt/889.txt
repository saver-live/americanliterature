<p>Published under the pen name, Edith Van Dyne, L. Frank Baum wrote <cite class="al-title">The Flying Girl</cite> (1911), a bold story, considering the <a href="http://www.eyewitnesstohistory.com/wright.htm">Wright Brothers' first flight</a> was 1903, and <a href="http://www.ameliaearhart.com/about/bio.html">Amelia Earhart</a> didn't take her first flight until 1920. We think children will still find its story appealing, though perhaps a bit less sensational than for the children who read it when flying still seemed a bit like magic.</p><p>This book is featured in our collection of <a href="/books-for-young-readers">Books for Young Readers</a>.</p><p><img alt="The Flying Girl Orissa in her plane" title="The Flying Girl Orissa in her plane" itemprop="image" src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/book/the-flying-girl-4.jpg" style="max-width:400px;
