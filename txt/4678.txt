<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The careful text-books measure
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Let all who build beware!)
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The load, the shock, the pressure
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Material can bear.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So, when the buckled girder
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lets down the grinding span,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The blame of loss, or murder,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is laid upon the man.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not on the Stuff, the Man!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But, in our daily dealing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With stone and steel, we find
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Gods have no such feeling
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of justice toward mankind.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To no set gauge they make us,,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For no laid course prepare,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And presently oertake us
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With loads we cannot bear:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Too merciless to bear.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The prudent text-books give it
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In tables at the end,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The stress that shears a rivet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or makes a tie-bar bend,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What traffic wrecks macadam,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What concrete should endure,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But we, poor Sons of Adam,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Have no such literature,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To warn us or make sure!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We hold all Earth to plunder,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All Time and Space as well
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Too wonder-stale to wonder
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At each new miracle;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till, in the mid-illusion
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of Godhead neath our hand,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Falls multiple confusion
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On all we did or planned,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The mighty works we planned.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We only of Creation
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Oh, luckier bridge and rail!)
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Abide the twin-damnation,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To fail and know we fail.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet we, by which sole token
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We know we once were Gods,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Take shame in being broken
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;However great the odds
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Burden or the Odds.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Oh, veiled and secret Power
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose paths we seek in vain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be with us in our hour
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of overthrow and pain;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That we, by which sure token
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We know Thy ways are true,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In spite of being broken,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Because of being broken,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;May rise and build anew.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stand up and build anew!
