<p><cite class="al-title">The Pilgrim's Progress</cite>, subtitled, "from This World, to That Which Is to Come" was published in 1678. It is a Christian allegory, a blueprint for a young person's moral journey. The book is one of the most translated (100 languages) and most-read books after <a href="/holy-bible/home">The Bible</a> (but that was before best-selling mystery writer, <a href="/author/agatha-christie">Agatha Christie</a> sold 2 billion books). We offer the 1909 edition, subtitled, "Every Child Can Read" edited by Rev. Jesse Lyman Hurlbut, D.D. We feature the book in our collection of <a href="/books-for-young-readers">Books for Young Readers</a>, though its popularity has waned in recent generations.</p><p><img alt="The Pilgrim's Progress, cover" title="The Pilgrim's Progress, cover" itemprop="image" src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/kids-stories/the-pilgrims-progress-thumb.jpg" style="max-width:350px; float:right; margin-right: 12px; margin-bottom: 12px;" data-pagespeed-url-hash="2155485252" onload="pagespeed.CriticalImages.checkImageForCriticality(this);"> </p><p><img alt="The Pilgrim's Progress, Christian feeling guilty" title="The Pilgrim's Progress, Christian feeling guilty" itemprop="image" src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/book/pilgrims-progress-christian.jpg" style="max-width:350px; float:right; margin-right: 12px; margin-bottom: 12px;" data-pagespeed-url-hash="3571893184" onload="pagespeed.CriticalImages.checkImageForCriticality(this);"></p><p><img alt="The Pilgrim's Progress, Christian's wife" title="The Pilgrim's Progress, Christian's wife" itemprop="image" src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/book/the-pilgrims-progress-wife.jpg" style="max-width:350px; float:right; margin-right: 12px; margin-bottom: 12px;" data-pagespeed-url-hash="1241822724" onload="pagespeed.CriticalImages.checkImageForCriticality(this);"> </p><p><a href="preface">Preface</a></p><p><a href="part-i-chapter-i">Part I -
