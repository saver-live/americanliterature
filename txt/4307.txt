<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O soft embalmer of the still midnight!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shutting, with careful fingers and benign,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Our gloom-pleas'd eyes, embower'd from the light,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enshaded in forgetfulness divine;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O soothest Sleep! if so it please thee, close,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In midst of this thine hymn, my willing eyes.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or wait the Amen, ere thy poppy throws
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Around my bed its lulling charities;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then save me, or the passed day will shine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Upon my pillow, breeding many woes;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Save me from curious conscience, that still hoards
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Its strength for darkness, burrowing like a mole;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Turn the key deftly in the oiled wards,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And seal the hushed casket of my soul.
</pre>
