&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sadly as some old mediaeval knight
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gazed at the arms he could no longer wield,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sword two-handed and the shining shield
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suspended in the hall, and full in sight,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While secret longings for the lost delight
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of tourney or adventure in the field
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Came over him, and tears but half concealed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Trembled and fell upon his beard of white,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So I behold these books upon their shelf,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My ornaments and arms of other days;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not wholly useless, though no longer used,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For they remind me of my other self,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Younger and stronger, and the pleasant ways
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In which I walked, now clouded and confused.
