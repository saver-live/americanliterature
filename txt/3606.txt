&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'Tis spring; come out to ramble
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The hilly brakes around,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For under thorn and bramble
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;About the hollow ground
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The primroses are found.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And there's the windflower chilly
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With all the winds at play,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And there's the Lenten lily
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That has not long to stay
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And dies on Easter day.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And since till girls go maying
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You find the primrose still,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And find the windflower playing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With every wind at will,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But not the daffodil,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bring baskets now, and sally
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Upon the spring's array,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And bear from hill and valley
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The daffodil away
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That dies on Easter day.
</pre>
