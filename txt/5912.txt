<pre>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The tide rises, the tide falls,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The twilight darkens, the curlew calls;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Along the sea-sands damp and brown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The traveller hastens toward the town,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; And the tide rises, the tide falls.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Darkness settles on roofs and walls,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the sea in the darkness calls and calls;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The little waves, with their soft, white hands,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Efface the footprints in the sands,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the tide rises, the tide falls.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The morning breaks; the steeds in their stalls
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stamp and neigh, as the hostler calls;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The day returns, but nevermore
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Returns the traveller to the shore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; And the tide rises, the tide falls.
</pre>
