<p><cite class="al-title">The Dreams in the Witch House</cite> is described as <i>"a story of mathematics, witchcraft and Walpurgis Night, in which the horror creeps and grows."</i> It was originally published in <cite class="al-periodical">Weird Tales</cite> in July, 1933 as part of the Cthulhu Mythos cycle. It may have been inspired by <a href="/author/nathaniel-hawthorne">Nathaniel Hawthorne</a>'s unfinished novel, <a href="/author/nathaniel-hawthorne/book/septimius-felton/summary">Septimius Felton</a>.</p><p><img alt="John Anster Fitzgerald, Fairies by Moonlight, Brownies and Banshees, 1875" title="John Anster Fitzgerald, Fairies by Moonlight, Brownies and Banshees, 1875" itemprop="image" src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/poem/haunted-houses.jpg" style="max-width:350px; float:right; margin-left: 12px; margin-bottom: 12px;" data-pagespeed-url-hash="3115748924" onload="pagespeed.CriticalImages.checkImageForCriticality(this);"> </p><p><a href="the-dreams-in-the-witch-house">The Dreams in the Witch House</a></p><p style="margin-top: 26px;"> Return to the <a href="/author/h-p-lovecraft">H.
