<p><cite class="al-title">The Jungle</cite> (1906) originally appeared in serial form as <cite class="al-title">The Appeal to Reason</cite> in a socialist newspaper. It was so well received, he republished the work as a novel that same year. Chronicling the plight of Jurgis and Ona, Lithuanian immigrants in Chicago, the story documents the plight of the workers in the meatpacking yards of Chicago. <cite class="al-title">The Jungle</cite> had a deep and immediate political impact on the country, sending shock waves throughout the United States and causing cries for labor and agricultural reform.
