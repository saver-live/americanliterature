<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The ceaseless rain is falling fast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And yonder gilded vane,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Immovable for three days past,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Points to the misty main,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It drives me in upon myself
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And to the fireside gleams,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To pleasant books that crowd my shelf,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And still more pleasant dreams,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I read whatever bards have sung
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of lands beyond the sea,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the bright days when I was young
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come thronging back to me.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In fancy I can hear again
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Alpine torrent's roar,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The mule-bells on the hills of Spain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sea at Elsinore.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I see the convent's gleaming wall
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rise from its groves of pine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And towers of old cathedrals tall,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And castles by the Rhine.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I journey on by park and spire,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beneath centennial trees,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through fields with poppies all on fire,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And gleams of distant seas.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I fear no more the dust and heat,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No more I feel fatigue,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While journeying with another's feet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er many a lengthening league.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let others traverse sea and land,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And toil through various climes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I turn the world round with my hand
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Reading these poets' rhymes.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From them I learn whatever lies
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beneath each changing zone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And see, when looking with their eyes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Better than with mine own.
</pre>
