<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leafless are the trees; their purple branches
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Spread themselves abroad, like reefs of coral,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rising silent
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the Red Sea of the Winter sunset.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the hundred chimneys of the village,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the Afreet in the Arabian story,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Smoky columns
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tower aloft into the air of amber.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At the window winks the flickering fire-light;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here and there the lamps of evening glimmer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Social watch-fires
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Answering one another through the darkness.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the hearth the lighted logs are glowing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And like Ariel in the cloven pine-tree
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For its freedom
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Groans and sighs the air imprisoned in them.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the fireside there are old men seated,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seeing ruined cities in the ashes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Asking sadly
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the Past what it can ne'er restore them.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the fireside there are youthful dreamers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Building castles fair, with stately stairways,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Asking blindly
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the Future what it cannot give them.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the fireside tragedies are acted
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In whose scenes appear two actors only,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wife and husband,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And above them God the sole spectator.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the fireside there are peace and comfort,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wives and children, with fair, thoughtful faces,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Waiting, watching
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For a well-known footstep in the passage.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Each man's chimney is his Golden Mile-stone;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is the central point, from which he measures
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Every distance
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through the gateways of the world around him.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In his farthest wanderings still he sees it;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hears the talking flame, the answering night-wind,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As he heard them
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When he sat with those who were, but are not.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Happy he whom neither wealth nor fashion,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor the march of the encroaching city,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drives an exile
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the hearth of his ancestral homestead.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We may build more splendid habitations,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fill our rooms with paintings and with sculptures,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But we cannot
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Buy with gold the old associations!
</pre>
