<pre>I

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love me Sweet, with all thou art,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Feeling, thinking, seeing;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love me in the lightest part,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love me in full being.

II

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love me with thine open youth
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In its frank surrender;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the vowing of thy mouth,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With its silence tender.

III

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love me with thine azure eyes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Made for earnest grantings;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Taking colour from the skies,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Can Heaven's truth be wanting?

IV

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love me with their lids, that fall
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Snow-like at first meeting;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love me with thine heart, that all
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Neighbours then see beating.

V

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love me with thine hand stretched out
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Freely, open-minded:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love me with thy loitering foot,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hearing one behind it.

VI

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love me with thy voice, that turns
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sudden faint above me;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love me with thy blush that burns
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When I murmur 'Love me!'

VII

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love me with thy thinking soul,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Break it to love-sighing;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love me with thy thoughts that roll
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On through living, dying.

VIII

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love me in thy gorgeous airs,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When the world has crowned thee;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love me, kneeling at thy prayers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the angels round thee.

IX

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love me pure, as muses do,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Up the woodlands shady:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love me gaily, fast and true,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As a winsome lady.

X

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through all hopes that keep us brave,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Farther off or nigher,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love me for the house and grave,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And for something higher.

XI

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus, if thou wilt prove me, Dear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Woman's love no fable,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will love thee, half a year
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As a man is able.
</pre>
