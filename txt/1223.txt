<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I tell you, hopeless grief is passionless;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That only men incredulous of despair,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Half-taught in anguish, through the midnight air
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beat upward to God's throne in loud access
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of shrieking and reproach. Full desertness,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In souls as countries, lieth silent-bare
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Under the blanching, vertical eye-glare
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the absolute Heavens. Deep-hearted man, express
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Grief for thy Dead in silence like to death
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Most like a monumental statue set
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In everlasting watch and moveless woe
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till itself crumble to the dust beneath.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Touch it; the marble eyelids are not wet:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If it could weep, it could arise and go.
</pre>
