<p><cite class="al-title">The Land That Time Forgot</cite> was the first of a serial published in <cite class="al-title">Blue Book Magazine</cite> in 1918; it was published as a novel in 1924. The second in the serial was Burroughs' <a href="/author/edgar-rice-burroughs/book/the-people-that-time-forgot/summary">The People That Time Forgot</a>. Inspired by other "lost world" stories, <a href="/author/jules-verne">Jules Verne</a>'s <a href="/author/jules-verne/book/the-mysterious-island/summary">The Mysterious Island</a> (1874), and <a href="/author/sir-arthur-conan-doyle">Sir Arthur Conan Doyle</a>'s <a href="/author/sir-arthur-conan-doyle/book/the-lost-world/summary">The Lost World</a> (1912), these novels remain cult favorites of dinosaur and B-movie fans, featured in <a href="/dystopian-stories">Dystopian Stories</a>, <a href="/science-fiction-study-guide">Science Fiction</a>, and <a href="/books-for-young-readers">Books for Young Readers</a>.</p><p><img alt="The Land That Time Forgot, p. 1138" title="The Land That Time Forgot, p. 1138" itemprop="image" src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/book/the-land-that-time-forgot-p1138.jpg" style="max-width:350px; float:right; margin-right: 12px; margin-bottom: 12px;" data-pagespeed-url-hash="2623840762" onload="pagespeed.CriticalImages.checkImageForCriticality(this);"> </p><p><a href="chapter-i">Chapter I</a></p><p><a href="chapter-ii">Chapter II</a></p><p><a href="chapter-iii">Chapter III</a></p><p><a href="chapter-iv">Chapter IV</a></p><p><a href="chapter-v">Chapter V</a></p><p><a href="chapter-vi">Chapter VI</a></p><p><a href="chapter-vii">Chapter VII</a></p><p><a href="chapter-viii">Chapter VIII</a></p><p><a href="chapter-ix">Chapter IX</a></p><p><a href="chapter-x">Chapter X</a></p><p style="margin-top: 26px;"> Return to the <a href="/author/edgar-rice-burroughs">Edgar Rice Burroughs</a> library. </p>
