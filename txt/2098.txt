<p>THOU art not so black as my heart,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Nor half so brittle as her heart, thou art;<br> What would'st thou say? shall both our properties by thee be spoke,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>—Nothing more endless, nothing sooner broke?<br> <br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:3em;">&nbsp;</span>Marriage rings are not of this stuff;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Oh, why should ought less precious, or less tough<br> Figure our loves? except in thy name thou have bid it say,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>"—I'm cheap, and nought but fashion; fling me away."<br> <br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:3em;">&nbsp;</span>Yet stay with me since thou art come,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Circle this finger's top, which didst her thumb;<br> Be justly proud, and gladly safe, that thou dost dwell with me;<br> She that, O! broke her faith, would soon break thee.</p>
