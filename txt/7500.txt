<p>The formative anti-slavery novel published in 1852, <span class="al-title">Uncle Tom's Cabin; or, Life Among the Lowly</span> had a profound impact on Americans' public opinion and is widely credited with fueling the abolitionist movement. Its publication materially contributed to the tensions leading up to the American Civil War.</p><p> Featured in our collection of <a href="/twenty-five-great-american-novels">25 Great American Novels</a>. Readers interested in <span class="al-title">Uncle Tom's Cabin</span> might also appreciate Solomon Northup's <a href="/author/solomon-northup/book/twelve-years-a-slave/summary">Twelve Years a Slave</a>, an 1853 memoir that is the basis of Steve McQueen's 2013 blockbuster movie "12 Years a Slave." Taken together, <span class="al-title">Uncle Tom's Cabin</span> and <span class="al-title">Twelve Years a Slave</span> altered the course of United States history. Stowe's book was adapted for children, <cite class="al-title"><a href="/author/harriet-beecher-stowe/short-story/uncle-toms-cabin-young-folks-edition">Uncle Tom's Cabin, Young Folks' Edition</a></cite>, published in 1913.</p><p>Find out more at <a href="/history">American History</a>, the <a href="/african-american-library">African American Library</a>, and our collection of <a
