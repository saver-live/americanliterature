<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<i>INSCRIBED TO MY FRIEND E. C.</i>


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If God compel thee to this destiny,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To die alone, with none beside thy bed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To ruffle round with sobs thy last word said
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And mark with tears the pulses ebb from thee, 
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray then alone,' O Christ, come tenderly!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By thy forsaken Sonship in the red
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drear wine-press, by the wilderness out-spread, 
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the lone garden where thine agony
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fell bloody from thy brow, by all of those
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Permitted desolations, comfort mine!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No earthly friend being near me, interpose
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No deathly angel 'twixt my face aud thine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But stoop Thyself to gather my life's rose,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And smile away my mortal to Divine!'
</pre>
