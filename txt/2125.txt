<p>He is stark mad, whoever says,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>That he hath been in love an hour,<br> Yet not that love so soon decays,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>But that it can ten in less space devour;<br> Who will believe me, if I swear<br> That I have had the plague a year?<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Who would not laugh at me, if I should say<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>I saw a flash of powder burn a day?<br> <br> Ah, what a trifle is a heart,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>If once into love's hands it come!<br> All other griefs allow a part<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>To other griefs, and ask themselves but some;<br> They come to us, but us love draws;<br> He swallows us and never chaws;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>By him, as by chain'd shot, whole ranks do die;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>He is the tyrant pike, our hearts the fry.<br> <br> If 'twere not so, what did become<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Of my heart when I first saw thee?<br> I brought a heart into the room,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>But from the room I carried none with me.<br> If it had gone to thee, I know<br> Mine would have taught thine heart to show<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>More pity unto me; but Love, alas!<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>At one first blow did shiver it as glass.<br> <br> Yet nothing can to nothing fall,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Nor any place be empty quite;<br> Therefore I think my breast hath all<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Those pieces still, though they be not unite;<br> And now, as broken glasses show<br> A hundred lesser faces, so<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>My rags of heart can like, wish, and adore,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>But after one such love, can love no more.</p>
