<p>The most popular of Cooper's <i>Leatherstocking Tales</i>, <cite class="al-title">The Last of the Mohicans</cite> takes place in 1757 during the French and Indian War and recounts the story of an unarmed massacre, the kidnapping of two sisters, and their rescue by Hawk-eye and his two Mohican friends Uncas and Chingachook. The novel was quite popular when published (1826) and is still a staple in most American Literature courses, and a great example of <a href="/romanticism-study-guide">Romanticism</a>. </p><p>Featured in our collection of <a href="/twenty-five-great-american-novels">25 Great American Novels</a>.
