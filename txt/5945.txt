&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ACT I.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SCENE I.--The COUNT OF LARA'S chambers.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Night.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The COUNT in his dressing-gown, smoking and conversing with DON CARLOS.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You were not at the play tonight, Don Carlos;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How happened it?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I had engagements elsewhere.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray who was there?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why all the town and court.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The house was crowded; and the busy fans
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Among the gayly dressed and perfumed ladies
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fluttered like butterflies among the flowers.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There was the Countess of Medina Celi;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Goblin Lady with her Phantom Lover,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Her Lindo Don Diego; Dona Sol,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Dona Serafina, and her cousins.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What was the play?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was a dull affair;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One of those comedies in which you see,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As Lope says, the history of the world
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Brought down from Genesis to the Day of Judgment.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There were three duels fought in the first act,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Three gentlemen receiving deadly wounds,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Laying their hands upon their hearts, and saying,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"O, I am dead!" a lover in a closet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;An old hidalgo, and a gay Don Juan,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A Dona Inez with a black mantilla,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Followed at twilight by an unknown lover,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who looks intently where he knows she is not!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of course, the Preciosa danced to-night?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And never better.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Every footstep fell
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As lightly as a sunbeam on the water.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I think the girl extremely beautiful.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Almost beyond the privilege of woman!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I saw her in the Prado yesterday.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Her step was royal,--queen-like,--and her face
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As beautiful as a saint's in Paradise.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;May not a saint fall from her Paradise,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And be no more a saint?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why do you ask?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Because I have heard it said this angel fell,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And though she is a virgin outwardly,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Within she is a sinner; like those panels
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of doors and altar-pieces the old monks
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Painted in convents, with the Virgin Mary
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the outside, and on the inside Venus!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You do her wrong; indeed, you do her wrong!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She is as virtuous as she is fair.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How credulous you are!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why look you, friend,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There's not a virtuous woman in Madrid,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In this whole city!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And would you persuade me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That a mere dancing-girl, who shows herself,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nightly, half naked, on the stage, for money,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And with voluptuous motions fires the blood
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of inconsiderate youth, is to be held
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A model for her virtue?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You forget
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She is a Gypsy girl.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And therefore won
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The easier.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nay, not to be won at all!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The only virtue that a Gypsy prizes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is chastity.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That is her only virtue.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dearer than life she holds it.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I remember
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A Gypsy woman, a vile, shameless bawd,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose craft was to betray the young and fair;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And yet this woman was above all bribes.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And when a noble lord, touched by her beauty,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The wild and wizard beauty of her race,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Offered her gold to be what she made others,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She turned upon him, with a look of scorn,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And smote him in the face!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And does that prove
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That Preciosa is above suspicion?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It proves a nobleman may be repulsed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When he thinks conquest easy.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I believe
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That woman, in her deepest degradation,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Holds something sacred, something undefiled,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some pledge and keepsake of her higher nature,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, like the diamond in the dark, retains
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some quenchless gleam of the celestial light!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet Preciosa would have taken the gold.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(rising).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I do not think so.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am sure of it.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But why this haste?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stay yet a little longer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And fight the battles of your Dulcinea.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T is late.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I must begone, for if I stay
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You will not be persuaded.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes; persuade me.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No one so deaf as he who will not hear!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No one so blind as he who will not see!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And so good night.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I wish you pleasant dreams,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And greater faith in woman.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;[Exit.]

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Greater faith!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have the greatest faith; for I believe
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Victorian is her lover.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I believe
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That I shall be to-morrow; and thereafter
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Another, and another, and another,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chasing each other through her zodiac,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As Taurus chases Aries.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Enter FRANCISCO with a casket.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Well, Francisco,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What speed with Preciosa?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fran.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;None, my lord.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She sends your jewels back, and bids me tell you
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She is not to be purchased by your gold.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then I will try some other way to win her.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray, dost thou know Victorian?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fran.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes, my lord;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I saw him at the jeweller's to-day.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What was he doing there?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fran.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I saw him buy
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A golden ring, that had a ruby in it.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was there another like it?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fran.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One so like it
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I could not choose between them.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is well.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To-morrow morning bring that ring to me.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Do not forget. Now light me to my bed.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;[Exeunt.]


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SCENE II. -- A street in Madrid.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enter CHISPA, followed by musicians, with a bagpipe, guitars, and other instruments.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Abernuncio Satanas! and a plague on all lovers who ramble&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;about at night, drinking the elements, instead of sleeping quietly in&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;their beds.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Every dead man to his cemetery, say I; and every friar to his monastery.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now, here's my master, Victorian, yesterday a cow-keeper, and to-day a gentleman; yesterday a student, and to-day a lover; and I must be up later than the nightingale, for as the abbot sings so must the sacristan respond.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;God grant he may soon be&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;married, for then shall all this serenading cease.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay, marry! marry!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;marry! Mother, what does marry mean?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It means to spin, to bear children, and to weep, my daughter!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, of a truth, there is something more in matrimony than the wedding-ring.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(To the musicians.)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And now, gentlemen, Pax vobiscum! as the ass said to the cabbages.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray, walk this way; and don't hang down your heads. It is no disgrace to have an old father and a ragged shirt.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now, look you,&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;you are gentlemen who lead the life of crickets; you enjoy hunger by&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;day and noise by night.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet, I beseech you, for this once be not loud, but pathetic; for it is a serenade to a damsel in bed, and not to the Man in the Moon. Your object is not to arouse and terrify, but to soothe and bring lulling dreams.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Therefore, each shall not play upon his instrument as if it were the only one in the universe, but gently, and with a certain modesty, according with the others.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray, how may I call thy name, friend?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;First Mus.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Geronimo Gil, at your service.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Every tub smells of the wine that is in it.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray, Geronimo, is not Saturday an unpleasant day with thee?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;First Mus.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why so?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Because I have heard it said that Saturday is an unpleasant day with those who have but one shirt.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Moreover, I have seen thee at the tavern, and if thou canst run as fast as thou canst&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;drink, I should like to hunt hares with thee.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What instrument is that?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;First Mus.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;An Aragonese bagpipe.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray, art thou related to the bagpiper of Bujalance, who&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;asked a maravedi for playing, and ten for leaving off?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;First Mus.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No, your honor.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am glad of it.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What other instruments have we?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Second and Third Musicians.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We play the bandurria.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A pleasing instrument. And thou?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fourth Mus.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The fife.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I like it; it has a cheerful, soul-stirring sound, that soars up to my lady's window like the song of a swallow.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; And you others?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Other Mus.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We are the singers, please your honor.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You are too many.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Do you think we are going to sing mass in the cathedral of Cordova?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Four men can make but little use of one shoe, and I see not how you can all sing in one song. But follow me along the garden wall.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That is the way my master climbs to the lady's window, it is by the Vicar's skirts that the Devil climbs into the belfry.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come, follow me, and make no noise.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; [Exeunt.]


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SCENE III. -- PRECIOSA'S chamber. She stands at the open window.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How slowly through the lilac-scented air
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Descends the tranquil moon!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like thistle-down
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The vapory clouds float in the peaceful sky;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And sweetly from yon hollow vaults of shade
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The nightingales breathe out their souls in song.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And hark! what songs of love, what soul-like sounds,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Answer them from below!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SERENADE.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stars of the summer night!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Far in yon azure deeps,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hide, hide your golden light!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She sleeps!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My lady sleeps!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sleeps!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Moon of the summer night!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Far down yon western steeps,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sink, sink in silver light!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She sleeps!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My lady sleeps!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sleeps!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wind of the summer night!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where yonder woodbine creeps,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fold, fold thy pinions light!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She sleeps!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My lady sleeps!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sleeps!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dreams of the summer night!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tell her, her lover keeps
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Watch! while in slumbers light
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She sleeps
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My lady sleeps
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sleeps!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Enter VICTORIAN by the balcony.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Poor little dove!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou tremblest like a leaf!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am so frightened!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T is for thee I tremble!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hate to have thee climb that wall by night!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Did no one see thee?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;None, my love, but thou.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Prec. 'T is very dangerous; and when thou art gone
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I chide myself for letting thee come here
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus stealthily by night.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where hast thou been?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Since yesterday I have no news from thee.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Since yesterday I have been in Alcala.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Erelong the time will come, sweet Preciosa,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When that dull distance shall no more divide us;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I no more shall scale thy wall by night
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To steal a kiss from thee, as I do now.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;An honest thief, to steal but what thou givest.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And we shall sit together unmolested,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And words of true love pass from tongue to tongue,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As singing birds from one bough to another.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That were a life to make time envious!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I knew that thou wouldst come to me to-night.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I saw thee at the play.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sweet child of air!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never did I behold thee so attired
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And garmented in beauty as to-night!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What hast thou done to make thee look so fair?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Am I not always fair?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay, and so fair
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That I am jealous of all eyes that see thee,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And wish that they were blind.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I heed them not;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When thou art present, I see none but thee!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There's nothing fair nor beautiful, but takes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Something from thee, that makes it beautiful.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And yet thou leavest me for those dusty books.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou comest between me and those books too often!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I see thy face in everything I see!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The paintings in the chapel wear thy looks,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The canticles are changed to sarabands,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And with the leaned doctors of the schools
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I see thee dance cachuchas.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In good sooth,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I dance with learned doctors of the schools
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To-morrow morning.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And with whom, I pray?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A grave and reverend Cardinal, and his Grace
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Archbishop of Toledo.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What mad jest
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is this?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is no jest; indeed it is not.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prithee, explain thyself.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why, simply thus.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou knowest the Pope has sent here into Spain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To put a stop to dances on the stage.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have heard it whispered.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now the Cardinal,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who for this purpose comes, would fain behold
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With his own eyes these dances; and the Archbishop
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Has sent for me--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That thou mayst dance before them!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now viva la cachucha!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It will breathe
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The fire of youth into these gray old men!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T will be thy proudest conquest!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Saving one.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And yet I fear these dances will be stopped,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Preciosa be once more a beggar.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sweetest beggar that e'er asked for alms;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With such beseeching eyes, that when I saw thee
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I gave my heart away!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dost thou remember
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When first we met?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was at Cordova,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the cathedral garden. Thou wast sitting
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Under the orange-trees, beside a fountain.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec. 'T was Easter-Sunday.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The full-blossomed trees
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Filled all the air with fragrance and with joy.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The priests were singing, and the organ sounded,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And then anon the great cathedral bell.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was the elevation of the Host.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We both of us fell down upon our knees,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Under the orange boughs, and prayed together.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I never had been happy till that moment.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou blessed angel!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And when thou wast gone
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I felt an acting here.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I did not speak
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To any one that day.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But from that day
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bartolome grew hateful unto me.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Remember him no more.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let not his shadow
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come between thee and me.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sweet Preciosa!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I loved thee even then, though I was silent!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I thought I ne'er should see thy face again.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy farewell had a sound of sorrow in it.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That was the first sound in the song of love!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Scarce more than silence is, and yet a sound.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hands of invisible spirits touch the strings
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of that mysterious instrument, the soul,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And play the prelude of our fate.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We hear
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The voice prophetic, and are not alone.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That is my faith.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dust thou believe these warnings?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So far as this.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Our feelings and our thoughts
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tend ever on, and rest not in the Present.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As drops of rain fall into some dark well,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And from below comes a scarce audible sound,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So fall our thoughts into the dark Hereafter,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And their mysterious echo reaches us.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have felt it so, but found no words to say it!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I cannot reason; I can only feel!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But thou hast language for all thoughts and feelings.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou art a scholar; and sometimes I think
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We cannot walk together in this world!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The distance that divides us is too great!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Henceforth thy pathway lies among the stars;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I must not hold thee back.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou little sceptic!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dost thou still doubt?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What I most prize in woman
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is her affections, not her intellect!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The intellect is finite; but the affections
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are infinite, and cannot be exhausted.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Compare me with the great men of the earth;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What am I?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why, a pygmy among giants!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But if thou lovest,--mark me! I say lovest,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The greatest of thy sex excels thee not!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The world of the affections is thy world,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not that of man's ambition.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In that stillness
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which most becomes a woman, calm and holy,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou sittest by the fireside of the heart,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Feeding its flame.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The element of fire
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is pure.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It cannot change nor hide its nature,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But burns as brightly in a Gypsy camp
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As in a palace hall.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Art thou convinced?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes, that I love thee, as the good love heaven;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But not that I am worthy of that heaven.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How shall I more deserve it?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Loving more.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I cannot love thee more; my heart is full.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then let it overflow, and I will drink it,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As in the summer-time the thirsty sands
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drink the swift waters of the Manzanares,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And still do thirst for more.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; A&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Watchman (in the street).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ave Maria
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Purissima!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T is midnight and serene!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hear'st thou that cry?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is a hateful sound,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To scare thee from me!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the hunter's horn
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Doth scare the timid stag, or bark of hounds
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The moor-fowl from his mate.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray, do not go!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I must away to Alcala to-night.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Think of me when I am away.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fear not!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have no thoughts that do not think of thee.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(giving her a ring).
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And to remind thee of my love, take this;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A serpent, emblem of Eternity;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A ruby,--say, a drop of my heart's blood.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is an ancient saying, that the ruby
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Brings gladness to the wearer, and preserves
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The heart pure, and, if laid beneath the pillow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drives away evil dreams.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But then, alas!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was a serpent tempted Eve to sin.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What convent of barefooted Carmelites
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Taught thee so much theology?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec. (laying her hand upon his mouth).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hush! hush!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Good night! and may all holy angels guard thee!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Good night! good night!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou art my guardian angel!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have no other saint than thou to pray to!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(He descends by the balcony.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Take care, and do not hurt thee.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Art thou safe?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(from the garden).
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Safe as my love for thee!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But art thou safe?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Others can climb a balcony by moonlight
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As well as I.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray shut thy window close;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am jealous of the perfumed air of night
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That from this garden climbs to kiss thy lips.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(throwing down her handkerchief).
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou silly child!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Take this to blind thine eyes.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is my benison!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And brings to me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sweet fragrance from thy lips, as the soft wind
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wafts to the out-bound mariner the breath
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the beloved land he leaves behind.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Make not thy voyage long.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To-morrow night
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shall see me safe returned.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou art the star
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To guide me to an anchorage.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Good night!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My beauteous star!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My star of love, good night!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec. Good night!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Watchman (at a distance).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ave Maria Purissima!



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Scene IV. -- An inn on the road to Alcala.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;BALTASAR asleep on a bench.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enter CHISPA.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And here we are, halfway to Alcala, between cocks and midnight.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Body o' me! what an inn this is!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The lights out, and the&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;landlord asleep.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hola! ancient Baltasar!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bal. (waking).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here I am.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes, there you are, like a one-eyed Alcalde in a town without inhabitants.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bring a light, and let me have supper.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bal.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where is your master?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispo.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Do not trouble yourself about him.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We have stopped a moment to breathe our horses; and, if he chooses to walk up and down in the open air, looking into the sky as one who hears it rain, that does not satisfy my hunger, you know.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But be quick, for I am in a hurry, and every man stretches his legs according to the length of his coverlet.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What have we here?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bal. (setting a light on the table).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stewed rabbit.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa (eating).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conscience of Portalegre! Stewed kitten, you mean!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bal.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And a pitcher of Pedro Ximenes, with a roasted pear in it.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa (drinking).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ancient Baltasar, amigo!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You know how to cry wine and sell vinegar.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I tell you this is nothing but Vino Tinto of La Mancha, with a tang of the swine-skin.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bal.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I swear to you by Saint Simon and Judas, it is all as I say.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I swear to you by Saint Peter and Saint Paul, that it is no such thing.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Moreover, your supper is like the hidalgo's dinner, very little meat and a great deal of tablecloth.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bal.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ha! ha! ha!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa. And more noise than nuts.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bal.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ha! ha! ha!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You must have your joke, Master Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But shall I not ask Don Victorian in, to take a draught of the Pedro Ximenes?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No; you might as well say, "Don't-you-want-some?" to a dead man.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bal.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why does he go so often to Madrid?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For the same reason that he eats no supper.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He is in love.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Were you ever in love, Baltasar?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bal.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I was never out of it, good Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It has been the torment of my life.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What! are you on fire, too, old hay-stack?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why, we shall never be able to put you out.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict. (without).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Go to bed, Pero Grullo, for the cocks are crowing.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ea! Chispa! Chispa!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ea! Senor.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come with me, ancient Baltasar, and bring water for the horses.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will pay for the supper tomorrow.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; [Exeunt.]



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SCENE V. -- VICTORIAN'S chambers at&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alcala.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;HYPOLITO asleep in an arm-chair.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He awakes slowly.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I must have been asleep! ay, sound asleep!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And it was all a dream.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O sleep, sweet sleep
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whatever form thou takest, thou art fair,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Holding unto our lips thy goblet filled
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Out of Oblivion's well, a healing draught!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The candles have burned low; it must be late.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where can Victorian be?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like Fray Carrillo,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The only place in which one cannot find him
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is his own cell.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here's his guitar, that seldom
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Feels the caresses of its master's hand.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Open thy silent lips, sweet instrument!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And make dull midnight merry with a song.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (He plays and sings.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre Francisco!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre Francisco!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What do you want of Padre Francisco?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here is a pretty young maiden
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who wants to confess her sins!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Open the door and let her come in,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will shrive her from every sin.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Enter VICTORIAN.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre Hypolito!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre Hypolito!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What do you want of Padre Hypolito?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come, shrive me straight; for, if love be a sin,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am the greatest sinner that doth live.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will confess the sweetest of all crimes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A maiden wooed and won.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The same old tale
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the old woman in the chimney-corner,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who, while the pot boils, says, "Come here, my child;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I'll tell thee a story of my wedding-day."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nay, listen, for my heart is full; so full
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That I must speak.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alas! that heart of thine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is like a scene in the old play; the curtain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rises to solemn music, and lo! enter
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The eleven thousand virgins of Cologne!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nay, like the Sibyl's volumes, thou shouldst say;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Those that remained, after the six were burned,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Being held more precious than the nine together.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But listen to my tale.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dost thou remember
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Gypsy girl we saw at Cordova
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dance the Romalis in the market-place?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou meanest Preciosa.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay, the same.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou knowest how her image haunted me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Long after we returned to Alcala.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She's in Madrid.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I know it.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I'm in love.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And therefore in Madrid when thou shouldst be
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In Alcala.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O pardon me, my friend,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If I so long have kept this secret from thee;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But silence is the charm that guards such treasures,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, if a word be spoken ere the time,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They sink again, they were not meant for us.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alas! alas!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I see thou art in love.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love keeps the cold out better than a cloak.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It serves for food and raiment.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Give a Spaniard
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His mass, his olla, and his Dona Luisa--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou knowest the proverb.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But pray tell me, lover,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How speeds thy wooing?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is the maiden coy?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Write her a song, beginning with an Ave;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sing as the monk sang to the Virgin Mary,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Ave! cujus calcem clare
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Nec centenni commendare
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sciret Seraph studio!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray, do not jest!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This is no time for it!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am in earnest!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seriously enamored?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What, ho!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Primus of great Alcala
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enamored of a Gypsy?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tell me frankly,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How meanest thou?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I mean it honestly.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Surely thou wilt not marry her!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why not?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She was betrothed to one Bartolome,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If I remember rightly, a young Gypsy
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who danced with her at Cordova.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They quarrelled,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And so the matter ended.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But in truth
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou wilt not marry her.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In truth I will.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The angels sang in heaven when she was born!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She is a precious jewel I have found
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Among the filth and rubbish of the world.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I'll stoop for it; but when I wear it here,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Set on my forehead like the morning star,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The world may wonder, but it will not laugh.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If thou wear'st nothing else upon thy forehead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T will be indeed a wonder.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Out upon thee
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With thy unseasonable jests!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray tell me,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is there no virtue in the world?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not much.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What, think'st thou, is she doing at this moment;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now, while we speak of her?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She lies asleep,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And from her parted lips her gentle breath
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Comes like the fragrance from the lips of flowers.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Her tender limbs are still, and on her breast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The cross she prayed to, ere she fell asleep,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rises and falls with the soft tide of dreams,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a light barge safe moored.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which means, in prose,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She's sleeping with her mouth a little open!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, would I had the old magician's glass
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To see her as she lies in childlike sleep!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And wouldst thou venture?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay, indeed I would!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou art courageous.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hast thou e'er reflected
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How much lies hidden in that one word, NOW?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes; all the awful mystery of Life!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I oft have thought, my dear Hypolito,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That could we, by some spell of magic, change
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The world and its inhabitants to stone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the same attitudes they now are in,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What fearful glances downward might we cast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into the hollow chasms of human life!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What groups should we behold about the death-bed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Putting to shame the group of Niobe!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What joyful welcomes, and what sad farewells!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What stony tears in those congealed eyes!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What visible joy or anguish in those cheeks!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What bridal pomps, and what funereal shows!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What foes, like gladiators, fierce and struggling!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What lovers with their marble lips together!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay, there it is! and, if I were in love,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That is the very point I most should dread.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This magic glass, these magic spells of thine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Might tell a tale were better left untold.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For instance, they might show us thy fair cousin,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Lady Violante, bathed in tears
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of love and anger, like the maid of Colchis,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whom thou, another faithless Argonaut,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Having won that golden fleece, a woman's love,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Desertest for this Glauce.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hold thy peace!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She cares not for me.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She may wed another,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or go into a convent, and, thus dying,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Marry Achilles in the Elysian Fields.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Hyp. (rising).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And so, good night!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Good morning, I should say.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Clock strikes three.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hark! how the loud and ponderous mace of Time
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Knocks at the golden portals of the day!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And so, once more, good night!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We'll speak more largely
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of Preciosa when we meet again.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Get thee to bed, and the magician, Sleep,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shall show her to thee, in his magic glass,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In all her loveliness.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Good night!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;[Exit.]

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Good night!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But not to bed; for I must read awhile.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Throws himself into the arm-chair which HYPOLITO has left, and lays a large book open upon his knees.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Must read, or sit in revery and watch
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The changing color of the waves that break
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Upon the idle sea-shore of the mind!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Visions of Fame! that once did visit me,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Making night glorious with your smile, where are ye?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, who shall give me, now that ye are gone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Juices of those immortal plants that bloom
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Upon Olympus, making us immortal?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or teach me where that wondrous mandrake grows
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose magic root, torn from the earth with groans,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At midnight hour, can scare the fiends away,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And make the mind prolific in its fancies!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have the wish, but want the will, to act!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Souls of great men departed!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ye whose words
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Have come to light from the swift river of Time,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like Roman swords found in the Tagus' bed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where is the strength to wield the arms ye bore?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the barred visor of Antiquity
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Reflected shines the eternal light of Truth,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As from a mirror!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All the means of action--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The shapeless masses, the materials--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lie everywhere about us.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What we need
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is the celestial fire to change the flint
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into transparent crystal, bright and clear.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That fire is genius!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The rude peasant sits
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At evening in his smoky cot, and draws
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With charcoal uncouth figures on the wall.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The son of genius comes, foot-sore with travel,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And begs a shelter from the inclement night.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He takes the charcoal from the peasant's hand,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, by the magic of his touch at once
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Transfigured, all its hidden virtues shine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, in the eyes of the astonished clown,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It gleams a diamond!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Even thus transformed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rude popular traditions and old tales
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shine as immortal poems, at the touch
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of some poor, houseless, homeless, wandering bard,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who had but a night's lodging for his pains.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But there are brighter dreams than those of Fame,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which are the dreams of Love!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Out of the heart
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rises the bright ideal of these dreams,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As from some woodland fount a spirit rises
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And sinks again into its silent deeps,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ere the enamored knight can touch her robe!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T is this ideal that the soul of man,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the enamored knight beside the fountain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Waits for upon the margin of Life's stream;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Waits to behold her rise from the dark waters,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Clad in a mortal shape!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alas! how many
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Must wait in vain!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The stream flows evermore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But from its silent deeps no spirit rises!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet I, born under a propitious star,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Have found the bright ideal of my dreams.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes! she is ever with me.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I can feel,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here, as I sit at midnight and alone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Her gentle breathing! on my breast can feel
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The pressure of her head!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;God's benison
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rest ever on it!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Close those beauteous eyes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sweet Sleep! and all the flowers that bloom at night
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With balmy lips breathe in her ears my name!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Gradually sinks asleep.)



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ACT II.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SCENE I. -- PRECIOSA'S chamber.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Morning.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;PRECIOSA and ANGELICA.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why will you go so soon?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stay yet awhile.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The poor too often turn away unheard
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From hearts that shut against them with a sound
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That will be heard in heaven.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray, tell me more
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of your adversities.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Keep nothing from me.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What is your landlord's name?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ang.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Count of Lara.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Count of Lara?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, beware that man!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mistrust his pity,--hold no parley with him!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And rather die an outcast in the streets
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Than touch his gold.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ang.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You know him, then!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As much
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As any woman may, and yet be pure.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As you would keep your name without a blemish,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beware of him!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ang.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alas! what can I do?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I cannot choose my friends.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Each word of kindness,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come whence it may, is welcome to the poor.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Make me your friend.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A girl so young and fair
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Should have no friends but those of her own sex.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What is your name?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ang.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Angelica.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That name
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was given you, that you might be an angel
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To her who bore you!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When your infant smile
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Made her home Paradise, you were her angel.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, be an angel still!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She needs that smile.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So long as you are innocent, fear nothing.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No one can harm you!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am a poor girl,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whom chance has taken from the public streets.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have no other shield than mine own virtue.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That is the charm which has protected me!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Amid a thousand perils, I have worn it
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here on my heart!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is my guardian angel.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ang. (rising).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I thank you for this counsel, dearest lady.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thank me by following it.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ang.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Indeed I will.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray, do not go.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have much more to say.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ang.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My mother is alone.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I dare not leave her.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some other time, then, when we meet again.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You must not go away with words alone.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Gives her a purse.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Take this.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Would it were more.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ang.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I thank you, lady.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No thanks.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To-morrow come to me again.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I dance to-night,--perhaps for the last time.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But what I gain, I promise shall be yours,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If that can save you from the Count of Lara.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ang.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, my dear lady! how shall I be grateful
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For so much kindness?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;deserve no thanks,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thank Heaven, not me.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ang.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Both Heaven and you.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Farewell.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Remember that you come again tomorrow.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ang.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And may the Blessed Virgin guard you,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And all good angels.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;[Exit.]

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;May they guard thee too,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And all the poor; for they have need of angels.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;bring me, dear Dolores, my basquina,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My richest maja dress,--my dancing dress,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And my most precious jewels!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Make me look
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fairer than night e'er saw me!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I've a prize
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To win this day, worthy of Preciosa!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Enter BELTRAN CRUZADO.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ave Maria!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O God! my evil genius!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What seekest thou here to-day?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thyself,--my child.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What is thy will with me?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gold! gold!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I gave thee yesterday; I have no more.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The gold of the Busne,--give me his gold!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I gave the last in charity to-day.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That is a foolish lie.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is the truth.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Curses upon thee!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou art not my child!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hast thou given gold away, and not to me?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not to thy father?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To whom, then?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To one
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who needs it more.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No one can need it more.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou art not poor.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What, I, who lurk about
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In dismal suburbs and unwholesome lanes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I, who am housed worse than the galley slave;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I, who am fed worse than the kennelled hound;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I, who am clothed in rags,--Beltran Cruzado,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not poor!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec. Thou hast a stout heart and strong hands.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou canst supply thy wants; what wouldst thou more?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The gold of the Busne! give me his gold!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beltran Cruzado! hear me once for all.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I speak the truth.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So long as I had gold,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I gave it to thee freely, at all times,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never denied thee; never had a wish
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But to fulfil thine own.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now go in peace!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be merciful, be patient, and ere long
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou shalt have more.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And if I have it not,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou shalt no longer dwell here in rich chambers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wear silken dresses, feed on dainty food,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And live in idleness; but go with me,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dance the Romalis in the public streets,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And wander wild again o'er field and fell;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For here we stay not long.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What! march again?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay, with all speed. I hate the crowded town!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I cannot breathe shut up within its gates
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Air,--I want air, and sunshine, and blue sky,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The feeling of the breeze upon my face,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The feeling of the turf beneath my feet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And no walls but the far-off mountain-tops.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then I am free and strong,--once more myself,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beltran Cruzado, Count of the Cales!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;God speed thee on thy march!--I cannot go.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Remember who I am, and who thou art
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be silent and obey! Yet one thing more.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bartolome Roman--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec. (with emotion).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, I beseech thee
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If my obedience and blameless life,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If my humility and meek submission
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In all things hitherto, can move in thee
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One feeling of compassion; if thou art
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Indeed my father, and canst trace in me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One look of her who bore me, or one tone
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That doth remind thee of her, let it plead
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In my behalf, who am a feeble girl,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Too feeble to resist, and do not force me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To wed that man!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am afraid of him!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I do not love him! On my knees I beg thee
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To use no violence, nor do in haste
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What cannot be undone!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O child, child, child!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou hast betrayed thy secret, as a bird
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Betrays her nest, by striving to conceal it.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will not leave thee here in the great city
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To be a grandee's mistress.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Make thee ready
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To go with us; and until then remember
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A watchful eye is on thee.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;[Exit.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Woe is me!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have a strange misgiving in my heart!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But that one deed of charity I'll do,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Befall what may; they cannot take that from me.



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SCENE II --&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A room in the ARCHBISHOP'S Palace. The ARCHBISHOP and a CARDINAL seated.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Arch.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Knowing how near it touched the public morals,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And that our age is grown corrupt and rotten
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By such excesses, we have sent to Rome,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beseeching that his Holiness would aid
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In curing the gross surfeit of the time,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By seasonable stop put here in Spain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To bull-fights and lewd dances on the stage.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All this you know.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Card.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Know and approve.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Arch.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And further,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That, by a mandate from his Holiness,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The first have been suppressed.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Card.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I trust forever.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was a cruel sport.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Arch.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A barbarous pastime,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Disgraceful to the land that calls itself
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Most Catholic and Christian.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Card.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet the people
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Murmur at this; and, if the public dances
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Should be condemned upon too slight occasion,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Worse ills might follow than the ills we cure.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As Panem et Circenses was the cry
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Among the Roman populace of old,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So Pan y Toros is the cry in Spain.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hence I would act advisedly herein;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And therefore have induced your Grace to see
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These national dances, ere we interdict them.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Enter a Servant)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Serv.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The dancing-girl, and with her the musicians
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Your Grace was pleased to order, wait without.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Arch.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bid them come in.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now shall your eyes behold
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In what angelic, yet voluptuous shape
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Devil came to tempt Saint Anthony.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Enter PRECIOSA, with a mantle thrown over her head.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She advances slowly, in modest, half-timid attitude.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Card. (aside).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, what a fair and ministering angel
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was lost to heaven when this sweet woman fell!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec. (kneeling before the ARCHBISHOP).
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have obeyed the order of your Grace.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If I intrude upon your better hours,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I proffer this excuse, and here beseech
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Your holy benediction.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Arch.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;May God bless thee,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And lead thee to a better life.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Arise.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Card. (aside).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Her acts are modest, and her words discreet!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I did not look for this!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come hither, child.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is thy name Preciosa?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus I am called.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Card.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That is a Gypsy name.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who is thy father?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beltran Cruzado, Count of the Cales.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Arch.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have a dim remembrance of that man:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He was a bold and reckless character,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A sun-burnt Ishmael!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Card.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dost thou remember
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy earlier days?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes; by the Darro's side
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My childhood passed.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I can remember still
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The river, and the mountains capped with snow
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The village, where, yet a little child,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I told the traveller's fortune in the street;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The smuggler's horse, the brigand and the shepherd;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The march across the moor; the halt at noon;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The red fire of the evening camp, that lighted
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The forest where we slept; and, further back,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As in a dream or in some former life,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gardens and palace walls.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Arch.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T is the Alhambra,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Under whose towers the Gypsy camp was pitched.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the time wears; and we would see thee dance.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Your Grace shall be obeyed.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (She lays aside her mantilla.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The music of the cachucha is played, and the dance begins.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The ARCHBISHOP and the CARDINAL look on with gravity and an occasional frown; then make signs to each other; and, as the dance continues, become more and more pleased and excited; and at length rise from their seats, throw their caps in the air, and applaud vehemently as the scene closes.)



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SCENE III. -- The Prado.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A long avenue of trees leading to the gate of Atocha.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the right the dome and spires of a convent.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A fountain. Evening, DON CARLOS and HYPOLITO meeting.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hola! good evening, Don Hypolito.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And a good evening to my friend, Don Carlos.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some lucky star has led my steps this way.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I was in search of you.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don. C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Command me always.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp. Do you remember, in Quevedo's Dreams,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The miser, who, upon the Day of Judgment,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Asks if his money-bags would rise?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I do;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But what of that?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am that wretched man.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You mean to tell me yours have risen empty?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And amen! said my Cid the Campeador.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray, how much need you?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some half-dozen ounces,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which, with due interest--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C. (giving his purse).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What, am I a Jew
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To put my moneys out at usury?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here is my purse.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thank you.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A pretty purse.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Made by the hand of some fair Madrilena;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Perhaps a keepsake.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No, 't is at your service.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thank you again.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lie there, good Chrysostom,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And with thy golden mouth remind me often,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am the debtor of my friend.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But tell me,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come you to-day from Alcala?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This moment.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And pray, how fares the brave Victorian?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Indifferent well; that is to say, not well.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A damsel has ensnared him with the glances
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of her dark, roving eyes, as herdsmen catch
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A steer of Andalusia with a lazo.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He is in love.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And is it faring ill
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To be in love?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In his case very ill.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why so?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For many reasons.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;First and foremost,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Because he is in love with an ideal;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A creature of his own imagination;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A child of air; an echo of his heart;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, like a lily on a river floating,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She floats upon the river of his thoughts!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A common thing with poets.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But who is
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This floating lily?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For, in fine, some woman,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some living woman,--not a mere ideal,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Must wear the outward semblance of his thought.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who is it?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tell me.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Well, it is a woman!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But, look you, from the coffer of his heart
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He brings forth precious jewels to adorn her,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As pious priests adorn some favorite saint
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With gems and gold, until at length she gleams
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One blaze of glory.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Without these, you know,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the priest's benediction, 't is a doll.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Well, well! who is this doll?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why, who do you think?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His cousin Violante.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Guess again.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To ease his laboring heart, in the last storm
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He threw her overboard, with all her ingots.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I cannot guess; so tell me who it is.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not I.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don. C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why not?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp. (mysteriously).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Because Mari Franca
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was married four leagues out of Salamanca!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Jesting aside, who is it?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Preciosa.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Impossible!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Count of Lara tells me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She is not virtuous.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Did I say she was?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Roman Emperor Claudius had a wife
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose name was Messalina, as I think;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Valeria Messalina was her name.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But hist! I see him yonder through the trees,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Walking as in a dream.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He comes this way.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It has been truly said by some wise man,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That money, grief, and love cannot be hidden.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Enter VICTORIAN in front.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where'er thy step has passed is holy ground!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These groves are sacred!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I behold thee walking
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Under these shadowy trees, where we have walked
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At evening, and I feel thy presence now;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Feel that the place has taken a charm from thee,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And is forever hallowed.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mark him well!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;See how he strides away with lordly air,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like that odd guest of stone, that grim Commander
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who comes to sup with Juan in the play.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What ho!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Victorian!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wilt thou sup with us?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hola! amigos!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Faith, I did not see you.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How fares Don Carlos?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At your service ever.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How is that young and green-eyed Gaditana
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That you both wot of?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay, soft, emerald eyes!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She has gone back to Cadiz.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay de mi!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You are much to blame for letting her go back.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A pretty girl; and in her tender eyes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Just that soft shade of green we sometimes see
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In evening skies.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But, speaking of green eyes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are thine green?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not a whit.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why so?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I think
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The slightest shade of green would be becoming,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For thou art jealous.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vid.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No, I am not jealous.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou shouldst be.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Because thou art in love.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And they who are in love are always jealous.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Therefore thou shouldst be.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Marry, is that all?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Farewell; I am in haste.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Farewell, Don Carlos.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou sayest I should be jealous?


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay, in truth
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I fear there is reason.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be upon thy guard.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hear it whispered that the Count of Lara

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Indeed!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then he will have his labor for his pains.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He does not think so, and Don Carlos tells me

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How's this, Don Carlos?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don. C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some hints of it I heard from his own lips.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Death and damnation!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I'll cut his lying tongue out of his mouth,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And throw it to my dog!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But no, no, no!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This cannot be.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You jest, indeed you jest.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hunting the traitor Quadros to his death,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;[Exeunt.



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SCENE IV. -- PRECIOSA'S chamber.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She is sitting, with a book in her hand, near a table, on which are flowers.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A bird singing in its cage. The COUNT OF LARA enters behind unperceived.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec. (reads).
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; All are sleeping, weary heart!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Thou, thou only sleepless art!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Heigho! I wish Victorian were here.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I know not what it is makes me so restless!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(The bird sings.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou little prisoner with thy motley coat,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That from thy vaulted, wiry dungeon singest,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like thee I am a captive, and, like thee,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have a gentle jailer.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lack-a-day!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; All are sleeping, weary heart!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Thou, thou only sleepless art!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; All this throbbing, all this aching,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Evermore shall keep thee waking,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; For a heart in sorrow breaking
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Thinketh ever of its smart!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou speakest truly, poet! and methinks
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;More hearts are breaking in this world of ours
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Than one would say.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In distant villages
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And solitudes remote, where winds have wafted
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The barbed seeds of love, or birds of passage
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Scattered them in their flight, do they take root,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And grow in silence, and in silence perish.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who hears the falling of the forest leaf?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or who takes note of every flower that dies?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Heigho! I wish Victorian would come.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dolores!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Turns to lay down her boot and perceives the COUNT.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Ha!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Senora, pardon me.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How's this?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dolores!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pardon me--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dolores!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be not alarmed; I found no one in waiting.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If I have been too bold--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec. (turning her back upon him).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You are too bold!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Retire! retire, and leave me!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My dear lady,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;First hear me!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I beseech you, let me speak!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T is for your good I come.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec. (turning toward him with indignation).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Begone! begone!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You are the Count of Lara, but your deeds
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Would make the statues of your ancestors
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blush on their tombs!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is it Castilian honor,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is it Castilian pride, to steal in here
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Upon a friendless girl, to do her wrong?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O shame! shame! shame! that you, a nobleman,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Should be so little noble in your thoughts
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As to send jewels here to win my love,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And think to buy my honor with your gold!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have no words to tell you how I scorn you!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Begone!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sight of you is hateful to me!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Begone, I say!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be calm; I will not harm you.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Because you dare not.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I dare anything!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Therefore beware!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You are deceived in me.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In this false world, we do not always know
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who are our friends and who our enemies.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We all have enemies, and all need friends.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Even you, fair Preciosa, here at court
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Have foes, who seek to wrong you.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If to this
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I owe the honor of the present visit,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You might have spared the coming.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Raving spoken,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Once more I beg you, leave me to myself.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I thought it but a friendly part to tell you
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What strange reports are current here in town.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For my own self, I do not credit them;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But there are many who, not knowing you,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Will lend a readier ear.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There was no need
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That you should take upon yourself the duty
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of telling me these tales.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Malicious tongues
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are ever busy with your name.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alas!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I've no protectors.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am a poor girl,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Exposed to insults and unfeeling jests.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They wound me, yet I cannot shield myself.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I give no cause for these reports.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I live
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Retired; am visited by none.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By none?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, then, indeed, you are much wronged!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How mean you?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nay, nay; I will not wound your gentle soul
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the report of idle tales.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Speak out!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What are these idle tales? You need not spare me.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will deal frankly with you.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pardon me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This window, as I think, looks toward the street,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And this into the Prado, does it not?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In yon high house, beyond the garden wall,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You see the roof there just above the trees,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There lives a friend, who told me yesterday,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That on a certain night,--be not offended
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If I too plainly speak,--he saw a man
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Climb to your chamber window.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You are silent!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I would not blame you, being young and fair--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(He tries to embrace her. She starts back, and draws a dagger from her bosom.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beware! beware!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am a Gypsy girl!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lay not your hand upon me.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One step nearer
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I will strike!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray you, put up that dagger.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fear not.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec. I do not fear.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have a heart
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In whose strength I can trust.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Listen to me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I come here as your friend,--I am your friend,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And by a single word can put a stop
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To all those idle tales, and make your name
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Spotless as lilies are.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here on my knees,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fair Preciosa! on my knees I swear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I love you even to madness, and that love
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Has driven me to break the rules of custom,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And force myself unasked into your presence.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(VICTORIAN enters behind.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rise, Count of Lara!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That is not the place
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For such as you are.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It becomes you not
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To kneel before me.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am strangely moved
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To see one of your rank thus low and humbled;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For your sake I will put aside all anger,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All unkind feeling, all dislike, and speak
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In gentleness, as most becomes a woman,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And as my heart now prompts me.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I no more
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Will hate you, for all hate is painful to me.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But if, without offending modesty
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And that reserve which is a woman's glory,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I may speak freely, I will teach my heart
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To love you.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O sweet angel!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay, in truth,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Far better than you love yourself or me.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Give me some sign of this,--the slightest token.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let me but kiss your hand!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nay, come no nearer.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The words I utter are its sign and token.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Misunderstand me not!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be not deceived!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The love wherewith I love you is not such
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As you would offer me.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For you come here
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To take from me the only thing I have,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My honor.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You are wealthy, you have friends
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And kindred, and a thousand pleasant hopes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That fill your heart with happiness; but I
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Am poor, and friendless, having but one treasure,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And you would take that from me, and for what?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To flatter your own vanity, and make me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What you would most despise.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O sir, such love,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That seeks to harm me, cannot be true love.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Indeed it cannot.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But my love for you
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is of a different kind.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It seeks your good.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is a holier feeling.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It rebukes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Your earthly passion, your unchaste desires,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And bids you look into your heart, and see
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How you do wrong that better nature in you,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And grieve your soul with sin.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I swear to you,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I would not harm you; I would only love you.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I would not take your honor, but restore it,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And in return I ask but some slight mark
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of your affection.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If indeed you love me,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As you confess you do, O let me thus
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With this embrace--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict. (rushing forward).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hold! hold!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This is too much.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What means this outrage?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;First, what right have you
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To question thus a nobleman of Spain?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I too am noble, and you are no more!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Out of my sight!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are you the master here?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay, here and elsewhere, when the wrong of others
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gives me the right!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec. (to LARA).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Go! I beseech you, go!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I shall have business with you, Count, anon!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You cannot come too soon!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; [Exit.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Victorian!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, we have been betrayed!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ha! ha! betrayed!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T is I have been betrayed, not we!--not we!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dost thou imagine--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I imagine nothing;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I see how 't is thou whilest the time away
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When I am gone!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O speak not in that tone!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It wounds me deeply.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T was not meant to flatter.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Too well thou knowest the presence of that man
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is hateful to me!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet I saw thee stand
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And listen to him, when he told his love.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I did not heed his words.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Indeed thou didst,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And answeredst them with love.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hadst thou heard all--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I heard enough.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be not so angry with me.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am not angry; I am very calm.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If thou wilt let me speak--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nay, say no more.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I know too much already.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou art false!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I do not like these Gypsy marriages!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where is the ring I gave thee?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In my casket.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There let it rest!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I would not have thee wear it:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I thought thee spotless, and thou art polluted!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I call the Heavens to witness--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nay, nay, nay!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Take not the name of Heaven upon thy lips!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They are forsworn!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Victorian! dear Victorian!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I gave up all for thee; myself, my fame,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My hopes of fortune, ay, my very soul!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And thou hast been my ruin!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now, go on!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Laugh at my folly with thy paramour,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, sitting on the Count of Lara's knee,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Say what a poor, fond fool Victorian was!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(He casts her from him and rushes out.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And this from thee!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Scene closes.)



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SCENE V. -- The COUNT OF LARA'S rooms.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enter the COUNT.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There's nothing in this world so sweet as love,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And next to love the sweetest thing is hate!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I've learned to hate, and therefore am revenged.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A silly girl to play the prude with me!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The fire that I have kindled--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Enter FRANCISCO.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Well, Francisco,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What tidings from Don Juan?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fran.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Good, my lord;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He will be present.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the Duke of Lermos?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fran.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was not at home.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How with the rest?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fran.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I've found
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The men you wanted.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They will all be there,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And at the given signal raise a whirlwind
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of such discordant noises, that the dance
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Must cease for lack of music.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bravely done.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah! little dost thou dream, sweet Preciosa,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What lies in wait for thee.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sleep shall not close
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thine eyes this night!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Give me my cloak and sword. [Exeunt.



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SCENE VI. -- A retired spot beyond the city gates.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enter VICTORIAN and HYPOLITO.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O shame! O shame!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why do I walk abroad
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By daylight, when the very sunshine mocks me,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And voices, and familiar sights and sounds
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cry, "Hide thyself!"&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O what a thin partition
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Doth shut out from the curious world the knowledge
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of evil deeds that have been done in darkness!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Disgrace has many tongues.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My fears are windows,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through which all eyes seem gazing.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Every face
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Expresses some suspicion of my shame,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And in derision seems to smile at me!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Did I not caution thee?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Did I not tell thee
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I was but half persuaded of her virtue?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And yet, Hypolito, we may be wrong,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We may be over-hasty in condemning!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Count of Lara is a cursed villain.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And therefore is she cursed, loving him.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vid.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She does not love him! 'T is for gold! for gold!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay, but remember, in the public streets
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He shows a golden ring the Gypsy gave him,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A serpent with a ruby in its mouth.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She had that ring from me!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;God! she is false!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But I will be revenged!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The hour is passed.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where stays the coward?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nay, he is no coward;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A villain, if thou wilt, but not a coward.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I've seen him play with swords; it is his pastime.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And therefore be not over-confident,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He'll task thy skill anon.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Look, here he comes.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Enter LARA followed by FRNANCISCO)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Good evening, gentlemen.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Good evening, Count.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I trust I have not kept you long in waiting.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not long, and yet too long.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are you prepared?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It grieves me much to see this quarrel
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Between you, gentlemen.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is there no way
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Left open to accord this difference,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But you must make one with your swords?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No! none!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I do entreat thee, dear Hypolito,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stand not between me an my foe.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Too long
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Our tongues have spoken.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let these tongues of steel
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;End our debate.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Upon your guard, Sir Count.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(They fight. VICTORIAN disarms the COUNT.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Your life is mine; and what shall now withhold me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From sending your vile soul to its account?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Strike! strike!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You are disarmed.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will not kill you.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will not murder you.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Take up your sword.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(FRANCISCO hands the COUNT his sword, and HYPOLITO interposes.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enough!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let it end here!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Count of Lara
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Has shown himself a brave man, and Victorian
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A generous one, as ever.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now be friends.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Put up your swords; for, to speak frankly to you,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Your cause of quarrel is too slight a thing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To move you to extremes.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am content,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I sought no quarrel.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A few hasty words,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Spoken in the heat of blood, have led to this.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nay, something more than that.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I understand you.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Therein I did not mean to cross your path.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To me the door stood open, as to others.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But, had I known the girl belonged to you,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never would I have sought to win her from you.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The truth stands now revealed; she has been false
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To both of us.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay, false as hell itself!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In truth, I did not seek her; she sought me;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And told me how to win her, telling me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The hours when she was oftenest left alone.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Say, can you prove this to me?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, pluck out
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These awful doubts, that goad me into madness!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let me know all! all! all!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You shall know all.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here is my page, who was the messenger
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Between us.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Question him.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was it not so,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Francisco?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fran.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay, my lord.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If further proof
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is needful, I have here a ring she gave me.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict. Pray let me see that ring!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is the same!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Throws it upon the ground, and tramples upon it.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus may she perish who once wore that ring!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus do I spurn her from me; do thus trample
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Her memory in the dust!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O Count of Lara,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We both have been abused, been much abused!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I thank you for your courtesy and frankness.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Though, like the surgeon's hand, yours gave me pain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet it has cured my blindness, and I thank you.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I now can see the folly I have done,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Though 't is, alas! too late.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So fare you well!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To-night I leave this hateful town forever.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Regard me as your friend. Once more farewell!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Farewell, Sir Count.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; [Exeunt VICTORIAN and HYPOLITO.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lara.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Farewell! farewell! farewell!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus have I cleared the field of my worst foe!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have none else to fear; the fight is done,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The citadel is stormed, the victory won!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;[Exit with FRANCISCO.]



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SCENE VII. -- A lane in the suburbs.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Night.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enter CRUZADO and BARTOLOME.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And so, Bartolome, the expedition failed.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But where wast thou for the most part?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bart.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the Guadarrama mountains, near San Ildefonso.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And thou bringest nothing back with thee?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Didst thou rob no one?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bart.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There was no one to rob, save a party of students from Segovia, who looked as if they would rob us; and a jolly little friar, who had nothing in his pockets but a missal and a loaf of bread.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray, then, what brings thee back to Madrid?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bart.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;First tell me what keeps thee here?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Preciosa.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bart.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And she brings me back.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hast thou forgotten thy promise?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The two years are not passed yet.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wait patiently.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The girl shall be thine.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bart.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hear she has a Busne lover.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That is nothing.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bart.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I do not like it.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hate him,--the son of a Busne harlot.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He goes in and out, and speaks with her alone, and I must stand aside, and wait his pleasure.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be patient, I say.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou shalt have thy revenge.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When the time comes, thou shalt waylay him.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bart.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Meanwhile, show me her house.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cruz.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come this way.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But thou wilt not find her.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She dances at the play to-night.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bart.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No matter.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Show me the house.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ACT III.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SCENE I. -- A cross-road through a wood.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the background a distant village spire.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;VICTORIAN and HYPOLITO, as travelling students, with guitars, sitting under the trees.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;HYPOLITO plays and sings.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SONG.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Ah, Love!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Perjured, false, treacherous Love!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Enemy
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of all that mankind may not rue!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Most untrue
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To him who keeps most faith with thee.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Woe is me!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The falcon has the eyes of the dove.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Ah, Love!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Perjured, false, treacherous Love!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes, Love is ever busy with his shuttle,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is ever weaving into life's dull warp
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bright, gorgeous flowers and scenes Arcadian;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hanging our gloomy prison-house about
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With tapestries, that make its walls dilate
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In never-ending vistas of delight.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thinking to walk in those Arcadian pastures,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou hast run thy noble head against the wall.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SONG (continued).

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Thy deceits
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Give us clearly to comprehend,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Whither tend
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All thy pleasures, all thy sweets!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; They are cheats,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thorns below and flowers above.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Ah, Love!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Perjured, false, treacherous Love!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A very pretty song.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I thank thee for it.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It suits thy case.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Indeed, I think it does.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What wise man wrote it?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lopez Maldonado.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In truth, a pretty song.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With much truth in it.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hope thou wilt profit by it; and in earnest
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Try to forget this lady of thy love.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will forget her!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All dear recollections
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pressed in my heart, like flowers within a book,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shall be torn out, and scattered to the winds!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will forget her!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But perhaps hereafter,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When she shall learn how heartless is the world,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A voice within her will repeat my name,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And she will say, "He was indeed my friend!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, would I were a soldier, not a scholar,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That the loud march, the deafening beat of drums,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The shattering blast of the brass-throated trumpet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The din of arms, the onslaught and the storm,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And a swift death, might make me deaf forever
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the upbraidings of this foolish heart!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then let that foolish heart upbraid no more!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To conquer love, one need but will to conquer.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet, good Hypolito, it is in vain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I throw into Oblivion's sea the sword
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That pierces me; for, like Excalibar,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With gemmed and flashing hilt, it will not sink.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There rises from below a hand that grasp it,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And waves it in the air; and wailing voices
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are heard along the shore.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And yet at last
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Down sank Excalibar to rise no more.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This is not well.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In truth, it vexes me.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Instead of whistling to the steeds of Time,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To make them jog on merrily with life's burden,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a dead weight thou hangest on the wheels.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou art too young, too full of lusty health
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To talk of dying.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet I fain would die!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To go through life, unloving and unloved;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To feel that thirst and hunger of the soul
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We cannot still; that longing, that wild impulse,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And struggle after something we have not
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And cannot have; the effort to be strong
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, like the Spartan boy, to smile, and smile,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While secret wounds do bleed beneath our cloaks
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All this the dead feel not,--the dead alone!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Would I were with them!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We shall all be soon.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It cannot be too soon; for I am weary
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the bewildering masquerade of Life,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where strangers walk as friends, and friends as strangers;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where whispers overheard betray false hearts;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And through the mazes of the crowd we chase
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some form of loveliness, that smiles, and beckons,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And cheats us with fair words, only to leave us
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A mockery and a jest; maddened,--confused,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not knowing friend from foe.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why seek to know?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enjoy the merry shrove-tide of thy youth!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Take each fair mask for what it gives itself,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor strive to look beneath it.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I confess,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That were the wiser part.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But Hope no longer
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Comforts my soul.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am a wretched man,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Much like a poor and shipwrecked mariner,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who, struggling to climb up into the boat,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Has both his bruised and bleeding hands cut off,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And sinks again into the weltering sea,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Helpless and hopeless!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet thou shalt not perish.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The strength of thine own arm is thy salvation.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Above thy head, through rifted clouds, there shines
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A glorious star.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be patient.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Trust thy star!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Sound of a village belt in the distance.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ave Maria!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hear the sacristan
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ringing the chimes from yonder village belfry!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A solemn sound, that echoes far and wide
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over the red roofs of the cottages,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And bids the laboring hind a-field, the shepherd,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Guarding his flock, the lonely muleteer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And all the crowd in village streets, stand still,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And breathe a prayer unto the blessed Virgin!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Amen! amen!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not half a league from hence
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The village lies.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This path will lead us to it,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over the wheat-fields, where the shadows sail
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Across the running sea, now green, now blue,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, like an idle mariner on the main,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whistles the quail.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come, let us hasten on.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;[Exeunt.]



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SCENE II. -- Public square in the village of Guadarrama.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Ave Maria still tolling.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A crowd of villagers, with their hats in their hands, as if in prayer.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In front, a group of Gypsies.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The bell rings a merrier peal.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A Gypsy dance.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enter PANCHO, followed by PEDRO CRESPO.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pancho.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Make room, ye vagabonds and Gypsy thieves!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Make room for the Alcalde and for me!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pedro C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Keep silence all!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have an edict here
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From our most gracious lord, the King of Spain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Jerusalem, and the Canary Islands,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which I shall publish in the market-place.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Open your ears and listen!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Enter the PADRE CURA at the door of his cottage.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre Cura,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Good day! and, pray you, hear this edict read.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Good day, and God be with you!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray, what is it?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pedro C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;An act of banishment against the Gypsies!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Agitation and murmurs in the crowd.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pancho.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Silence!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pedro C. (reads).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I hereby order and command,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That the Egyptian an Chaldean strangers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Known by the name of Gypsies, shall henceforth
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be banished from the realm, as vagabonds
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And beggars; and if, after seventy days,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Any be found within our kingdom's bounds,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They shall receive a hundred lashes each;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The second time, shall have their ears cut off;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The third, be slaves for life to him who takes them,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or burnt as heretics.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Signed, I, the King."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vile miscreants and creatures unbaptized!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You hear the law!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Obey and disappear!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pancho.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And if in seventy days you are not gone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dead or alive I make you all my slaves.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(The Gypsies go out in confusion, showing signs of fear and discontent.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;PANCHO follows.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A righteous law!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A very righteous law!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray you, sit down.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pedro C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I thank you heartily.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(They seat themselves on a bench at the PADRE CURAS door.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sound of guitars heard at a distance, approaching during the dialogue which follows.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A very righteous judgment, as you say.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now tell me, Padre Cura,--you know all things,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How came these Gypsies into Spain?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why, look you;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They came with Hercules from Palestine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And hence are thieves and vagrants, Sir Alcalde,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the Simoniacs from Simon Magus,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, look you, as Fray Jayme Bleda says,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There are a hundred marks to prove a Moor
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is not a Christian, so 't is with the Gypsies.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They never marry, never go to mass,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never baptize their children, nor keep Lent,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor see the inside of a church,--nor--nor--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pedro C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Good reasons, good, substantial reasons all!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No matter for the other ninety-five.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They should be burnt, I see it plain enough,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They should be bunt.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Enter VICTORIAN and HYPOLITO playing.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And pray, whom have we here?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pedro C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;More vagrants!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By Saint Lazarus, more vagrants!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Good evening, gentlemen!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is this Guadarrama?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes, Guadarrama, and good evening to you.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We seek the Padre Cura of the village;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, judging from your dress and reverend mien,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You must be he.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray, what's your pleasure?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We are poor students, traveling in vacation.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You know this mark?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Touching the wooden spoon in his hat-band.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C. (joyfully).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay, know it, and have worn it.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pedro C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(aside).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Soup-eaters! by the mass!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The worst of vagrants!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And there's no law against them.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sir, your servant.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;[Exit.]

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Your servant, Pedro Crespo.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Padre Cura,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Front the first moment I beheld your face,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I said within myself, "This is the man!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is a certain something in your looks,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A certain scholar-like and studious something,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You understand,--which cannot be mistaken;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which marks you as a very learned man,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In fine, as one of us.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict. (aside).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What impudence!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As we approached, I said to my companion,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"That is the Padre Cura; mark my words!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Meaning your Grace.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"The other man," said I,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who sits so awkwardly upon the bench,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Must be the sacristan."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah! said you so?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why, that was Pedro Crespo, the alcalde!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Indeed! you much astonish me!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His air
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was not so full of dignity and grace
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As an alcalde's should be.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That is true.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He's out of humor with some vagrant Gypsies,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who have their camp here in the neighborhood.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There's nothing so undignified as anger.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Padre Cura will excuse our boldness,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If, from his well-known hospitality,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We crave a lodging for the night.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I pray you!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You do me honor!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am but too happy
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To have such guests beneath my humble roof.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is not often that I have occasion
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To speak with scholars; and Emollit mores,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nec sinit esse feros, Cicero says.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T is Ovid, is it not?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No, Cicero.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Your Grace is right.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You are the better scholar.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now what a dunce was I to think it Ovid!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But hang me if it is not! (Aside.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pass this way.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He was a very great man, was Cicero!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray you, go in, go in! no ceremony.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;[Exeunt.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SCENE III. -- A room in the PADRE CURA'S&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;house.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enter the PADRE and HYPOLITO.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So then, Senor, you come from Alcala.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am glad to hear it.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was there I studied.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And left behind an honored name, no doubt.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How may I call your Grace?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Geronimo
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;De Santillana, at your Honor's service.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Descended from the Marquis Santillana?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the distinguished poet?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the Marquis,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not from the poet.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why, they were the same.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let me embrace you!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O some lucky star
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Has brought me hither!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet once more!--once more!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Your name is ever green in Alcala,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And our professor, when we are unruly,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Will shake his hoary head, and say, "Alas!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was not so in Santillana's time!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I did not think my name remembered there.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;More than remembered; it is idolized.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of what professor speak you?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Timoneda.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C. I don't remember any Timoneda.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A grave and sombre man, whose beetling brow
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'erhangs the rushing current of his speech
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As rocks o'er rivers hang.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Have you forgotten?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Indeed, I have.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, those were pleasant days,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Those college days!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I ne'er shall see the like!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I had not buried then so many hopes!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I had not buried then so many friends!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I've turned my back on what was then before me;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the bright faces of my young companions
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are wrinkled like my own, or are no more.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Do you remember Cueva?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cueva?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cueva?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fool that I am!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He was before your time.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You're a mere boy, and I am an old man.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I should not like to try my strength with you.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Well, well.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But I forget; you must be hungry.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Martina! ho! Martina!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T is my niece.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Enter MARTINA.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You may be proud of such a niece as that.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I wish I had a niece.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Emollit mores.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Aside.)
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He was a very great man, was Cicero!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Your servant, fair Martina.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mart.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Servant, sir.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This gentleman is hungry.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;See thou to it.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let us have supper.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mart.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T will be ready soon.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And bring a bottle of my Val-de-Penas
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Out of the cellar.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stay; I'll go myself.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray you. Senor, excuse me.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;[Exit.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hist! Martina!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One word with you.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bless me I what handsome eyes!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To-day there have been Gypsies in the village.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is it not so?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mart.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There have been Gypsies here.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes, and have told your fortune.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mart. (embarrassed).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Told my fortune?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes, yes; I know they did.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Give me your hand.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I'll tell you what they said.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They said,--they said,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The shepherd boy that loved you was a clown,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And him you should not marry.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was it not?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mart. (surprised).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How know you that?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, I know more than that,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What a soft, little hand!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And then they said,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A cavalier from court, handsome, and tall
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And rich, should come one day to marry you,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And you should be a lady.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was it not!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He has arrived, the handsome cavalier.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Tries to kiss her.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She runs off.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enter VICTORIAN, with a letter.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The muleteer has come.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So soon?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I found him
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sitting at supper by the tavern door,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, from a pitcher that he held aloft
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His whole arm's length, drinking the blood-red wine.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What news from Court?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He brought this letter only.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Reads.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O cursed perfidy!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why did I let
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That lying tongue deceive me!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Preciosa,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sweet Preciosa! how art thou avenged!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What news is this, that makes thy cheek turn pale,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And thy hand tremble?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, most infamous!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Count of Lara is a worthless villain!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That is no news, forsooth.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He strove in vain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To steal from me the jewel of my soul,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The love of Preciosa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not succeeding,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He swore to be revenged; and set on foot
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A plot to ruin her, which has succeeded.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She has been hissed and hooted from the stage,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Her reputation stained by slanderous lies
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Too foul to speak of; and, once more a beggar,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She roams a wanderer over God's green earth
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Housing with Gypsies!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To renew again
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Age of Gold, and make the shepherd swains
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Desperate with love, like Gasper Gil's Diana.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Redit et Virgo!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dear Hypolito,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How have I wronged that meek, confiding heart!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will go seek for her; and with my tears
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wash out the wrong I've done her!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; O beware!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Act not that folly o'er again.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Ay, folly,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Delusion, madness, call it what thou wilt,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will confess my weakness,--I still love her!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Still fondly love her!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Enter the PADRE CURA.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tell us, Padre Cura,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who are these Gypsies in the neighborhood?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beltran Cruzado and his crew.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kind Heaven,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I thank thee!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She is found! is found again!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And have they with them a pale, beautiful girl,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Called Preciosa?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay, a pretty girl.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The gentleman seems moved.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes, moved with hunger,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He is half famished with this long day's journey.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Padre C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then, pray you, come this way.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The supper waits.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;[Exeunt.]



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SCENE IV. -- A post-house on the road to Segovia, not far from the village of Guadarrama.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enter CHISPA, cracking a whip, and singing the cachucha.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Halloo!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don Fulano!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let us have horses, and quickly. Alas, poor Chispa! what a dog's life dost thou lead!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I thought, when I left my old master Victorian, the student, to serve my new master Don Carlos, the gentleman, that I, too, should lead the life of a gentleman; should go to bed early, and get up late. For when the abbot plays cards, what can you expect of the friars?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But, in running away from the thunder, I have run into the lightning. Here I am in hot chase after my master and his Gypsy girl. And a good beginning of the week it is, as he said who was hanged on Monday morning.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Enter DON CARLOS)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are not the horses ready yet?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I should think not, for the hostler seems to be asleep. Ho! within there!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Horses! horses! horses!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(He knocks at the gate&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;with his whip, and enter MOSQUITO, putting on his jacket.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mosq.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray, have a little patience.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I'm not a musket.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Health and pistareens!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I'm glad to see you come on dancing, padre!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pray, what's the news?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mosq.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You cannot have fresh horses; because there are none.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cachiporra! Throw that bone to another dog.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Do I look like your aunt?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mosq.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No; she has a beard.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Go to! go to!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mosq.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are you from Madrid?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes; and going to Estramadura.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Get us horses.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mosq.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What's the news at Court?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why, the latest news is, that I am going to set up a coach, and I have already bought the whip.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Strikes him round the legs.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mosq.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Oh! oh! You hurt me!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enough of this folly.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let us have horses.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Gives money to MOSQUITO.)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is almost dark; and we are in haste.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But tell me, has a band of Gypsies passed this way of late?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mosq.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes; and they are still in the neighborhood.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And where?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mosq.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Across the fields yonder, in the woods near Guadarrama.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; [Exit.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now this is lucky.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We will visit the Gypsy camp.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are you not afraid of the evil eye?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Have you a stag's horn with you?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fear not.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We will pass the night at the village.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And sleep like the Squires of Hernan Daza, nine under one blanket.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hope we may find the Preciosa among them.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Among the Squires?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No; among the Gypsies, blockhead!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hope we may; for we are giving ourselves trouble enough on her account.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don't you think so?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;However, there is no catching trout without wetting one's trousers.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yonder come the horses.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; [Exeunt.]

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; His body is in Segovia,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; His soul is in Madrid,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is this a dream?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, if it be a dream,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let me sleep on, and do not wake me yet!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Repeat thy story!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Say I'm not deceived!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Say that I do not dream!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am awake;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This is the Gypsy camp; this is Victorian,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And this his friend, Hypolito!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Speak! speak!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let me not wake and find it all a dream!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is a dream, sweet child! a waking dream,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A blissful certainty, a vision bright
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of that rare happiness, which even on earth
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Heaven gives to those it loves.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now art thou rich,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As thou wast ever beautiful and good;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I am now the beggar.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec. (giving him her hand).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have still
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A hand to give.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa (aside).&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I have two to take.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I've heard my grandmother say, that Heaven gives almonds
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To those who have no teeth.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That's nuts to crack,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I've teeth to spare, but where shall I find almonds?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What more of this strange story?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nothing more.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Your friend, Don Carlos, is now at the village
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Showing to Pedro Crespo, the Alcalde,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The proofs of what I tell you.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The old hag,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who stole you in your childhood, has confessed;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And probably they'll hang her for the crime,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To make the celebration more complete.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No; let it be a day of general joy;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fortune comes well to all, that comes not late.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now let us join Don Carlos.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So farewell,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The student's wandering life!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sweet serenades,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sung under ladies' windows in the night,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And all that makes vacation beautiful!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To you, ye cloistered shades of Alcala,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To you, ye radiant visions of romance,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Written in books, but here surpassed by truth,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Bachelor Hypolito returns,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And leaves the Gypsy with the Spanish Student.



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SCENE VI. -- A pass in the Guadarrama mountains.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Early morning. A muleteer crosses the stage, sitting sideways on his mule and lighting a paper cigar with flint and steel.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SONG.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If thou art sleeping, maiden,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Awake and open thy door,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T is the break of day, and we must away,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er meadow, and mount, and moor.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wait not to find thy slippers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But come with thy naked feet;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We shall have to pass through the dewy grass,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And waters wide and fleet.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Disappears down the pass.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enter a Monk.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A shepherd appears on the rocks above.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Monk.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ave Maria, gratia plena.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ola! good man!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shep.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ola!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Monk.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is this the road to Segovia?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shep.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is, your reverence.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Monk.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How far is it?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shep.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I do not know.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Monk.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What is that yonder in the valley?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shep.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;San Ildefonso.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Monk.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A long way to breakfast.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shep.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay, marry.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Monk.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are there robbers in these mountains?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shep.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes, and worse than that.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Monk.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shep.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wolves.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Monk.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Santa Maria!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come with me to San Ildefonso, and thou shalt be well rewarded.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shep.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What wilt thou give me?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Monk.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;An Agnus Dei and my benediction.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(They disappear.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A mounted Contrabandista passes, wrapped in his cloak, and a gun at his saddle-bow.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He goes down the pass singing.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SONG.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Worn with speed is my good steed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I march me hurried, worried;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Onward, caballito mio,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the white star in thy forehead!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Onward, for here comes the Ronda,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I hear their rifles crack!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay, jaleo!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay, ay, jaleo!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ay, jaleo!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They cross our track.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Song dies away.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enter PRECIOSA, on horseback, attended by VICTORIAN, HYPOLITO, DON CARLOS, and CHISPA, on foot, and armed.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This is the highest point.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here let us rest.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;See, Preciosa, see how all about us
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kneeling, like hooded friars, the misty mountains
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Receive the benediction of the sun!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O glorious sight!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Most beautiful indeed!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Most wonderful!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And in the vale below,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where yonder steeples flash like lifted halberds,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;San Ildefonso, from its noisy belfries,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sends up a salutation to the morn,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As if an army smote their brazen shields,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And shouted victory!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And which way lies Segovia?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At a great distance yonder.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dost thou not see it?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I do not see it.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The merest flaw that dents the horizon's edge.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There, yonder!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyp.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T is a notable old town,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Boasting an ancient Roman aqueduct,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And an Alcazar, builded by the Moors,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wherein, you may remember, poor Gil Blas
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was fed on Pan del Rey.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, many a time
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Out of its grated windows have I looked
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hundreds of feet plumb down to the Eresma,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That, like a serpent through the valley creeping,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Glides at its foot.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O yes!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I see it now,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet rather with my heart than with mine eyes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So faint it is.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And all my thoughts sail thither,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Freighted with prayers and hopes, and forward urged
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Against all stress of accident, as in
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Eastern Tale, against the wind and tide
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Great ships were drawn to the Magnetic Mountains,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And there were wrecked, and perished in the sea!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(She weeps.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vict.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O gentle spirit!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou didst bear unmoved
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blasts of adversity and frosts of fate!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the first ray of sunshine that falls on thee
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Melts thee to tears!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, let thy weary heart
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lean upon mine! and it shall faint no more,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor thirst, nor hunger; but be comforted
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And filled with my affection.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prec.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stay no longer!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My father waits.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Methinks I see him there,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now looking from the window, and now watching
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Each sound of wheels or footfall in the street,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And saying, "Hark! she comes!"&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O father! father!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(They descend the pass.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;CHISPA remains behind.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chispa.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have a father, too, but he is a dead one.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alas and alack-a-day.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Poor was I born, and poor do I remain.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I neither win nor lose.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus I was, through the world, half the time on foot, and the other half walking; and always as merry as a thunder-storm in the night.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And so we plough along, as the fly said to the ox.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who knows what may happen?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Patience, and shuffle the cards!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am not yet so bald that you can see my brains; and perhaps, after all, I shall some day go to Rome, and come back Saint Peter.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Benedicite!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;[Exit.]

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(A pause.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then enter BARTOLOME wildly, as if in pursuit, with a carbine in his hand.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bart.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They passed this way!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hear their horses' hoofs!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yonder I see them!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come, sweet caramillo,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This serenade shall be the Gypsy's last!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Fires down the pass.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ha! ha!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Well whistled, my sweet caramillo!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Well whistled!--I have missed her!--O my God!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(The shot is returned.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;BARTOLOME falls).
