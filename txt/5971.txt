<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T is late at night, and in the realm of sleep
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My little lambs are folded like the flocks;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From room to room I hear the wakeful clocks
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Challenge the passing hour, like guards that keep
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their solitary watch on tower and steep;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Far off I hear the crowing of the cocks,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And through the opening door that time unlocks
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Feel the fresh breathing of To-morrow creep.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To-morrow! the mysterious, unknown guest,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who cries to me: "Remember Barmecide,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And tremble to be happy with the rest."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I make answer: "I am satisfied;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I dare not ask; I know not what is best;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;God hath already said what shall betide."
</pre>
