<pre>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I see amid the fields of Ayr
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A ploughman, who, in foul and fair,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sings at his task
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So clear, we know not if it is
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The laverock's song we hear, or his,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor care to ask.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For him the ploughing of those fields
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A more ethereal harvest yields
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Than sheaves of grain;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Songs flush with Purple bloom the rye,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The plover's call, the curlew's cry,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sing in his brain.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Touched by his hand, the wayside weed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Becomes a flower; the lowliest reed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beside the stream
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is clothed with beauty; gorse and grass
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And heather, where his footsteps pass,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The brighter seem.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He sings of love, whose flame illumes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The darkness of lone cottage rooms;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He feels the force,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The treacherous undertow and stress
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of wayward passions, and no less
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The keen remorse.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At moments, wrestling with his fate,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His voice is harsh, but not with hate;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The brushwood, hung
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Above the tavern door, lets fall
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Its bitter leaf, its drop of gall
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Upon his tongue.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But still the music of his song
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rises o'er all elate and strong;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Its master-chords
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are Manhood, Freedom, Brotherhood,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Its discords but an interlude
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Between the words.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And then to die so young and leave
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unfinished what he might achieve!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet better sure
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is this, than wandering up and down
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;An old man in a country town,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Infirm and poor.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For now he haunts his native land
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As an immortal youth; his hand
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Guides every plough;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He sits beside each ingle-nook,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His voice is in each rushing brook,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Each rustling bough.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His presence haunts this room to-night,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A form of mingled mist and light
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From that far coast.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Welcome beneath this roof of mine!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Welcome! this vacant chair is thine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dear guest and ghost!
</pre>
