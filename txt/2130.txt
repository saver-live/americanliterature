<p><span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>SHE's dead; and all which die<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>To their first elements resolve;<br> And we were mutual elements to us,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>And made of one another.<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>My body then doth hers involve,<br> And those things whereof I consist hereby<br> In me abundant grow, and burdenous,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>And nourish not, but smother.<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>My fire of passion, sighs of air,<br> Water of tears, and earthly sad despair,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:3em;">&nbsp;</span>Which my materials be,<br> But near worn out by love's security,<br> She, to my loss, doth by her death repair.<br> And I might live long wretched so,<br> But that my fire doth with my fuel grow.<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Now, as those active kings<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Whose foreign conquest treasure brings,<br> Receive more, and spend more, and soonest break,<br> This —which I am amazed that I can speak—<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>This death, hath with my store<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:3em;">&nbsp;</span>My use increased.<br> And so my soul, more earnestly released,<br> Will outstrip hers; as bullets flown before<br> A latter bullet may o'ertake, the powder being more.</p>
