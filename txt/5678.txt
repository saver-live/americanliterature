<pre>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come, old friend! sit down and listen!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the pitcher, placed between us,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How the waters laugh and glisten
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the head of old Silenus!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Old Silenus, bloated, drunken,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Led by his inebriate Satyrs;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On his breast his head is sunken,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vacantly he leers and chatters.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fauns with youthful Bacchus follow;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ivy crowns that brow supernal
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the forehead of Apollo,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And possessing youth eternal.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Round about him, fair Bacchantes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bearing cymbals, flutes, and thyrses,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wild from Naxian groves, or Zante's
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vineyards, sing delirious verses.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus he won, through all the nations,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bloodless victories, and the farmer
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bore, as trophies and oblations,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vines for banners, ploughs for armor.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Judged by no o'erzealous rigor,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Much this mystic throng expresses:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bacchus was the type of vigor,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Silenus of excesses.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These are ancient ethnic revels,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of a faith long since forsaken;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now the Satyrs, changed to devils,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Frighten mortals wine-o'ertaken.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now to rivulets from the mountains
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Point the rods of fortune-tellers;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Youth perpetual dwells in fountains,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not in flasks, and casks, and cellars.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Claudius, though he sang of flagons
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And huge tankards filled with Rhenish,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From that fiery blood of dragons
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never would his own replenish.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Even Redi, though he chaunted
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bacchus in the Tuscan valleys,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never drank the wine he vaunted
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In his dithyrambic sallies.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then with water fill the pitcher
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wreathed about with classic fables;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ne'er Falernian threw a richer
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Light upon Lucullus' tables.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come, old friend, sit down and listen
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As it passes thus between us,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How its wavelets laugh and glisten
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the head of old Silenus!
</pre>
