<p>TWICE or thrice had I loved thee,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Before I knew thy face or name;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>So in a voice, so in a shapeless flame<br> Angels affect us oft, and worshipp'd be.<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Still when, to where thou wert, I came,<br> Some lovely glorious nothing did I see.<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>But since my soul, whose child love is,<br> Takes limbs of flesh, and else could nothing do,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>More subtle than the parent is<br> Love must not be, but take a body too;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>And therefore what thou wert, and who,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>I bid Love ask, and now<br> That it assume thy body, I allow,<br> And fix itself in thy lip, eye, and brow.<br> <br> Whilst thus to ballast love I thought,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>And so more steadily to have gone,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>With wares which would sink admiration,<br> I saw I had love's pinnace overfraught;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Thy every hair for love to work upon<br> Is much too much; some fitter must be sought;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>For, nor in nothing, nor in things<br> Extreme, and scattering bright, can love inhere;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Then as an angel face and wings<br> Of air, not pure as it, yet pure doth wear,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>So thy love may be my love's sphere;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Just such disparity<br> As is 'twixt air's and angels' purity,<br> 'Twixt women's love, and men's, will ever be.</p>
