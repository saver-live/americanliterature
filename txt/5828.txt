<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;After so long an absence
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At last we meet again:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Does the meeting give us pleasure,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or does it give us pain?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The tree of life has been shaken,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And but few of us linger now,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the Prophet's two or three berries
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the top of the uppermost bough.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We cordially greet each other
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the old, familiar tone;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And we think, though we do not say it,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How old and gray he is grown!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We speak of a Merry Christmas
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And many a Happy New Year
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But each in his heart is thinking
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of those that are not here.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We speak of friends and their fortunes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And of what they did and said,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till the dead alone seem living,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the living alone seem dead.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And at last we hardly distinguish
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Between the ghosts and the guests;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And a mist and shadow of sadness
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Steals over our merriest jests.
</pre>
