<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I stand beneath the tree, whose branches shade
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy western window, Chapel of St. John!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And hear its leaves repeat their benison
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On him, whose hand if thy stones memorial laid;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then I remember one of whom was said
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the world's darkest hour, "Behold thy son!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And see him living still, and wandering on
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And waiting for the advent long delayed.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not only tongues of the apostles teach
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lessons of love and light, but these expanding
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And sheltering boughs with all their leaves implore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And say in language clear as human speech,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"The peace of God, that passeth understanding,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be and abide with you forevermore!"
</pre>
