<p>I pray to the ray from the window-pane –<br> It's pale, thin, and straight.<br> All morning I was silent,<br> My heart – split in two.<br> The copper of my wash-basin<br> Is green with verdigris,<br> But sunlight plays there,<br> How joyously.<br> So simple it is, so innocent,<br> In evening quiet,<br> Yet in this bare shrine,<br> It's a gold celebration,<br> A consolation, I find.<br></p>
