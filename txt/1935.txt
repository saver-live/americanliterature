<p><cite class="al-title">The Fortunes and Misfortunes of Moll Flanders</cite> was published in 1722, following Defoe's acclaimed novel, <cite class="al-title">Robinson Crusoe</cite> in 1719. It purports to be the true account of the life of Moll Flanders in the year 1683, and whose full title almost tells the whole story: <br><i>"The Fortunes and Misfortunes of the Famous Moll Flanders, &amp;c. Who was Born in Newgate, and during a Life of continu'd Variety for Threescore Years, besides her Childhood, was Twelve Year a Whore, five times a Wife (whereof once to her own Brother), Twelve Year a Thief, Eight Year a Transported Felon in Virginia, at last grew Rich, liv'd Honest, and died a Penitent. Written from her own Memorandums."</i></p><p><img alt="The Fortunes and Misfortunes of Moll Flanders title page" title="The Fortunes and Misfortunes of Moll Flanders title page" itemprop="image" src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/book/the-fortunes-and-misfortunes-of-moll-flanders-cover.jpg" style="max-width:400px; float:right; margin-right: 12px; margin-bottom: 10px;" data-pagespeed-url-hash="228353584" onload="pagespeed.CriticalImages.checkImageForCriticality(this);"> </p><p><a href="preface">Preface</a></p><p><a href="moll-flanders">Moll Flanders</a></p><p><a href="now-was-i-a-poor-gentlewoman-indeed">Now Was I a Poor Gentlewoman Indeed</a></p><p><a href="the-bare-loss-of-him-as-a-gallant">The Bare Loss of Him as a Gallant</a></p><p><a href="i-picked-out-my-man-without-much-difficulty">I Picked Out My Man Without Much Difficulty</a></p><p><a href="i-came-away-for-england-in-the-month-of-august">I Came Away For England in the Month of August</a></p><p><a href="that-alters-the-case-indeed-sir">That Alters the Case Indeed, Sir</a></p><p><a href="i-now-grew-big">I Now Grew Big</a></p><p><a href="tis-time-enough">'Tis Time Enough</a></p><p><a href="this-was-the-greatest-and-the-worst-prize">This Was the Greatest and the Worst Prize</a></p><p><a href="away-she-comes-to-me-and-tells-me-this-story">Away She Comes to Me and Tells Me This Story</a></p><p><a href="fortune-had-smiled-upon-me-to-that-degree">Fortune Had Smiled Upon Me to That Degree</a></p><p><a href="when-the-trial-began-the-indictment-was-read">When the Trial Began, the Indictment Was Read</a></p><p><a href="with-this-resolution-i-proposed-to-my-husband-our-going-away">With This Resolution I Proposed to My Husband Our Going Away</a></p><p style="margin-top: 26px;"> Return to the <a href="/author/daniel-defoe">Daniel Defoe</a> library. </p>
