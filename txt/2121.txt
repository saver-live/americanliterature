<p><span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>ALL kings, and all their favourites,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>All glory of honours, beauties, wits,<br> The sun it self, which makes time, as they pass,<br> Is elder by a year now than it was<br> When thou and I first one another saw.<br> All other things to their destruction draw,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Only our love hath no decay;<br> This no to-morrow hath, nor yesterday;<br> Running it never runs from us away,<br> But truly keeps his first, last, everlasting day.<br> <br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Two graves must hide thine and my corse;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>If one might, death were no divorce.<br> Alas! as well as other princes, we<br> —Who prince enough in one another be—<br> Must leave at last in death these eyes and ears,<br> Oft fed with true oaths, and with sweet salt tears;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>But souls where nothing dwells but love<br> —All other thoughts being inmates—then shall prove<br> This or a love increasèd there above,<br> When bodies to their graves, souls from their graves remove.<br> <br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>And then we shall be throughly blest;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>But now no more than all the rest.<br> Here upon earth we're kings, and none but we<br> Can be such kings, nor of such subjects be.<br> Who is so safe as we? where none can do<br> Treason to us, except one of us two.<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>True and false fears let us refrain,<br> Let us love nobly, and live, and add again<br> Years and years unto years, till we attain<br> To write threescore; this is the second of our reign.</p>
