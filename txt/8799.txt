<p>H.G. Wells' most notorious science fiction novel, <span class="al-title">The War of the Worlds</span> (1898) was first serialized in <cite class="al-periodical">Pearson's Magazine</cite> in England (1897), and <cite class="al-periodical">Cosmopolitan</cite> in the U.S. on October 30, 1938, the day before Halloween. The story was broadcast on <cite class="al-title">The Mercury Theatre on the Air</cite> radio show by <cite class="al-person">Orson Welles</cite>, spooking countless listeners who thought the martian invasion "coverage" was real.</p><p>This book is featured in our collection of <a href="/books-for-young-readers">Books for Young Readers</a>, <a href="/science-fiction-study-guide">Science Fiction Study Guide</a>, and <a href="/the-unreliable-narrator">The Unreliable Narrator</a>.</p><p><img alt="The War of the Worlds, cover" title="The War of the Worlds cover" itemprop="image" src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/book/the-war-of-the-worlds-cover.jpg" style="max-width:350px; float:right; margin-left: 12px; margin-bottom: 12px;" data-pagespeed-url-hash="3390052859" onload="pagespeed.CriticalImages.checkImageForCriticality(this);"><img alt="The War of the Worlds invasion" title="The War of the Worlds inasion" itemprop="image" src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/book/war-of-the-worlds.jpg" style="max-width:350px; float:right; margin-left: 12px; margin-bottom: 12px;" data-pagespeed-url-hash="3103455909" onload="pagespeed.CriticalImages.checkImageForCriticality(this);"> </p><p><a href="book-i-the-eve-of-the-war">Book I -
