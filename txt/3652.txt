<pre>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The night is freezing fast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To-morrow comes December;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And winterfalls of old
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are with me from the past;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And chiefly I remember
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How Dick would hate the cold.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fall, winter, fall; for he,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prompt hand and headpiece clever,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Has woven a winter robe,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And made of earth and sea
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His overcoat for ever,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And wears the turning globe.
</pre>
