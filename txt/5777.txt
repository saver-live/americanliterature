<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I heard a voice, that cried,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Balder the Beautiful
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is dead, is dead!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And through the misty air
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Passed like the mournful cry
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of sunward sailing cranes.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I saw the pallid corpse
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the dead sun
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Borne through the Northern sky.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blasts from Niffelheim
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lifted the sheeted mists
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Around him as he passed.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the voice forever cried,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Balder the Beautiful
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is dead, is dead!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And died away
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through the dreary night,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In accents of despair.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Balder the Beautiful,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;God of the summer sun,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fairest of all the Gods!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Light from his forehead beamed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Runes were upon his tongue,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As on the warrior's sword.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All things in earth and air
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bound were by magic spell
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never to do him harm;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Even the plants and stones;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All save the mistletoe,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sacred mistletoe!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hoeder, the blind old God,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose feet are shod with silence,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pierced through that gentle breast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With his sharp spear, by fraud
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Made of the mistletoe,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The accursed mistletoe!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They laid him in his ship,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With horse and harness,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As on a funeral pyre.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Odin placed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A ring upon his finger,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And whispered in his ear.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They launched the burning ship!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It floated far away
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over the misty sea,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till like the sun it seemed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sinking beneath the waves.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Balder returned no more!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So perish the old Gods!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But out of the sea of Time
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rises a new land of song,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fairer than the old.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over its meadows green
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Walk the young bards and sing.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Build it again,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O ye bards,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fairer than before!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ye fathers of the new race,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Feed upon morning dew,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sing the new Song of Love!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The law of force is dead!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The law of love prevails!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thor, the thunderer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shall rule the earth no more,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No more, with threats,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Challenge the meek Christ.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sing no more,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O ye bards of the North,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of Vikings and of Jarls!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the days of Eld
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Preserve the freedom only,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not the deeds of blood!
</pre>
