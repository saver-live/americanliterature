&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;THE CHALLENGE OF THOR

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am the God Thor,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am the War God,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am the Thunderer!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here in my Northland,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My fastness and fortress,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Reign I forever!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here amid icebergs
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rule I the nations;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This is my hammer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Miolner the mighty;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Giants and sorcerers
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cannot withstand it!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These are the gauntlets
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wherewith I wield it,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And hurl it afar off;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This is my girdle;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whenever I brace it,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Strength is redoubled!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The light thou beholdest
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stream through the heavens,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In flashes of crimson,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is but my red beard
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blown by the night-wind,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Affrighting the nations!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Jove is my brother;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mine eyes are the lightning;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The wheels of my chariot
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Roll in the thunder,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The blows of my hammer
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ring in the earthquake!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Force rules the world still,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Has ruled it, shall rule it;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Meekness is weakness,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Strength is triumphant,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over the whole earth
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Still is it Thor's-Day!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou art a God too,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O Galilean!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And thus single-handed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unto the combat,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gauntlet or Gospel,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here I defy thee!



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;II

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;KING OLAF'S RETURN

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And King Olaf heard the cry,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Saw the red light in the sky,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Laid his hand upon his sword,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As he leaned upon the railing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And his ships went sailing, sailing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Northward into Drontheim fiord.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There he stood as one who dreamed;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the red light glanced and gleamed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the armor that he wore;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And he shouted, as the rifled
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Streamers o'er him shook and shifted,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I accept thy challenge, Thor!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To avenge his father slain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And reconquer realm and reign,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Came the youthful Olaf home,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through the midnight sailing, sailing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Listening to the wild wind's wailing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the dashing of the foam.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To his thoughts the sacred name
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of his mother Astrid came,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the tale she oft had told
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of her flight by secret passes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through the mountains and morasses,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the home of Hakon old.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then strange memories crowded back
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of Queen Gunhild's wrath and wrack,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And a hurried flight by sea;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of grim Vikings, and the rapture
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the sea-fight, and the capture,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the life of slavery.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How a stranger watched his face
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the Esthonian market-place,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Scanned his features one by one,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Saying, "We should know each other;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am Sigurd, Astrid's brother,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou art Olaf, Astrid's son!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then as Queen Allogia's page,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Old in honors, young in age,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chief of all her men-at-arms;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till vague whispers, and mysterious,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Reached King Valdemar, the imperious,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Filling him with strange alarms.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then his cruisings o'er the seas,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Westward to the Hebrides,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And to Scilly's rocky shore;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the hermit's cavern dismal,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Christ's great name and rites baptismal
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;in the ocean's rush and roar.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All these thoughts of love and strife
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Glimmered through his lurid life,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the stars' intenser light
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through the red flames o'er him trailing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As his ships went sailing, sailing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Northward in the summer night.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Trained for either camp or court,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Skilful in each manly sport,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Young and beautiful and tall;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Art of warfare, craft of chases,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Swimming, skating, snow-shoe races
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Excellent alike in all.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When at sea, with all his rowers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He along the bending oars
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Outside of his ship could run.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He the Smalsor Horn ascended,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And his shining shield suspended,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On its summit, like a sun.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the ship-rails he could stand,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wield his sword with either hand,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And at once two javelins throw;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At all feasts where ale was strongest
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sat the merry monarch longest,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;First to come and last to go.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Norway never yet had seen
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One so beautiful of mien,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One so royal in attire,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When in arms completely furnished,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Harness gold-inlaid and burnished,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mantle like a flame of fire.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus came Olaf to his own,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When upon the night-wind blown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Passed that cry along the shore;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And he answered, while the rifted
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Streamers o'er him shook and shifted,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I accept thy challenge, Thor!"



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;III

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;THORA OF RIMOL

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Thora of Rimol! hide me! hide me!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Danger and shame and death betide me!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For Olaf the King is hunting me down
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through field and forest, through thorp and town!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus cried Jarl Hakon
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To Thora, the fairest of women.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hakon Jarl! for the love I bear thee
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Neither shall shame nor death come near thee!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the hiding-place wherein thou must lie
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is the cave underneath the swine in the sty."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus to Jarl Hakon
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Said Thora, the fairest of women.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So Hakon Jarl and his base thrall Karker
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crouched in the cave, than a dungeon darker,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As Olaf came riding, with men in mail,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through the forest roads into Orkadale,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Demanding Jarl Hakon
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of Thorn, the fairest of women.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Rich and honored shall be whoever
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The head of Hakon Jarl shall dissever!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hakon heard him, and Karker the slave,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through the breathing-holes of the darksome cave.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alone in her chamber
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wept Thora, the fairest of women.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Said Karker, the crafty, "I will not slay thee!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For all the king's gold I will never betray thee!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Then why dost thou turn so pale, O churl,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And then again black as the earth?" said the Earl.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;More pale and more faithful
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was Thora, the fairest of women.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From a dream in the night the thrall started, saying,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Round my neck a gold ring King Olaf was laying!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Hakon answered, "Beware of the king!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He will lay round thy neck a blood-red ring."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At the ring on her finger
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gazed Thorn, the fairest of women.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At daybreak slept Hakon, with sorrows encumbered,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But screamed and drew up his feet as he slumbered;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The thrall in the darkness plunged with his knife,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the Earl awakened no more in this life.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But wakeful and weeping
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sat Thorn, the fairest of women.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At Nidarholm the priests are all singing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Two ghastly heads on the gibbet are swinging;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One is Jarl Hakon's and one is his thrall's,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the people are shouting from windows and walls;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While alone in her chamber
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Swoons Thorn, the fairest of women.



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;IV

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;QUEEN SIGRID THE HAUGHTY

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Queen Sigrid the Haughty sat proud and aloft
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In her chamber, that looked over meadow and croft.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Heart's dearest,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why dost thou sorrow so?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The floor with tassels of fir was besprent,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Filling the room with their fragrant scent.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She heard the birds sing, she saw the sun shine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The air of summer was sweeter than wine.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a sword without scabbard the bright river lay
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Between her own kingdom and Norroway.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But Olaf the King had sued for her hand,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sword would be sheathed, the river be spanned.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Her maidens were seated around her knee,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Working bright figures in tapestry.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And one was singing the ancient rune
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of Brynhilda's love and the wrath of Gudrun.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And through it, and round it, and over it all
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sounded incessant the waterfall.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Queen in her hand held a ring of gold,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the door of Lade's Temple old.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;King Olaf had sent her this wedding gift,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But her thoughts as arrows were keen and swift.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She had given the ring to her goldsmiths twain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who smiled, as they handed it back again.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Sigrid the Queen, in her haughty way,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Said, "Why do you smile, my goldsmiths, say?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And they answered: "O Queen! if the truth must be told,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The ring is of copper, and not of gold!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The lightning flashed o'er her forehead and cheek,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She only murmured, she did not speak:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"If in his gifts he can faithless be,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There will be no gold in his love to me."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A footstep was heard on the outer stair,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And in strode King Olaf with royal air.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He kissed the Queen's hand, and he whispered of love,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And swore to be true as the stars are above.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But she smiled with contempt as she answered: "O King,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Will you swear it, as Odin once swore, on the ring?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the King: "O speak not of Odin to me,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The wife of King Olaf a Christian must be."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Looking straight at the King, with her level brows,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She said, "I keep true to my faith and my vows."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then the face of King Olaf was darkened with gloom,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He rose in his anger and strode through the room.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Why, then, should I care to have thee?" he said,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"A faded old woman, a heathenish jade!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His zeal was stronger than fear or love,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And he struck the Queen in the face with his glove.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then forth from the chamber in anger he fled,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the wooden stairway shook with his tread.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Queen Sigrid the Haughty said under her breath,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"This insult, King Olaf, shall be thy death!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Heart's dearest,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why dost thou sorrow so?



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;V

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;THE SKERRY OF SHRIEKS

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now from all King Olaf's farms
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His men-at-arms
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gathered on the Eve of Easter;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To his house at Angvalds-ness
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fast they press,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drinking with the royal feaster.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Loudly through the wide-flung door
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Came the roar
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the sea upon the Skerry;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And its thunder loud and near
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Reached the ear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mingling with their voices merry.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Hark!" said Olaf to his Scald,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Halfred the Bald,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Listen to that song, and learn it!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Half my kingdom would I give,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As I live,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If by such songs you would earn it!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"For of all the runes and rhymes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of all times,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Best I like the ocean's dirges,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When the old harper heaves and rocks,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His hoary locks
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flowing and flashing in the surges!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Halfred answered: "I am called
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Unappalled!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nothing hinders me or daunts me.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hearken to me, then, O King,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While I sing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The great Ocean Song that haunts me."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I will hear your song sublime
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some other time,"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Says the drowsy monarch, yawning,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And retires; each laughing guest
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Applauds the jest;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then they sleep till day is dawning.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Facing up and down the yard,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;King Olaf's guard
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Saw the sea-mist slowly creeping
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er the sands, and up the hill,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gathering still
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Round the house where they were sleeping.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was not the fog he saw,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor misty flaw,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That above the landscape brooded;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was Eyvind Kallda's crew
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of warlocks blue
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With their caps of darkness hooded!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Round and round the house they go,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Weaving slow
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Magic circles to encumber
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And imprison in their ring
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Olaf the King,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As he helpless lies in slumber.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then athwart the vapors dun
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Easter sun
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Streamed with one broad track of splendor!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;in their real forms appeared
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The warlocks weird,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Awful as the Witch of Endor.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blinded by the light that glared,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They groped and stared
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Round about with steps unsteady;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From his window Olaf gazed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, amazed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Who are these strange people?" said he.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Eyvind Kallda and his men!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Answered then
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the yard a sturdy farmer;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While the men-at-arms apace
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Filled the place,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Busily buckling on their armor.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the gates they sallied forth,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;South and north,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Scoured the island coast around them,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seizing all the warlock band,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Foot and hand
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the Skerry's rocks they bound them.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And at eve the king again
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Called his train,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, with all the candles burning,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Silent sat and heard once more
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sullen roar
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the ocean tides returning.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shrieks and cries of wild despair
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Filled the air,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Growing fainter as they listened;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then the bursting surge alone
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sounded on;--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus the sorcerers were christened!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Sing, O Scald, your song sublime,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Your ocean-rhyme,"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cried King Olaf: "it will cheer me!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Said the Scald, with pallid cheeks,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"The Skerry of Shrieks
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sings too loud for you to hear me!"



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;VI

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;THE WRAITH OF ODIN

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The guests were loud, the ale was strong,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;King Olaf feasted late and long;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The hoary Scalds together sang;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'erhead the smoky rafters rang.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dead rides Sir Morten of Fogelsang.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The door swung wide, with creak and din;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A blast of cold night-air came in,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And on the threshold shivering stood
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A one-eyed guest, with cloak and hood.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dead rides Sir Morten of Fogelsang.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The King exclaimed, "O graybeard pale!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come warm thee with this cup of ale."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The foaming draught the old man quaffed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The noisy guests looked on and laughed.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dead rides Sir Morten of Fogelsang.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then spake the King: "Be not afraid;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sit here by me."&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The guest obeyed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, seated at the table, told
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tales of the sea, and Sagas old.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dead rides Sir Morten of Fogelsang.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And ever, when the tale was o'er,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The King demanded yet one more;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till Sigurd the Bishop smiling said,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"'T is late, O King, and time for bed."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dead rides Sir Morten of Fogelsang.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The King retired; the stranger guest
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Followed and entered with the rest;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The lights were out, the pages gone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But still the garrulous guest spake on.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dead rides Sir Morten of Fogelsang.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As one who from a volume reads,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He spake of heroes and their deeds,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of lands and cities he had seen,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And stormy gulfs that tossed between.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dead rides Sir Morten of Fogelsang.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then from his lips in music rolled
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Havamal of Odin old,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With sounds mysterious as the roar
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of billows on a distant shore.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dead rides Sir Morten of Fogelsang.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Do we not learn from runes and rhymes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Made by the gods in elder times,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And do not still the great Scalds teach
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That silence better is than speech?"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dead rides Sir Morten of Fogelsang.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Smiling at this, the King replied,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Thy lore is by thy tongue belied;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For never was I so enthralled
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Either by Saga-man or Scald,"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dead rides Sir Morten of Fogelsang.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Bishop said, "Late hours we keep!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Night wanes, O King! 't is time for sleep!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then slept the King, and when he woke
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The guest was gone, the morning broke.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dead rides Sir Morten of Fogelsang.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They found the doors securely barred,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They found the watch-dog in the yard,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There was no footprint in the grass,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And none had seen the stranger pass.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dead rides Sir Morten of Fogelsang.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;King Olaf crossed himself and said:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I know that Odin the Great is dead;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sure is the triumph of our Faith,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The one-eyed stranger was his wraith."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dead rides Sir Morten of Fogelsang.



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;VII

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;IRON-BEARD

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Olaf the King, one summer morn,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blew a blast on his bugle-horn,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sending his signal through the land of Drontheim.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And to the Hus-Ting held at Mere
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gathered the farmers far and near,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With their war weapons ready to confront him.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ploughing under the morning star,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Old Iron-Beard in Yriar
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Heard the summons, chuckling with a low laugh.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He wiped the sweat-drops from his brow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unharnessed his horses from the plough,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And clattering came on horseback to King Olaf.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He was the churliest of the churls;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Little he cared for king or earls;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bitter as home-brewed ale were his foaming passions.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hodden-gray was the garb he wore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And by the Hammer of Thor he swore;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He hated the narrow town, and all its fashions.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But he loved the freedom of his farm,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His ale at night, by the fireside warm,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gudrun his daughter, with her flaxen tresses.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He loved his horses and his herds,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The smell of the earth, and the song of birds,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His well-filled barns, his brook with its water-cresses.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Huge and cumbersome was his frame;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His beard, from which he took his name,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Frosty and fierce, like that of Hymer the Giant.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So at the Hus-Ting he appeared,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The farmer of Yriar, Iron-Beard,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On horseback, in an attitude defiant.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And to King Olaf he cried aloud,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Out of the middle of the crowd,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That tossed about him like a stormy ocean:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Such sacrifices shalt thou bring;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To Odin and to Thor, O King,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As other kings have done in their devotion!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;King Olaf answered: "I command
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This land to be a Christian land;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here is my Bishop who the folk baptizes!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"But if you ask me to restore
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Your sacrifices, stained with gore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then will I offer human sacrifices!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Not slaves and peasants shall they be,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But men of note and high degree,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Such men as Orm of Lyra and Kar of Gryting!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Then to their Temple strode he in,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; And loud behind him heard the din
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of his men-at-arms and the peasants fiercely fighting.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There in the Temple, carved in wood,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The image of great Odin stood,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And other gods, with Thor supreme among them.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;King Olaf smote them with the blade
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of his huge war-axe, gold inlaid,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And downward shattered to the pavement flung them.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At the same moment rose without,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the contending crowd, a shout,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A mingled sound of triumph and of wailing.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And there upon the trampled plain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The farmer iron-Beard lay slain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Midway between the assailed and the assailing.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;King Olaf from the doorway spoke.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Choose ye between two things, my folk,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To be baptized or given up to slaughter!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And seeing their leader stark and dead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The people with a murmur said,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"O King, baptize us with thy holy water";

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So all the Drontheim land became
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A Christian land in name and fame,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the old gods no more believing and trusting.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And as a blood-atonement, soon
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;King Olaf wed the fair Gudrun;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And thus in peace ended the Drontheim Hus-Ting!



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;VIII

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;GUDRUN

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On King Olaf's bridal night
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shines the moon with tender light,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And across the chamber streams
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Its tide of dreams.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At the fatal midnight hour,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When all evil things have power,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the glimmer of the moon
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stands Gudrun.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Close against her heaving breast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Something in her hand is pressed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like an icicle, its sheen
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is cold and keen.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the cairn are fixed her eyes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where her murdered father lies,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And a voice remote and drear
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She seems to hear.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What a bridal night is this!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cold will be the dagger's kiss;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Laden with the chill of death
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is its breath.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the drifting snow she sweeps
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the couch where Olaf sleeps;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suddenly he wakes and stirs,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His eyes meet hers.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"What is that," King Olaf said,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Gleams so bright above thy head?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wherefore standest thou so white
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In pale moonlight?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"'T is the bodkin that I wear
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When at night I bind my hair;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It woke me falling on the floor;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T is nothing more."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Forests have ears, and fields have eyes;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Often treachery lurking lies
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Underneath the fairest hair!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gudrun beware!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ere the earliest peep of morn
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blew King Olaf's bugle-horn;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And forever sundered ride
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bridegroom and bride!



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;IX

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;THANGBRAND THE PRIEST

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Short of stature, large of limb,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Burly face and russet beard,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All the women stared at him,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When in Iceland he appeared.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Look!" they said,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With nodding head,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"There goes Thangbrand, Olaf's Priest."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All the prayers he knew by rote,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He could preach like Chrysostome,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the Fathers he could quote,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He had even been at Rome,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A learned clerk,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A man of mark,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was this Thangbrand, Olaf's Priest,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He was quarrelsome and loud,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And impatient of control,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Boisterous in the market crowd,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Boisterous at the wassail-bowl,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Everywhere
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Would drink and swear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Swaggering Thangbrand, Olaf's Priest

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In his house this malcontent
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Could the King no longer bear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So to Iceland he was sent
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To convert the heathen there,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And away
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One summer day
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sailed this Thangbrand, Olaf's Priest.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There in Iceland, o'er their books
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pored the people day and night,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But he did not like their looks,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor the songs they used to write.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"All this rhyme
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is waste of time!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Grumbled Thangbrand, Olaf's Priest.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the alehouse, where he sat
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Came the Scalds and Saga-men;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is it to be wondered at,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That they quarrelled now and then,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When o'er his beer
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Began to leer
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drunken Thangbrand, Olaf's Priest?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All the folk in Altafiord
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Boasted of their island grand;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Saying in a single word,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Iceland is the finest land
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That the sun
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Doth shine upon!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Loud laughed Thangbrand, Olaf's Priest.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And he answered: "What's the use
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of this bragging up and down,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When three women and one goose
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Make a market in your town!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Every Scald
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Satires scrawled
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On poor Thangbrand, Olaf's Priest.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Something worse they did than that;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And what vexed him most of all
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was a figure in shovel hat,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drawn in charcoal on the wall;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With words that go
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sprawling below,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"This is Thangbrand, Olaf's Priest."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hardly knowing what he did,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then he smote them might and main,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thorvald Veile and Veterlid
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lay there in the alehouse slain.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"To-day we are gold,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To-morrow mould!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Muttered Thangbrand, Olaf's Priest.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Much in fear of axe and rope,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Back to Norway sailed he then.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"O, King Olaf! little hope
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is there of these Iceland men!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Meekly said,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With bending head,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pious Thangbrand, Olaf's Priest.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;X

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;RAUD THE STRONG

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"All the old gods are dead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All the wild warlocks fled;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the White Christ lives and reigns,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And throughout my wide domains
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His Gospel shall be spread!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the Evangelists
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus swore King Olaf.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But still in dreams of the night
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beheld he the crimson light,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And heard the voice that defied
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Him who was crucified,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And challenged him to the fight.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To Sigurd the Bishop
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;King Olaf confessed it.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Sigurd the Bishop said,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"The old gods are not dead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For the great Thor still reigns,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And among the Jarls and Thanes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The old witchcraft still is spread."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus to King Olaf
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Said Sigurd the Bishop.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Far north in the Salten Fiord,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By rapine, fire, and sword,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lives the Viking, Raud the Strong;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All the Godoe Isles belong
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To him and his heathen horde."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Thus went on speaking
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Sigurd the Bishop.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"A warlock, a wizard is he,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And lord of the wind and the sea;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And whichever way he sails,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He has ever favoring gales,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By his craft in sorcery."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here the sign of the cross
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Made devoutly King Olaf.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"With rites that we both abhor,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He worships Odin and Thor;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So it cannot yet be said,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That all the old gods are dead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the warlocks are no more,"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flushing with anger
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Said Sigurd the Bishop.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then King Olaf cried aloud:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I will talk with this mighty Raud,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And along the Salten Fiord
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Preach the Gospel with my sword,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or be brought back in my shroud!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So northward from Drontheim
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sailed King Olaf!



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;XI

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;BISHOP SIGURD AT SALTEN FIORD

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Loud the angry wind was wailing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As King Olaf's ships came sailing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Northward out of Drontheim haven
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; To the mouth of Salten Fiord.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Though the flying sea-spray drenches
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fore and aft the rowers' benches,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not a single heart is craven
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the champions there on board.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All without the Fiord was quiet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But within it storm and riot,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Such as on his Viking cruises
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Raud the Strong was wont to ride.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the sea through all its tide-ways
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Swept the reeling vessels sideways,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the leaves are swept through sluices,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When the flood-gates open wide.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"'T is the warlock! 't is the demon
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Raud!" cried Sigurd to the seamen;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"But the Lord is not affrighted
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the witchcraft of his foes."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the ship's bow he ascended,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By his choristers attended,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Round him were the tapers lighted,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the sacred incense rose.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the bow stood Bishop Sigurd,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In his robes, as one transfigured,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the Crucifix he planted
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;High amid the rain and mist.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then with holy water sprinkled
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All the ship; the mass-bells tinkled;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Loud the monks around him chanted,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Loud he read the Evangelist.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As into the Fiord they darted,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On each side the water parted;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Down a path like silver molten
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Steadily rowed King Olaf's ships;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Steadily burned all night the tapers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the White Christ through the vapors
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gleamed across the Fiord of Salten,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As through John's Apocalypse,--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till at last they reached Raud's dwelling
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the little isle of Gelling;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not a guard was at the doorway,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not a glimmer of light was seen.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But at anchor, carved and gilded,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lay the dragon-ship he builded;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T was the grandest ship in Norway,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With its crest and scales of green.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Up the stairway, softly creeping,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the loft where Raud was sleeping,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With their fists they burst asunder
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bolt and bar that held the door.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drunken with sleep and ale they found him,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dragged him from his bed and bound him,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While he stared with stupid wonder,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At the look and garb they wore.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then King Olaf said: "O Sea-King!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Little time have we for speaking,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Choose between the good and evil;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be baptized, or thou shalt die!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But in scorn the heathen scoffer
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Answered: "I disdain thine offer;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Neither fear I God nor Devil;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thee and thy Gospel I defy!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then between his jaws distended,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When his frantic struggles ended,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through King Olaf's horn an adder,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Touched by fire, they forced to glide.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sharp his tooth was as an arrow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As he gnawed through bone and marrow;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But without a groan or shudder,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Raud the Strong blaspheming died.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then baptized they all that region,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Swarthy Lap and fair Norwegian,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Far as swims the salmon, leaping,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Up the streams of Salten Fiord.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In their temples Thor and Odin
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lay in dust and ashes trodden,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As King Olaf, onward sweeping,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Preached the Gospel with his sword.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then he took the carved and gilded
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dragon-ship that Raud had builded,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the tiller single-handed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Grasping, steered into the main.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Southward sailed the sea-gulls o'er him,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Southward sailed the ship that bore him,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till at Drontheim haven landed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Olaf and his crew again.




&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;XII

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;KING OLAF'S CHRISTMAS

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At Drontheim, Olaf the King
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Heard the bells of Yule-tide ring,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As he sat in his banquet-hall,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drinking the nut-brown ale,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With his bearded Berserks hale
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And tall.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Three days his Yule-tide feasts
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He held with Bishops and Priests,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And his horn filled up to the brim;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the ale was never too strong,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor the Saga-man's tale too long,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For him.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er his drinking-horn, the sign
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He made of the cross divine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As he drank, and muttered his prayers;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the Berserks evermore
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Made the sign of the Hammer of Thor
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over theirs.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The gleams of the fire-light dance
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Upon helmet and hauberk and lance,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And laugh in the eyes of the King;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And he cries to Halfred the Scald,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gray-bearded, wrinkled, and bald,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Sing!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Sing me a song divine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a sword in every line,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And this shall be thy reward."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And he loosened the belt at his waist,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And in front of the singer placed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His sword.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Quern-biter of Hakon the Good,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wherewith at a stroke he hewed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The millstone through and through,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Foot-breadth of Thoralf the Strong,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Were neither so broad nor so long,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor so true."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then the Scald took his harp and sang,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And loud though the music rang
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sound of that shining word;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the harp-strings a clangor made,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As if they were struck with the blade
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of a sword.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the Berserks round about
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Broke forth into a shout
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That made the rafters ring:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They smote with their fists on the board,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And shouted, "Long live the Sword,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the King!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the King said, "O my son,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I miss the bright word in one
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of thy measures and thy rhymes."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Halfred the Scald replied,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"In another 't was multiplied
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Three times."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then King Olaf raised the hilt
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of iron, cross-shaped and gilt,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And said, "Do not refuse;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Count well the gain and the loss,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thor's hammer or Christ's cross:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Choose!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Halfred the Scald said, "This
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the name of the Lord I kiss,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who on it was crucified!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And a shout went round the board,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"In the name of Christ the Lord,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who died!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then over the waste of snows
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The noonday sun uprose,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through the driving mists revealed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the lifting of the Host,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By incense-clouds almost
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Concealed.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the shining wall a vast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And shadowy cross was cast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the hilt of the lifted sword,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And in foaming cups of ale
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Berserks drank "Was-hael!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the Lord!"



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;XIII

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;THE BUILDING OF THE LONG SERPENT

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thorberg Skafting, master-builder,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In his ship-yard by the sea,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whistling, said, "It would bewilder
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Any man but Thorberg Skafting,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Any man but me!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Near him lay the Dragon stranded,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Built of old by Raud the Strong,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And King Olaf had commanded
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He should build another Dragon,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Twice as large and long.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Therefore whistled Thorberg Skafting,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As he sat with half-closed eyes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And his head turned sideways, drafting
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That new vessel for King Olaf
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Twice the Dragon's size.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Round him busily hewed and hammered
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mallet huge and heavy axe;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Workmen laughed and sang and clamored;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whirred the wheels, that into rigging
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Spun the shining flax!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All this tumult heard the master,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was music to his ear;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fancy whispered all the faster,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Men shall hear of Thorberg Skafting
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For a hundred year!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Workmen sweating at the forges
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fashioned iron bolt and bar,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a warlock's midnight orgies
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Smoked and bubbled the black caldron
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the boiling tar.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Did the warlocks mingle in it,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thorberg Skafting, any curse?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Could you not be gone a minute
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But some mischief must be doing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Turning bad to worse?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T was an ill wind that came wafting,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From his homestead words of woe
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To his farm went Thorberg Skafting,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Oft repeating to his workmen,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Build ye thus and so.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;After long delays returning
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Came the master back by night
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To his ship-yard longing, yearning,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hurried he, and did not leave it
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till the morning's light.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Come and see my ship, my darling"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the morrow said the King;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Finished now from keel to carling;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never yet was seen in Norway
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Such a wondrous thing!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the ship-yard, idly talking,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At the ship the workmen stared:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some one, all their labor balking,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Down her sides had cut deep gashes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not a plank was spared!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Death be to the evil-doer!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With an oath King Olaf spoke;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"But rewards to his pursuer
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And with wrath his face grew redder
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Than his scarlet cloak.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Straight the master-builder, smiling,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Answered thus the angry King:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Cease blaspheming and reviling,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Olaf, it was Thorberg Skafting
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who has done this thing!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then he chipped and smoothed the planking,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till the King, delighted, swore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With much lauding and much thanking,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Handsomer is now my Dragon
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Than she was before!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seventy ells and four extended
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the grass the vessel's keel;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;High above it, gilt and splendid,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rose the figure-head ferocious
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With its crest of steel.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then they launched her from the tressels,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the ship-yard by the sea;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She was the grandest of all vessels,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never ship was built in Norway
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Half so fine as she!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Long Serpent was she christened,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'Mid the roar of cheer on cheer!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They who to the Saga listened
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Heard the name of Thorberg Skafting
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For a hundred year!



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;XIV

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;THE CREW OF THE LONG SERPENT

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Safe at anchor in Drontheim bay
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;King Olaf's fleet assembled lay,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, striped with white and blue,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Downward fluttered sail and banner,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As alights the screaming lanner;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lustily cheered, in their wild manner,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Long Serpent's crew

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Her forecastle man was Ulf the Red,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a wolf's was his shaggy head,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His teeth as large and white;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His beard, of gray and russet blended,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Round as a swallow's nest descended;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As standard-bearer he defended
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Olaf's flag in the fight.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Near him Kolbiorn had his place,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the King in garb and face,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So gallant and so hale;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Every cabin-boy and varlet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wondered at his cloak of scarlet;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a river, frozen and star-lit,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gleamed his coat of mail.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the bulkhead, tall and dark,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stood Thrand Rame of Thelemark,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A figure gaunt and grand;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On his hairy arm imprinted
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was an anchor, azure-tinted;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like Thor's hammer, huge and dinted
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was his brawny hand.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Einar Tamberskelver, bare
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the winds his golden hair,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the mainmast stood;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Graceful was his form, and slender,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And his eyes were deep and tender
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As a woman's, in the splendor
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of her maidenhood.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the fore-hold Biorn and Bork
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Watched the sailors at their work:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Heavens! how they swore!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thirty men they each commanded,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Iron-sinewed, horny-handed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shoulders broad, and chests expanded.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Tugging at the oar.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These, and many more like these,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With King Olaf sailed the seas,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till the waters vast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Filled them with a vague devotion,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the freedom and the motion,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the roll and roar of ocean
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the sounding blast.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When they landed from the fleet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How they roared through Drontheim's street,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Boisterous as the gale!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How they laughed and stamped and pounded,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till the tavern roof resounded,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the host looked on astounded
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As they drank the ale!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never saw the wild North Sea
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Such a gallant company
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sail its billows blue!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never, while they cruised and quarrelled,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Old King Gorm, or Blue-Tooth Harald,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Owned a ship so well apparelled,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Boasted such a crew!



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;XV

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A LITTLE BIRD IN THE AIR

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A little bird in the air
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is singing of Thyri the fair,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sister of Svend the Dane;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the song of the garrulous bird
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the streets of the town is heard,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And repeated again and again.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hoist up your sails of silk,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And flee away from each other.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To King Burislaf, it is said,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was the beautiful Thyri wed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And a sorrowful bride went she;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And after a week and a day,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She has fled away and away,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From his town by the stormy sea.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hoist up your sails of silk,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And flee away from each other.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They say, that through heat and through cold,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through weald, they say, and through wold,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By day and by night, they say,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She has fled; and the gossips report
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She has come to King Olaf's court,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the town is all in dismay.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hoist up your sails of silk,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And flee away from each other.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is whispered King Olaf has seen,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Has talked with the beautiful Queen;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And they wonder how it will end;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For surely, if here she remain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is war with King Svend the Dane,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And King Burislaf the Vend!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hoist up your sails of silk,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And flee away from each other.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O, greatest wonder of all!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is published in hamlet and hall,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It roars like a flame that is fanned!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The King--yes, Olaf the King--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Has wedded her with his ring,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Thyri is Queen in the land!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hoist up your sails of silk,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And flee away from each other.



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;XVI

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;QUEEN THYRI AND THE ANGELICA STALKS

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Northward over Drontheim,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flew the clamorous sea-gulls,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sang the lark and linnet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the meadows green;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Weeping in her chamber,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lonely and unhappy,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sat the Drottning Thyri,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sat King Olaf's Queen.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In at all the windows
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Streamed the pleasant sunshine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the roof above her
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Softly cooed the dove;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the sound she heard not,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor the sunshine heeded,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For the thoughts of Thyri
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Were not thoughts of love,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then King Olaf entered,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beautiful as morning,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the sun at Easter
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shone his happy face;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In his hand he carried
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Angelicas uprooted,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With delicious fragrance
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Filling all the place.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a rainy midnight
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sat the Drottning Thyri,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Even the smile of Olaf
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Could not cheer her gloom;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor the stalks he gave her
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a gracious gesture,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And with words as pleasant
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As their own perfume.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In her hands he placed them,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And her jewelled fingers
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through the green leaves glistened
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the dews of morn;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But she cast them from her,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Haughty and indignant,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the floor she threw them
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a look of scorn.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Richer presents," said she,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Gave King Harald Gormson
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the Queen, my mother,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Than such worthless weeds;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"When he ravaged Norway,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Laying waste the kingdom,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seizing scatt and treasure
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For her royal needs.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"But thou darest not venture
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through the Sound to Vendland,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My domains to rescue
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From King Burislaf;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Lest King Svend of Denmark,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Forked Beard, my brother,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Scatter all thy vessels
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the wind the chaff."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then up sprang King Olaf,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a reindeer bounding,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With an oath he answered
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus the luckless Queen:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Never yet did Olaf
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fear King Svend of Denmark;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This right hand shall hale him
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By his forked chin!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then he left the chamber,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thundering through the doorway,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Loud his steps resounded
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Down the outer stair.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Smarting with the insult,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through the streets of Drontheim
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Strode he red and wrathful,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With his stately air.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All his ships he gathered,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Summoned all his forces,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Making his war levy
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the region round;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Down the coast of Norway,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a flock of sea-gulls,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sailed the fleet of Olaf
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through the Danish Sound.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With his own hand fearless,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Steered he the Long Serpent,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Strained the creaking cordage,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bent each boom and gaff;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till in Venland landing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The domains of Thyri
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He redeemed and rescued
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From King Burislaf.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then said Olaf, laughing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Not ten yoke of oxen
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Have the power to draw us
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a woman's hair!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Now will I confess it,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Better things are jewels
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Than angelica stalks are
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For a Queen to wear."



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;XVII

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;KING SVEND OF THE FORKED BEAR

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Loudly the sailors cheered
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Svend of the Forked Beard,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As with his fleet he steered
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Southward to Vendland;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where with their courses hauled
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All were together called,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Under the Isle of Svald
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Near to the mainland.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;After Queen Gunhild's death,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So the old Saga saith,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Plighted King Svend his faith
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To Sigrid the Haughty;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And to avenge his bride,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Soothing her wounded pride,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over the waters wide
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;King Olaf sought he.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Still on her scornful face,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blushing with deep disgrace,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bore she the crimson trace
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of Olaf's gauntlet;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a malignant star,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blazing in heaven afar,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Red shone the angry scar
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Under her frontlet.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Oft to King Svend she spake,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"For thine own honor's sake
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shalt thou swift vengeance take
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the vile coward!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Until the King at last,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gusty and overcast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a tempestuous blast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Threatened and lowered.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Soon as the Spring appeared,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Svend of the Forked Beard
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;High his red standard reared,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Eager for battle;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While every warlike Dane,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seizing his arms again,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Left all unsown the grain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unhoused the cattle.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Likewise the Swedish King
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Summoned in haste a Thing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Weapons and men to bring
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In aid of Denmark;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Erie the Norseman, too,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the war-tidings flew,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sailed with a chosen crew
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From Lapland and Finmark.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So upon Easter day
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sailed the three kings away,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Out of the sheltered bay,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the bright season;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With them Earl Sigvald came,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Eager for spoil and fame;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pity that such a name
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stooped to such treason!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Safe under Svald at last,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now were their anchors cast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Safe from the sea and blast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Plotted the three kings;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While, with a base intent,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Southward Earl Sigvald went,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On a foul errand bent,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unto the Sea-kings.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thence to hold on his course,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unto King Olaf's force,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lying within the hoarse
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mouths of Stet-haven;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Him to ensnare and bring,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unto the Danish king,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who his dead corse would fling
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Forth to the raven!



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;XVIII

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;KING OLAF AND EARL SIGVALD

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the gray sea-sands
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;King Olaf stands,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Northward and seaward
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He points with his hands.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With eddy and whirl
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sea-tides curl,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Washing the sandals
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of Sigvald the Earl.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The mariners shout,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The ships swing about,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The yards are all hoisted,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sails flutter out.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The war-horns are played,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The anchors are weighed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like moths in the distance
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sails flit and fade.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sea is like lead
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The harbor lies dead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As a corse on the sea-shore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose spirit has fled!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On that fatal day,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The histories say,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seventy vessels
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sailed out of the bay.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But soon scattered wide
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er the billows they ride,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While Sigvald and Olaf
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sail side by side.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cried the Earl: "Follow me!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I your pilot will be,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For I know all the channels
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where flows the deep sea!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So into the strait
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where his foes lie in wait,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gallant King Olaf
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sails to his fate!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then the sea-fog veils
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The ships and their sails;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Queen Sigrid the Haughty,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thy vengeance prevails!



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;XIX

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;KING OLAF'S WAR-HORNS

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Strike the sails!" King Olaf said;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Never shall men of mine take flight;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never away from battle I fled,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never away from my foes!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let God dispose
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of my life in the fight!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Sound the horns!" said Olaf the King;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And suddenly through the drifting brume
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The blare of the horns began to ring,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the terrible trumpet shock
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of Regnarock,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the Day of Doom!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Louder and louder the war-horns sang
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over the level floor of the flood;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All the sails came down with a clang,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And there in the mist overhead
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sun hung red
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As a drop of blood.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drifting down on the Danish fleet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Three together the ships were lashed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So that neither should turn and retreat;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the midst, but in front of the rest
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The burnished crest
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the Serpent flashed.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;King Olaf stood on the quarter-deck,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With bow of ash and arrows of oak,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His gilded shield was without a fleck,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His helmet inlaid with gold,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And in many a fold
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hung his crimson cloak.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the forecastle Ulf the Red
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Watched the lashing of the ships;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"If the Serpent lie so far ahead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We shall have hard work of it here,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Said he with a sneer
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On his bearded lips.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;King Olaf laid an arrow on string,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Have I a coward on board?" said he.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Shoot it another way, O King!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sullenly answered Ulf,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The old sea-wolf;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"You have need of me!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In front came Svend, the King of the Danes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sweeping down with his fifty rowers;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the right, the Swedish king with his thanes;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And on board of the Iron Beard
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Earl Eric steered
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the left with his oars.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"These soft Danes and Swedes," said the King,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"At home with their wives had better stay,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Than come within reach of my Serpent's sting:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But where Eric the Norseman leads
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Heroic deeds
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Will be done to-day!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then as together the vessels crashed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Eric severed the cables of hide,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With which King Olaf's ships were lashed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And left them to drive and drift
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the currents swift
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the outward tide.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Louder the war-horns growl and snarl,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sharper the dragons bite and sting!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Eric the son of Hakon Jarl
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A death-drink salt as the sea
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pledges to thee,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Olaf the King!



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;XX

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;EINAR TAMBERSKELVER

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was Einar Tamberskelver
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stood beside the mast;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From his yew-bow, tipped with silver,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flew the arrows fast;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aimed at Eric unavailing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As he sat concealed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Half behind the quarter-railing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Half behind his shield.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;First an arrow struck the tiller,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Just above his head;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Sing, O Eyvind Skaldaspiller,"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then Earl Eric said.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Sing the song of Hakon dying,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sing his funeral wail!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And another arrow flying
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Grazed his coat of mail.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Turning to a Lapland yeoman,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the arrow passed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Said Earl Eric, "Shoot that bowman
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Standing by the mast."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sooner than the word was spoken
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flew the yeoman's shaft;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Einar's bow in twain was broken,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Einar only laughed.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"What was that?" said Olaf, standing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the quarter-deck.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Something heard I like the stranding
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of a shattered wreck."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Einar then, the arrow taking
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the loosened string,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Answered, "That was Norway breaking
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From thy hand, O King!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Thou art but a poor diviner,"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Straightway Olaf said;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Take my bow, and swifter, Einar,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let thy shafts be sped."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of his bows the fairest choosing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Reached he from above;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Einar saw the blood-drops oozing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through his iron glove.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the bow was thin and narrow;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At the first assay,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er its head he drew the arrow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flung the bow away;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Said, with hot and angry temper
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flushing in his cheek,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Olaf! for so great a Kamper
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are thy bows too weak!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then, with smile of joy defiant
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On his beardless lip,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Scaled he, light and self-reliant,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Eric's dragon-ship.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Loose his golden locks were flowing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bright his armor gleamed;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like Saint Michael overthrowing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lucifer he seemed.



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;XXI

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;KING OLAF'S DEATH-DRINK

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All day has the battle raged,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All day have the ships engaged,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But not yet is assuaged
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The vengeance of Eric the Earl.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The decks with blood are red,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The arrows of death are sped,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The ships are filled with the dead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the spears the champions hurl.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They drift as wrecks on the tide,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The grappling-irons are plied,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The boarders climb up the side,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The shouts are feeble and few.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah! never shall Norway again
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;See her sailors come back o'er the main;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They all lie wounded or slain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or asleep in the billows blue!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the deck stands Olaf the King,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Around him whistle and sing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The spears that the foemen fling,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the stones they hurl with their hands.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the midst of the stones and the spears,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kolbiorn, the marshal, appears,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His shield in the air he uprears,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the side of King Olaf he stands.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over the slippery wreck
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the Long Serpent's deck
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sweeps Eric with hardly a check,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His lips with anger are pale;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He hews with his axe at the mast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till it falls, with the sails overcast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a snow-covered pine in the vast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dim forests of Orkadale.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seeking King Olaf then,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He rushes aft with his men,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As a hunter into the den
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the bear, when he stands at bay.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Remember Jarl Hakon!" he cries;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When lo! on his wondering eyes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Two kingly figures arise,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Two Olaf's in warlike array!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then Kolbiorn speaks in the ear
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of King Olaf a word of cheer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In a whisper that none may hear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a smile on his tremulous lip;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Two shields raised high in the air,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Two flashes of golden hair,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Two scarlet meteors' glare,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And both have leaped from the ship.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Earl Eric's men in the boats
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seize Kolbiorn's shield as it floats,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And cry, from their hairy throats,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"See! it is Olaf the King!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While far on the opposite side
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Floats another shield on the tide,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a jewel set in the wide
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sea-current's eddying ring.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is told a wonderful tale,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How the King stripped off his mail,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like leaves of the brown sea-kale,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As he swam beneath the main;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the young grew old and gray,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And never, by night or by day,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In his kingdom of Norroway
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was King Olaf seen again!



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;XXII

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;THE NUN OF NIDAROS

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the convent of Drontheim,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alone in her chamber
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Knelt Astrid the Abbess,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At midnight, adoring,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beseeching, entreating
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Virgin and Mother.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She heard in the silence
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The voice of one speaking,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Without in the darkness,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In gusts of the night-wind
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now louder, now nearer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now lost in the distance.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The voice of a stranger
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It seemed as she listened,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of some one who answered,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beseeching, imploring,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A cry from afar off
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She could not distinguish.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The voice of Saint John,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The beloved disciple,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who wandered and waited
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Master's appearance.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alone in the darkness,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unsheltered and friendless.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"It is accepted
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The angry defiance
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The challenge of battle!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is accepted,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But not with the weapons
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of war that thou wieldest!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Cross against corselet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love against hatred,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Peace-cry for war-cry!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Patience is powerful;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He that o'ercometh
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hath power o'er the nations!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"As torrents in summer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Half dried in their channels,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suddenly rise, though the
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sky is still cloudless,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For rain has been falling
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Far off at their fountains;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So hearts that are fainting
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Grow full to o'erflowing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And they that behold it
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Marvel, and know not
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That God at their fountains
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Far off has been raining!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Stronger than steel
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is the sword of the Spirit;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Swifter than arrows
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The light of the truth is,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Greater than anger
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is love, and subdueth!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Thou art a phantom,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A shape of the sea-mist,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A shape of the brumal
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rain, and the darkness
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fearful and formless;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Day dawns and thou art not!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"The dawn is not distant,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor is the night starless;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love is eternal!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;God is still God, and
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His faith shall not fail us
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Christ is eternal!"




&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;INTERLUDE

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A strain of music closed the tale,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A low, monotonous, funeral wail,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That with its cadence, wild and sweet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Made the long Saga more complete.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Thank God," the Theologian said,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"The reign of violence is dead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or dying surely from the world;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While Love triumphant reigns instead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And in a brighter sky o'erhead
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His blessed banners are unfurled.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And most of all thank God for this:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The war and waste of clashing creeds
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now end in words, and not in deeds,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And no one suffers loss, or bleeds,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For thoughts that men call heresies.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I stand without here in the porch,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hear the bell's melodious din,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hear the organ peal within,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hear the prayer, with words that scorch
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like sparks from an inverted torch,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hear the sermon upon sin,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With threatenings of the last account.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And all, translated in the air,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Reach me but as our dear Lord's Prayer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And as the Sermon on the Mount.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Must it be Calvin, and not Christ?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Must it be Athanasian creeds,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or holy water, books, and beads?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Must struggling souls remain content
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With councils and decrees of Trend?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And can it be enough for these
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Christian Church the year embalms
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With evergreens and boughs of palms,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And fills the air with litanies?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I know that yonder Pharisee
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thanks God that he is not like me;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In my humiliation dressed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I only stand and beat my breast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And pray for human charity.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Not to one church alone, but seven,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The voice prophetic spake from heaven;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And unto each the promise came,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Diversified, but still the same;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For him that overcometh are
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The new name written on the stone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The raiment white, the crown, the throne,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I will give him the Morning Star!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Ah! to how many Faith has been
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No evidence of things unseen,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But a dim shadow, that recasts
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The creed of the Phantasiasts,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For whom no Man of Sorrows died,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For whom the Tragedy Divine
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was but a symbol and a sign,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Christ a phantom crucified!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"For others a diviner creed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is living in the life they lead.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The passing of their beautiful feet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blesses the pavement of the street
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And all their looks and words repeat
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Old Fuller's saying, wise and sweet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not as a vulture, but a dove,
