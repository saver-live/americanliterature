<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Oh that a Song would sing itself to me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Out of the heart of Nature, or the heart
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of man, the child of Nature, not of Art,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fresh as the morning, salt as the salt sea,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With just enough of bitterness to be
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A medicine to this sluggish mood, and start
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The life-blood in my veins, and so impart
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Healing and help in this dull lethargy!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alas! not always doth the breath of song
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Breathe on us.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is like the wind that bloweth
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At its own will, not ours, nor tarries long;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We hear the sound thereof, but no man knoweth
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From whence it comes, so sudden and swift and strong,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor whither in its wayward course it goeth.
</pre>
