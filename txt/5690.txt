<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;OCTOBER, 1746

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;MR. THOMAS PRINCE loquitur.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A fleet with flags arrayed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sailed from the port of Brest,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the Admiral's ship displayed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The signal: "Steer southwest."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For this Admiral D'Anville
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Had sworn by cross and crown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To ravage with fire and steel
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Our helpless Boston Town.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There were rumors in the street,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the houses there was fear
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the coming of the fleet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the danger hovering near.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And while from mouth to mouth
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Spread the tidings of dismay,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I stood in the Old South,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Saying humbly: "Let us pray!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"O Lord! we would not advise;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But if in thy Providence
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A tempest should arise
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To drive the French fleet hence,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And scatter it far and wide,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or sink it in the sea,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We should be satisfied,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And thine the glory be."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This was the prayer I made,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For my soul was all on flame,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And even as I prayed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The answering tempest came;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It came with a mighty power,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shaking the windows and walls,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And tolling the bell in the tower,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As it tolls at funerals.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The lightning suddenly
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unsheathed its flaming sword,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I cried: "Stand still, and see
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The salvation of the Lord!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The heavens were black with cloud,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sea was white with hail,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And ever more fierce and loud
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blew the October gale.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The fleet it overtook,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the broad sails in the van
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the tents of Cushan shook,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or the curtains of Midian.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Down on the reeling decks
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crashed the o'erwhelming seas;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah, never were there wrecks
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So pitiful as these!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a potter's vessel broke
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The great ships of the line;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They were carried away as a smoke,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or sank like lead in the brine.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O Lord! before thy path
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They vanished and ceased to be,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When thou didst walk in wrath
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With thine horses through the sea!
</pre>
