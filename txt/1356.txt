<p><cite class="al-title">Don Juan: In Sixteen Cantos</cite> is an "epic satire" in which Lord Byron reverses the legend of Don Juan into a man easily seduced by women. It was first published in 1824, with the seventeenth canto unfinished before his untimely death. We offer the 1857 edition, which includes his unfinished canto.</p><p><a href="dedication">Dedication</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-first">Canto the First</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-second">Canto the Second</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-third">Canto the Third</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-fourth">Canto the Fourth</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-fifth">Canto the Fifth</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-sixth">Canto the Sixth</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-seventh">Canto the Seventh</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-eighth">Canto the Eighth</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-ninth">Canto the Ninth</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-tenth">Canto the Tenth</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-eleventh">Canto the Eleventh</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-twelfth">Canto the Twelfth</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-thirteenth">Canto the Thirteenth</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-fourteenth">Canto the Fourteenth</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-fifteenth">Canto the Fifteenth</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-sixteenth">Canto the Sixteenth</a></p><p><a href="canto-the-seventeenth-unfinished">Canto the Seventeenth (unfinished)</a></p><p style="margin-top: 26px;"> Return to the <a href="/author/lord-byron">Lord Byron</a> library. </p>
