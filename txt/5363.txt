THE Owl and the Pussy-Cat went to sea
In a beautiful pea-green boat,
They took some honey, and plenty of money
Wrapped up in a five-pound note.


  <img data-pagespeed-lazy-src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/poem/the-owl-and-the-pussy-cat/the-owl-and-the-pussy-cat-image_02.jpg" alt="illustration for The Owl and the Pussy-Cat 2" title="illustration for The Owl and the Pussy-Cat 2" data-pagespeed-url-hash="1690982179" src="/pagespeed_static/1.JiBnMqyl6S.gif" onload="pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);" onerror="this.onerror=null;pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);">

The Owl looked up to the stars above,
And sang to a small guitar,

"O lovely Pussy! O Pussy, my love,
What a beautiful Pussy you are,
You are,
You are!
What a beautiful Pussy you are!"


  <img data-pagespeed-lazy-src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/poem/the-owl-and-the-pussy-cat/the-owl-and-the-pussy-cat-image_03.jpg" alt="illustration for The Owl and the Pussy-Cat 3" title="illustration for The Owl and the Pussy-Cat 3" data-pagespeed-url-hash="1985482100" src="/pagespeed_static/1.JiBnMqyl6S.gif" onload="pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);" onerror="this.onerror=null;pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);">

Pussy said to the Owl, "You elegant fowl!
How charmingly sweet you sing!

O let us be married! too long we have tarried:
But what shall we do for a ring?"


  <img data-pagespeed-lazy-src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/poem/the-owl-and-the-pussy-cat/the-owl-and-the-pussy-cat-image_04.jpg" alt="illustration for The Owl and the Pussy-Cat 4" title="illustration for The Owl and the Pussy-Cat 4" data-pagespeed-url-hash="2279982021" src="/pagespeed_static/1.JiBnMqyl6S.gif" onload="pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);" onerror="this.onerror=null;pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);">

They sailed away for a year and a day,
To the land where the Bong-tree grows,

And there in a wood a Piggy-wig stood,
With a ring at the end of his nose,
His nose,
His nose,
With a ring at the end of his nose.


  <img data-pagespeed-lazy-src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/poem/the-owl-and-the-pussy-cat/the-owl-and-the-pussy-cat-image_05.jpg" alt="illustration for The Owl and the Pussy-Cat 5" title="illustration for The Owl and the Pussy-Cat 5" data-pagespeed-url-hash="2574481942" src="/pagespeed_static/1.JiBnMqyl6S.gif" onload="pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);" onerror="this.onerror=null;pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);">

"Dear Pig, are you willing to sell for one shilling
Your ring?" Said the Piggy, "I will."


  <img data-pagespeed-lazy-src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/poem/the-owl-and-the-pussy-cat/the-owl-and-the-pussy-cat-image_06.jpg" alt="illustration for The Owl and the Pussy-Cat 6" title="illustration for The Owl and the Pussy-Cat 6" data-pagespeed-url-hash="2868981863" src="/pagespeed_static/1.JiBnMqyl6S.gif" onload="pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);" onerror="this.onerror=null;pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);">

So they took it away, and were married next day
By the Turkey who lives on the hill.

They din√®d on mince, and slices of quince
Which they ate with a runcible spoon;


  <img data-pagespeed-lazy-src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/poem/the-owl-and-the-pussy-cat/the-owl-and-the-pussy-cat-image_07.jpg" alt="illustration for The Owl and the Pussy-Cat 7" title="illustration for The Owl and the Pussy-Cat 7" data-pagespeed-url-hash="3163481784" src="/pagespeed_static/1.JiBnMqyl6S.gif" onload="pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);" onerror="this.onerror=null;pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);">

And hand in hand, on the edge of the sand,
They danced by the light of the moon,
The moon,
The moon,
They danced by the light of the moon.
