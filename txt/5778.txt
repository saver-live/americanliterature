<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Witlaf, a king of the Saxons,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ere yet his last he breathed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the merry monks of Croyland
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His drinking-horn bequeathed,--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That, whenever they sat at their revels,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And drank from the golden bowl,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They might remember the donor,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And breathe a prayer for his soul.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So sat they once at Christmas,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And bade the goblet pass;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In their beards the red wine glistened
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like dew-drops in the grass.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They drank to the soul of Witlaf,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They drank to Christ the Lord,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And to each of the Twelve Apostles,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who had preached his holy word.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They drank to the Saints and Martyrs
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the dismal days of yore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And as soon as the horn was empty
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They remembered one Saint more.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the reader droned from the pulpit
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the murmur of many bees,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The legend of good Saint Guthlac,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Saint Basil's homilies;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till the great bells of the convent,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From their prison in the tower,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Guthlac and Bartholomaeus,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Proclaimed the midnight hour.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the Yule-log cracked in the chimney,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the Abbot bowed his head,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the flamelets flapped and flickered,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the Abbot was stark and dead.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet still in his pallid fingers
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He clutched the golden bowl,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In which, like a pearl dissolving,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Had sunk and dissolved his soul.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But not for this their revels
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The jovial monks forbore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For they cried, "Fill high the goblet!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We must drink to one Saint more!"
</pre>
