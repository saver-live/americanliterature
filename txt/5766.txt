<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lull me to sleep, ye winds, whose fitful sound
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seems from some faint Aeolian harp-string caught;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seal up the hundred wakeful eyes of thought
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As Hermes with his lyre in sleep profound
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The hundred wakeful eyes of Argus bound;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For I am weary, and am overwrought
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With too much toil, with too much care distraught,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And with the iron crown of anguish crowned.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lay thy soft hand upon my brow and cheek,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O peaceful Sleep! until from pain released
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I breathe again uninterrupted breath!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah, with what subtile meaning did the Greek
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Call thee the lesser mystery at the feast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whereof the greater mystery is death!
</pre>
