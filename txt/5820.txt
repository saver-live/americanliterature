&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A vision as of crowded city streets,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With human life in endless overflow;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thunder of thoroughfares; trumpets that blow
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To battle; clamor, in obscure retreats,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of sailors landed from their anchored fleets;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tolling of bells in turrets, and below
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Voices of children, and bright flowers that throw
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O'er garden-walls their intermingled sweets!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This vision comes to me when I unfold
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The volume of the Poet paramount,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whom all the Muses loved, not one alone;--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into his hands they put the lyre of gold,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, crowned with sacred laurel at their fount,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Placed him as Musagetes on their throne.
