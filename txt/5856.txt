<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The day is done, and the darkness
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Falls from the wings of Night,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As a feather is wafted downward
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From an eagle in his flight.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I see the lights of the village
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gleam through the rain and the mist,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And a feeling of sadness comes o'er me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That my soul cannot resist:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A feeling of sadness and longing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That is not akin to pain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And resembles sorrow only
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the mist resembles the rain.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come, read to me some poem,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some simple and heartfelt lay,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That shall soothe this restless feeling,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And banish the thoughts of day.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not from the grand old masters,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not from the bards sublime,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose distant footsteps echo
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through the corridors of Time.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For, like strains of martial music,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their mighty thoughts suggest
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Life's endless toil and endeavor;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And to-night I long for rest.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Read from some humbler poet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose songs gushed from his heart,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As showers from the clouds of summer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or tears from the eyelids start;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who, through long days of labor,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And nights devoid of ease,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Still heard in his soul the music
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of wonderful melodies.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Such songs have power to quiet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The restless pulse of care,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And come like the benediction
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That follows after prayer.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then read from the treasured volume
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The poem of thy choice,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And lend to the rhyme of the poet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The beauty of thy voice.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the night shall be filled with music
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the cares, that infest the day,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shall fold their tents, like the Arabs,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And as silently steal away.
</pre>
