<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How many lives, made beautiful and sweet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By self-devotion and by self-restraint,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose pleasure is to run without complaint
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On unknown errands of the Paraclete,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wanting the reverence of unshodden feet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fail of the nimbus which the artists paint
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Around the shining forehead of the saint,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And are in their completeness incomplete!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the old Tuscan town stands Giotto's tower,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The lily of Florence blossoming in stone,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A vision, a delight, and a desire,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The builder's perfect and centennial flower,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That in the night of ages bloomed alone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But wanting still the glory of the spire.
</pre>
