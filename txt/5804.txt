<pre>"Combien faudrait-il de peaux d'Espagne pour faire un gant de cette grandeur?"&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A play upon the words gant, a glove, and Gand, the French for Ghent.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On St. Baron's tower, commanding
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Half of Flanders, his domain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Charles the Emperor once was standing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While beneath him on the landing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stood Duke Alva and his train.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a print in books of fables,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or a model made for show,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With its pointed roofs and gables,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dormer windows, scrolls and labels,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lay the city far below.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through its squares and streets and alleys
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Poured the populace of Ghent;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As a routed army rallies,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or as rivers run through valleys,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hurrying to their homes they went

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Nest of Lutheran misbelievers!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cried Duke Alva as he gazed;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Haunt of traitors and deceivers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stronghold of insurgent weavers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let it to the ground be razed!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the Emperor's cap the feather
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nods, as laughing he replies:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"How many skins of Spanish leather,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Think you, would, if stitched together
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Make a glove of such a size?"
</pre>
