&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;St. Botolph's Town!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hither across the plains
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And fens of Lincolnshire, in garb austere,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There came a Saxon monk, and founded here
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A Priory, pillaged by marauding Danes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So that thereof no vestige now remains;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Only a name, that, spoken loud and clear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And echoed in another hemisphere,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Survives the sculptured walls and painted panes.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;St. Botolph's Town!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Far over leagues of land
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And leagues of sea looks forth its noble tower,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And far around the chiming bells are heard;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So may that sacred name forever stand
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A landmark, and a symbol of the power,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That lies concentred in a single word.
</pre>
