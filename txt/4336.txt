<pre>I.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here all the summer could I stay,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For there's Bishop's teign
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And King's teign
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Coomb at the clear Teign head
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where close by the stream
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You may have your cream
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All spread upon barley bread.

II.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There's Arch Brook
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And there's Larch Brook
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Both turning many a mill,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And cooling the drouth
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the salmon's mouth
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And fattening his silver gill.

III.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is Wild wood,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A Mild hood
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the sheep on the lea o' the down,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where the golden furze,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With its green, thin spurs,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Doth catch at the maiden's gown.

IV.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is Newton Marsh
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With its spear grass harsh
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A pleasant summer level
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where the maidens sweet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the Market Street
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Do meet in the dusk to revel.

V.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There's the Barton rich
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With dyke and ditch
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And hedge for the thrush to live in,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the hollow tree
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For the buzzing bee
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And a bank for the wasp to hive in.

VI.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And O, and O
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The daisies blow
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the primroses are waken'd,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And violets white
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sit in silver plight,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the green bud's as long as the spike end.

VII.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then who would go
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into dark Soho,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And chatter with dack'd-hair'd critics,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When he can stay
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For the new-mown hay,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And startle the dappled Prickets?
</pre>
