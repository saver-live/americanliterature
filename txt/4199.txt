<p><cite class="al-title">The Trial</cite> is an exemplary dystopian novel written by Kafka in 1914, published posthumously in 1925. A man is arrested and prosecuted by a remote, inaccessible authority, without being told the nature of his crime. Kafka was inspired by <a href="/author/fyodor-dostoevsky">Fyodor Dostoevsky</a>'s similar themed novels about injustice, <a href="/author/fyodor-dostoevsky/book/crime-and-punishment/summary">Crime and Punishment</a>, and <a href="/author/fyodor-dostoevsky/book/the-brothers-karamazov/summary">The Brothers Karamazov</a>.</p><p><a href="arrest-conversation-with-mrs-grubach-then-miss-brstner">Arrest - Conversation with Mrs. Grubach - Then Miss Brstner</a></p><p><a href="first-cross-examination">First Cross-examination</a></p><p><a href="in-the-empty-courtroom-the-student-the-offices">In the empty Courtroom - The Student - The Offices</a></p><p><a href="miss-brstners-friend">Miss Brstner's Friend</a></p><p><a href="the-whip-man">The whip-man</a></p><p><a href="ks-uncle-leni">K.'s uncle - Leni</a></p><p><a href="lawyer-manufacturer-painter">Lawyer - Manufacturer - Painter</a></p><p><a href="block-the-businessman-dismissing-the-lawyer">Block, the businessman - Dismissing the lawyer</a></p><p><a href="in-the-cathedral">In the Cathedral</a></p><p><a href="end">End</a></p><p style="margin-top: 26px;"> Return to the <a href="/author/franz-kafka">Franz Kafka</a> library. </p>
