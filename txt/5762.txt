<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whene'er a noble deed is wrought,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whene'er is spoken a noble thought,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Our hearts, in glad surprise,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; To higher levels rise.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The tidal wave of deeper souls
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into our inmost being rolls,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; And lifts us unawares
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Out of all meaner cares.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Honor to those whose words or deeds
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus help us in our daily needs,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; And by their overflow
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Raise us from what is low!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus thought I, as by night I read
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the great army of the dead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The trenches cold and damp,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The starved and frozen camp,--

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The wounded from the battle-plain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In dreary hospitals of pain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The cheerless corridors,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The cold and stony floors.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lo! in that house of misery
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A lady with a lamp I see
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Pass through the glimmering gloom,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; And flit from room to room.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And slow, as in a dream of bliss,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The speechless sufferer turns to kiss
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Her shadow, as it falls
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Upon the darkening walls.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As if a door in heaven should be
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Opened and then closed suddenly,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The vision came and went,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The light shone and was spent.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On England's annals, through the long
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hereafter of her speech and song,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; That light its rays shall cast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; From portals of the past.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A Lady with a Lamp shall stand
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the great history of the land,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; A noble type of good,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Heroic womanhood.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor even shall be wanting here
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The palm, the lily, and the spear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The symbols that of yore
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Saint Filomena bore.
</pre>
