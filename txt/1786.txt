<p>Another great novel that inhabits the intersection of literature and history, <span class="al-title">For the Term of His Natural Life</span> (1870s) provides a vivid account of the brutality that abounded in the early colonial history of Australian penal colonies. The novel follows <span class="al-char">Rufus Dawes</span>, and portrays his life as a convict after he is found guilty of a murder he did not commit and is transported to a penal colony in 1827.</p><p>The novel humanizes <span class="al-char">Dawes</span> struggle to persevere with dignity and honor in an unfair system and documents the complex and intricate interactions between those that hold power the people who suffer under their dominion.</p><p><a href="dedication">Dedication</a></p><p><a href="prologue">Prologue</a></p><p><b><a href="book-i-the-sea-1827" style="color:black;
