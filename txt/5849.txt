<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is it so far from thee
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou canst no longer see,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the Chamber over the Gate,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That old man desolate,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Weeping and wailing sore
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For his son, who is no more?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O Absalom, my son!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is it so long ago
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That cry of human woe
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the walled city came,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Calling on his dear name,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That it has died away
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the distance of to-day?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O Absalom, my son!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is no far or near,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is neither there nor here,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is neither soon nor late,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In that Chamber over the Gate,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor any long ago
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To that cry of human woe,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O Absalom, my son!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the ages that are past
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The voice sounds like a blast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over seas that wreck and drown,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over tumult of traffic and town;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And from ages yet to be
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come the echoes back to me,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O Absalom, my son!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Somewhere at every hour
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The watchman on the tower
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Looks forth, and sees the fleet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Approach of the hurrying feet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of messengers, that bear
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The tidings of despair.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O Absalom, my son!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He goes forth from the door
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who shall return no more.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With him our joy departs;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The light goes out in our hearts;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the Chamber over the Gate
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We sit disconsolate.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O Absalom, my son!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That 't is a common grief
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bringeth but slight relief;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ours is the bitterest loss,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ours is the heaviest cross;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And forever the cry will be
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Would God I had died for thee,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O Absalom, my son!"
</pre>
