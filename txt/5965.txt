<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;See, the fire is sinking low,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dusky red the embers glow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While above them still I cower,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While a moment more I linger,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Though the clock, with lifted finger,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Points beyond the midnight hour.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sings the blackened log a tune
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Learned in some forgotten June
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From a school-boy at his play,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When they both were young together,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Heart of youth and summer weather
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Making all their holiday.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the night-wind rising, hark!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How above there in the dark,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the midnight and the snow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ever wilder, fiercer, grander,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the trumpets of Iskander,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All the noisy chimneys blow!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Every quivering tongue of flame
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seems to murmur some great name,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seems to say to me, "Aspire!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the night-wind answers, "Hollow
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are the visions that you follow,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into darkness sinks your fire!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then the flicker of the blaze
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gleams on volumes of old days,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Written by masters of the art,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Loud through whose majestic pages
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rolls the melody of ages,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Throb the harp-strings of the heart.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And again the tongues of flame
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Start exulting and exclaim:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"These are prophets, bards, and seers;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the horoscope of nations,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like ascendant constellations,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They control the coming years."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the night-wind cries: "Despair!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Those who walk with feet of air
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leave no long-enduring marks;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At God's forges incandescent
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mighty hammers beat incessant,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These are but the flying sparks.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Dust are all the hands that wrought;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Books are sepulchres of thought;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The dead laurels of the dead
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rustle for a moment only,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like the withered leaves in lonely
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Churchyards at some passing tread."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suddenly the flame sinks down;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sink the rumors of renown;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And alone the night-wind drear
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Clamors louder, wilder, vaguer,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"'T is the brand of Meleager
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dying on the hearth-stone here!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I answer,--"Though it be,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why should that discomfort me?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No endeavor is in vain;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Its reward is in the doing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the rapture of pursuing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is the prize the vanquished gain."
</pre>
