<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Far in a western brookland
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That bred me long ago
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The poplars stand and tremble
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By pools I used to know.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There, in the windless night-time,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The wanderer, marvelling why,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Halts on the bridge to hearken
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How soft the poplars sigh.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He hears: long since forgotten
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In fields where I was known,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here I lie down in London
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And turn to rest alone.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There, by the starlit fences,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The wanderer halts and hears
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My soul that lingers sighing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;About the glimmering weirs.
</pre>
