<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;TO ALFRED TENNYSON

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Poet! I come to touch thy lance with mine;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not as a knight, who on the listed field
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of tourney touched his adversary's shield
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In token of defiance, but in sign
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of homage to the mastery, which is thine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In English song; nor will I keep concealed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And voiceless as a rivulet frost-congealed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My admiration for thy verse divine.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not of the howling dervishes of song,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who craze the brain with their delirious dance,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Art thou, O sweet historian of the heart!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Therefore to thee the laurel-leaves belong,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To thee our love and our allegiance,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For thy allegiance to the poet's art.
</pre>
