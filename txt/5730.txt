<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Half of my life is gone, and I have let
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The years slip from me and have not fulfilled
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The aspiration of my youth, to build
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some tower of song with lofty parapet.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not indolence, nor pleasure, nor the fret
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of restless passions chat would not be stilled,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But sorrow, and a care that almost killed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kept me from what I may accomplish yet;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Though, half way up the hill, I see the Past
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lying beneath me with its sounds and sights,--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A city in the twilight dim and vast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With smoking roofs, soft bells, and gleaming lights.--
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And hear above me on the autumnal blast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The cataract of Death far thundering from the heights.
</pre>
