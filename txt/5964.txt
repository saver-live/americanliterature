<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Behold! a giant am I!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aloft here in my tower,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With my granite jaws I devour
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The maize, and the wheat, and the rye,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And grind them into flour.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I look down over the farms;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the fields of grain I see
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The harvest that is to be,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I fling to the air my arms,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For I know it is all for me.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hear the sound of flails
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Far off, from the threshing-floors
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In barns, with their open doors,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the wind, the wind in my sails,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Louder and louder roars.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I stand here in my place,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With my foot on the rock below,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And whichever way it may blow
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I meet it face to face,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As a brave man meets his foe.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And while we wrestle and strive
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My master, the miller, stands
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And feeds me with his hands;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For he knows who makes him thrive,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who makes him lord of lands.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On Sundays I take my rest;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Church-going bells begin
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their low, melodious din;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I cross my arms on my breast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And all is peace within.
</pre>
