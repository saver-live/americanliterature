<p>The Financial Panic of 1907 is often cited as a justification for the passage of the Federal Reserve Act of 1913 (signed into law on December 23rd . . . watch carefully what your legislators do to you during the holidays when nobody is paying attention, they continue to use this trick even today). </p><p> <cite class="al-title">The Moneychangers</cite> is Upton Sinclair's barely fictionalized account of the financial crisis, and in the novel a character based on J.P. Morgan is lauded for rescuing everyone from a crisis that he created.
