<p>Blasted with sighs, and surrounded with tears,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Hither I come to seek the spring,<br> And at mine eyes, and at mine ears,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Receive such balms as else cure every thing.<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>But O&nbsp;! self-traitor, I do bring<br> The spider Love, which transubstantiates all,<br> And can convert manna to gall;<br> And that this place may thoroughly be thought<br> True paradise, I have the serpent brought.<br> <br> 'Twere wholesomer for me that winter did<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Benight the glory of this place,<br> And that a grave frost did forbid<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>These trees to laugh and mock me to my face;<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>But that I may not this disgrace<br> Endure, nor yet leave loving, Love, let me<br> Some senseless piece of this place be;<br> Make me a mandrake, so I may grow here,<br> Or a stone fountain weeping out my year.<br> <br> Hither with crystal phials, lovers, come,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>And take my tears, which are love's wine,<br> And try your mistress' tears at home,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>For all are false, that taste not just like mine.<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Alas&nbsp;! hearts do not in eyes shine,<br> Nor can you more judge women's thoughts by tears,<br> Than by her shadow what she wears.<br> O perverse sex, where none is true but she,<br> Who's therefore true, because her truth kills me.<br></p>
