<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the outskirts of the town
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where of old the mile-stone stood.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now a stranger, looking down
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I behold the shadowy crown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the dark and haunted wood.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is it changed, or am I changed?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah! the oaks are fresh and green,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the friends with whom I ranged
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through their thickets are estranged
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the years that intervene.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bright as ever flows the sea,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bright as ever shines the sun,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But alas! they seem to me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not the sun that used to be,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not the tides that used to run.
</pre>
