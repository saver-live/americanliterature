<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On moonlit heath and lonesome bank
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sheep beside me graze;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And yon the gallows used to clank
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fast by the four cross ways.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A careless shepherd once would keep
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The flocks by moonlight there, <sup>[1]</sup>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And high amongst the glimmering sheep
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The dead man stood on air.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They hang us now in Shrewsbury jail:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The whistles blow forlorn,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And trains all night groan on the rail
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To men that die at morn.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There sleeps in Shrewsbury jail to-night,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or wakes, as may betide,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A better lad, if things went right,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Than most that sleep outside.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And naked to the hangman's noose
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The morning clocks will ring
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A neck God made for other use
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Than strangling in a string.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And sharp the link of life will snap,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And dead on air will stand
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Heels that held up as straight a chap
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As treads upon the land.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So here I'll watch the night and wait
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To see the morning shine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When he will hear the stroke of eight
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And not the stroke of nine;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And wish my friend as sound a sleep
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As lads' I did not know,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That shepherded the moonlit sheep
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A hundred years ago.
</pre>
