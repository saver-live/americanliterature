<pre>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As a pale phantom with a lamp
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ascends some ruin's haunted stair,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So glides the moon along the damp
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mysterious chambers of the air.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now hidden in cloud, and now revealed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As if this phantom, full of pain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Were by the crumbling walls concealed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And at the windows seen again.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Until at last, serene and proud
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In all the splendor of her light,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She walks the terraces of cloud,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Supreme as Empress of the Night.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I look, but recognize no more
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Objects familiar to my view;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The very pathway to my door
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is an enchanted avenue.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All things are changed.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One mass of shade,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The elm-trees drop their curtains down;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By palace, park, and colonnade
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I walk as in a foreign town.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The very ground beneath my feet
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is clothed with a diviner air;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;White marble paves the silent street
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And glimmers in the empty square.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Illusion!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Underneath there lies
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The common life of every day;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Only the spirit glorifies
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With its own tints the sober gray.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In vain we look, in vain uplift
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Our eyes to heaven, if we are blind,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We see but what we have the gift
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of seeing; what we bring we find.
</pre>
