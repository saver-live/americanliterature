&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wake: the silver dusk returning
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Up the beach of darkness brims,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the ship of sunrise burning
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Strands upon the eastern rims.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wake: the vaulted shadow shatters,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Trampled to the floor it spanned,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the tent of night in tatters
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Straws the sky-pavilioned land.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Up, lad, up, 'tis late for lying:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hear the drums of morning play;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hark, the empty highways crying
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Who'll beyond the hills away?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Towns and countries woo together,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Forelands beacon, belfries call;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never lad that trod on leather
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lived to feast his heart with all.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Up, lad: thews that lie and cumber
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sunlit pallets never thrive;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Morns abed and daylight slumber
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Were not meant for man alive.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Clay lies still, but blood's a rover;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Breath's a ware that will not keep
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Up, lad: when the journey's over
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There'll be time enough to sleep.
