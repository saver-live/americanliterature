<pre>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The vane on Hughley steeple
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Veers bright, a far-known sign,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And there lie Hughley people,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And there lie friends of mine.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tall in their midst the tower
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Divides the shade and sun,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the clock strikes the hour
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And tells the time to none.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To south the headstones cluster,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sunny mounds lie thick;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The dead are more in muster
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At Hughley than the quick.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;North, for a soon-told number,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chill graves the sexton delves,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And steeple-shadowed slumber
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The slayers of themselves.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To north, to south, lie parted,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With Hughley tower above,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The kind, the single-hearted,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The lads I used to love.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, south or north, 'tis only
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A choice of friends one knows,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I shall ne'er be lonely
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Asleep with these or those.
</pre>
