<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Take them, O Death! and bear away
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whatever thou canst call thine own!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thine image, stamped upon this clay,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Doth give thee that, but that alone!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Take them, O Grave! and let them lie
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Folded upon thy narrow shelves,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As garments by the soul laid by,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And precious only to ourselves!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Take them, O great Eternity!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Our little life is but a gust
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That bends the branches of thy tree,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And trails its blossoms in the dust!
</pre>
