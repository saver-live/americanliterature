&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Once into a quiet village,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Without haste and without heed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the golden prime of morning,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Strayed the poet's winged steed.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was Autumn, and incessant
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Piped the quails from shocks and sheaves,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, like living coals, the apples
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Burned among the withering leaves.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Loud the clamorous bell was ringing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From its belfry gaunt and grim;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T was the daily call to labor,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not a triumph meant for him.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not the less he saw the landscape,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In its gleaming vapor veiled;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not the less he breathed the odors
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That the dying leaves exhaled.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus, upon the village common,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the school-boys he was found;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the wise men, in their wisdom,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Put him straightway into pound.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then the sombre village crier,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ringing loud his brazen bell,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wandered down the street proclaiming
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There was an estray to sell.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the curious country people,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rich and poor, and young and old,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Came in haste to see this wondrous
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Winged steed, with mane of gold.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thus the day passed, and the evening
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fell, with vapors cold and dim;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But it brought no food nor shelter,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Brought no straw nor stall, for him.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Patiently, and still expectant,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Looked he through the wooden bars,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Saw the moon rise o'er the landscape,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Saw the tranquil, patient stars;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till at length the bell at midnight
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sounded from its dark abode,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, from out a neighboring farm-yard
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Loud the cock Alectryon crowed.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then, with nostrils wide distended,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Breaking from his iron chain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And unfolding far his pinions,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To those stars he soared again.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the morrow, when the village
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Woke to all its toil and care,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lo! the strange steed had departed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And they knew not when nor where.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But they found, upon the greensward
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where his straggling hoofs had trod,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pure and bright, a fountain flowing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the hoof-marks in the sod.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From that hour, the fount unfailing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gladdens the whole region round,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Strengthening all who drink its waters,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While it soothes them with its sound.
