&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"A soldier of the Union mustered out,"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is the inscription on an unknown grave
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At Newport News, beside the salt-sea wave,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nameless and dateless; sentinel or scout
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shot down in skirmish, or disastrous rout
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of battle, when the loud artillery drave
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Its iron wedges through the ranks of brave
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And doomed battalions, storming the redoubt.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou unknown hero sleeping by the sea
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In thy forgotten grave! with secret shame
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I feel my pulses beat, my forehead burn,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When I remember thou hast given for me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All that thou hadst, thy life, thy very name,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And I can give thee nothing in return.
