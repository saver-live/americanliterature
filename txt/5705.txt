<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As Seleucus narrates, Hermes describes the principles that rank as wholes in two myriads of books; or, as we are informed by Manetho, he perfectly unfolded these principles in three myriads six thousand five hundred and twenty-five volumes. . . .
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;. . . Our ancestors dedicated the inventions of&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;their wisdom to this deity, inscribing all their own writings with the name of Hermes.--IAMBLICUS.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Still through Egypt's desert places
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flows the lordly Nile,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From its banks the great stone faces
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gaze with patient smile.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Still the pyramids imperious
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pierce the cloudless skies,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the Sphinx stares with mysterious,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Solemn, stony eyes.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But where are the old Egyptian
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Demi-gods and kings?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nothing left but an inscription
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Graven on stones and rings.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where are Helios and Hephaestus,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gods of eldest eld?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where is Hermes Trismegistus,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who their secrets held?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where are now the many hundred
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thousand books he wrote?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the Thaumaturgists plundered,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lost in lands remote;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In oblivion sunk forever,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As when o'er the land
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blows a storm-wind, in the river
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Sinks the scattered sand.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Something unsubstantial, ghostly,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seems this Theurgist,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In deep meditation mostly
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wrapped, as in a mist.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vague, phantasmal, and unreal
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To our thought he seems,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Walking in a world ideal,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In a land of dreams.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was he one, or many, merging
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Name and fame in one,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a stream, to which, converging
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Many streamlets run?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Till, with gathered power proceeding,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ampler sweep it takes,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Downward the sweet waters leading
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From unnumbered lakes.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the Nile I see him wandering,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pausing now and then,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the mystic union pondering
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Between gods and men;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Half believing, wholly feeling,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With supreme delight,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How the gods, themselves concealing,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lift men to their height.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or in Thebes, the hundred-gated,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the thoroughfare
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Breathing, as if consecrated,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A diviner air;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And amid discordant noises,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the jostling throng,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hearing far, celestial voices
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of Olympian song.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who shall call his dreams fallacious?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who has searched or sought
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All the unexplored and spacious
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Universe of thought?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who, in his own skill confiding,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Shall with rule and line
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mark the border-land dividing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Human and divine?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Trismegistus! three times greatest!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How thy name sublime
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Has descended to this latest
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Progeny of time!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Happy they whose written pages
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Perish with their lives,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If amid the crumbling ages
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Still their name survives!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thine, O priest of Egypt, lately
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Found I in the vast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Weed-encumbered sombre, stately,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Grave-yard of the Past;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And a presence moved before me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On that gloomy shore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As a waft of wind, that o'er me
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Breathed, and was no more.
</pre>
