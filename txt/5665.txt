<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;An old man in a lodge within a park;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The chamber walls depicted all around
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With portraitures of huntsman, hawk, and hound.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the hurt deer.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He listeneth to the lark,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose song comes with the sunshine through the dark
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of painted glass in leaden lattice bound;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He listeneth and he laugheth at the sound,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then writeth in a book like any clerk.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He is the poet of the dawn, who wrote
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Canterbury Tales, and his old age
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Made beautiful with song; and as I read
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I hear the crowing cock, I hear the note
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of lark and linnet, and from every page
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rise odors of ploughed field or flowery mead.
</pre>
