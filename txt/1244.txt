<pre>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Go, sit upon the lofty hill,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And turn your eyes around,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where waving woods and waters wild
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Do hymn an autumn sound.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The summer sun is faint on them,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The summer flowers depart,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sit still, as all transform'd to stone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Except your musing heart.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How there you sat in summer-time,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;May yet be in your mind;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And how you heard the green woods sing
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beneath the freshening wind.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Though the same wind now blows around,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You would its blast recall;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For every breath that stirs the trees,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Doth cause a leaf to fall.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Oh! like that wind, is all the mirth
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That flesh and dust impart:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We cannot bear its visitings,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When change is on the heart.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gay words and jests may make us smile,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When Sorrow is asleep;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But other things must make us smile,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When Sorrow bids us weep!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The dearest hands that clasp our hands,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their presence may be o'er;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The dearest voice that meets our ear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That tone may come no more!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Youth fades; and then, the joys of youth,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which once refresh'd our mind,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shall come, as, on those sighing woods,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The chilling autumn wind.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hear not the wind, view not the woods;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Look out o'er vale and hill
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In spring, the sky encircled them,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sky is round them still.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come autumn's scathe, come winter's cold,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Come change, and human fate!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whatever prospect Heaven doth bound,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Can ne'er be desolate.
</pre>
