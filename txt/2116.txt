<p>FOR every hour that thou wilt spare me now,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:3em;">&nbsp;</span>I will allow,<br> Usurious god of love, twenty to thee,<br> When with my brown my gray hairs equal be.<br> Till then, Love, let my body range, and let<br> Me travel, sojourn, snatch, plot, have, forget,<br> Resume my last year's relict; think that yet<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:3em;">&nbsp;</span>We'd never met.<br> <br> Let me think any rival's letter mine,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:3em;">&nbsp;</span>And at next nine<br> Keep midnight's promise; mistake by the way<br> The maid, and tell the lady of that delay;<br> Only let me love none; no, not the sport<br> From country grass to confitures of court,<br> Or city's <i>quelque-choses</i>; let not report<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:3em;">&nbsp;</span>My mind transport.<br> <br> This bargain's good; if when I'm old, I be<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:3em;">&nbsp;</span>Inflamed by thee,<br> If thine own honour, or my shame and pain,<br> Thou covet most, at that age thou shalt gain.<br> Do thy will then; then subject and degree<br> And fruit of love, Love, I submit to thee.<br> Spare me till then; I'll bear it, though she be<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:3em;">&nbsp;</span>One that love me.</p>
