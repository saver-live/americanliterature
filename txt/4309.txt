<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Son of the old Moon-mountains African!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chief of the Pyramid and Crocodile!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We call thee fruitful, and that very while
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A desert fills our seeing's inward span:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nurse of swart nations since the world began,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Art thou so fruitful? or dost thou beguile
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Such men to honour thee, who, worn with toil,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Rest for a space 'twixt Cairo and Decan?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O may dark fancies err! They surely do;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'Tis ignorance that makes a barren waste
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of all beyond itself. Thou dost bedew
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Green rushes like our rivers, and dost taste
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The pleasant sunrise. Green isles hast thou too,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And to the sea as happily dost haste.
</pre>
