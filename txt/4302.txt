<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O Chatterton! how very sad thy fate!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dear child of sorrow son of misery!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How soon the film of death obscur'd that eye,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whence Genius mildly falsh'd, and high debate.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How soon that voice, majestic and elate,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Melted in dying numbers! Oh! how nigh
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was night to thy fair morning. Thou didst die
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A half-blown flow'ret which cold blasts amate.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But this is past: thou art among the stars
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of highest heaven: to the rolling spheres
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou sweetly singest: nought thy hymning mars,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Above the ingrate world and human fears.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On earth the good man base detraction bars
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From thy fair name, and waters it with tears.
</pre>
