<pre>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"E venni dal martirio a questa pace."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These words the poet heard in Paradise,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Uttered by one who, bravely dying here,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the true faith was living in that sphere
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where the celestial cross of sacrifice
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Spread its protecting arms athwart the skies;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And set thereon, like jewels crystal clear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The souls magnanimous, that knew not fear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flashed their effulgence on his dazzled eyes.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah me! how dark the discipline of pain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Were not the suffering followed by the sense
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of infinite rest and infinite release!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This is our consolation; and again
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A great soul cries to us in our suspense,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I came from martyrdom unto this peace!"
</pre>
