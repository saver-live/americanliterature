<p><cite class="al-title">Tolstoy on Shakespeare</cite> is a critical essay of Shakespeare's work, published in 1906. It was translated by V. Tchertkoffis, presented here as a novel due to its length and chapter format. His Shakespeare-bashing is quite thorough and cathartic, actually: <i>"Not only did I feel no delight, but I felt an irresistible repulsion and tedium, and doubted as to whether I was senseless in feeling works regarded as the summit of perfection by the whole of the civilized world to be trivial and positively bad..."</i></p><p><i>It may prompt you to re-read some of <a href="/author/william-shakespeare">Shakespeare</a>'s plays.
