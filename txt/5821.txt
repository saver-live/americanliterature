<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Southward with fleet of ice
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sailed the corsair Death;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wild and fast blew the blast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the east-wind was his breath.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His lordly ships of ice
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Glisten in the sun;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On each side, like pennons wide,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flashing crystal streamlets run.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His sails of white sea-mist
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dripped with silver rain;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But where he passed there were cast
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leaden shadows o'er the main.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Eastward from Campobello
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sir Humphrey Gilbert sailed;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Three days or more seaward he bore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then, alas! the land-wind failed.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alas! the land-wind failed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And ice-cold grew the night;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And nevermore, on sea or shore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Should Sir Humphrey see the light.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He sat upon the deck,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Book was in his hand
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Do not fear!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Heaven is as near,"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He said, "by water as by land!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the first watch of the night,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Without a signal's sound,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Out of the sea, mysteriously,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The fleet of Death rose all around.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The moon and the evening star
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Were hanging in the shrouds;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Every mast, as it passed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seemed to rake the passing clouds.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They grappled with their prize,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At midnight black and cold!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As of a rock was the shock;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Heavily the ground-swell rolled.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Southward through day and dark,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They drift in close embrace,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With mist and rain, o'er the open main;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yet there seems no change of place.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Southward, forever southward,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They drift through dark and day;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And like a dream, in the Gulf-Stream
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sinking, vanish all away.
</pre>
