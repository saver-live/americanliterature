<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bring, in this timeless grave to throw,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No cypress, sombre on the snow;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Snap not from the bitter yew
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His leaves that live December through;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Break no rosemary, bright with rime
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And sparkling to the cruel clime;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nor plod the winter land to look
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For willows in the icy brook
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To cast them leafless round him: bring
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No spray that ever buds in spring.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But if the Christmas field has kept
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Awns the last gleaner overstept,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or shrivelled flax, whose flower is blue
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A single season, never two;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or if one haulm whose year is o'er
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shivers on the upland frore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;-Oh, bring from hill and stream and plain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whatever will not flower again,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To give him comfort: he and those
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Shall bide eternal bedfellows
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where low upon the couch he lies
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whence he never shall arise.
</pre>
