<p><cite class="al-title">Lady Susan</cite> is a novel composed as a series of letters (called an "epistolary novel"), one of Austen's earliest works (completed in 1794), but not published until 1871. It's about a seemingly charming, recently widowed, unwanted house guest, at least for his brother's wife because she tried to prevent their marriage. She considers Susan "the most accomplished coquette in England."
