<p><cite class="al-title">Miss Lulu Bett</cite> (1920) was Gale's most acclaimed novel, which she adapted into a play that same year, for which she was the first woman to receive the Pulitzer Prize for Drama in 1923. The story is about Lulu, who essentially serves as a housemaid for her sister's family,and remains unhappy until she falls in love with her brother-in-law, Ninian. They accidentally get married, but their happiness doesn't last long, as Lulu discovers he's already married, among other things, as the drama unfolds. The novel consists of six chapters, each a month: April through September.</p><p><a href="i-april">I - April</a></p><p><a href="ii-may">II - May</a></p><p><a href="iii-june">III - June</a></p><p><a href="iv-july">IV - July</a></p><p><a href="v-august">V - August</a></p><p><a href="vi-september">VI - September</a></p><p style="margin-top: 26px;"> Return to the <a href="/author/zona-gale">Zona Gale</a> library. </p>
