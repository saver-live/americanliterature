<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I lay upon the headland-height, and listened
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the incessant sobbing of the sea
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In caverns under me,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And watched the waves, that tossed and fled and glistened,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Until the rolling meadows of amethyst
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Melted away in mist.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then suddenly, as one from sleep, I started;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For round about me all the sunny capes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seemed peopled with the shapes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of those whom I had known in days departed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Apparelled in the loveliness which gleams
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On faces seen in dreams.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A moment only, and the light and glory
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Faded away, and the disconsolate shore
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stood lonely as before;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the wild-roses of the promontory
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Around me shuddered in the wind, and shed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their petals of pale red.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There was an old belief that in the embers
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of all things their primordial form exists,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And cunning alchemists
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Could re-create the rose with all its members
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From its own ashes, but without the bloom,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Without the lost perfume.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah me! what wonder-working, occult science
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Can from the ashes in our hearts once more
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The rose of youth restore?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What craft of alchemy can bid defiance
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To time and change, and for a single hour
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Renew this phantom-flower?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"O, give me back," I cried, "the vanished splendors,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The breath of morn, and the exultant strife,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When the swift stream of life
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bounds o'er its rocky channel, and surrenders
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The pond, with all its lilies, for the leap
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into the unknown deep!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the sea answered, with a lamentation,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like some old prophet wailing, and it said,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Alas! thy youth is dead!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It breathes no more, its heart has no pulsation;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the dark places with the dead of old
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It lies forever cold!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then said I, "From its consecrated cerements
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will not drag this sacred dust again,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Only to give me pain;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But, still remembering all the lost endearments,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Go on my way, like one who looks before,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And turns to weep no more."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into what land of harvests, what plantations
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bright with autumnal foliage and the glow
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of sunsets burning low;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beneath what midnight skies, whose constellations
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Light up the spacious avenues between
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This world and the unseen!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Amid what friendly greetings and caresses,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What households, though not alien, yet not mine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What bowers of rest divine;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To what temptations in lone wildernesses,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What famine of the heart, what pain and loss,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The bearing of what cross!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I do not know; nor will I vainly question
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Those pages of the mystic book which hold
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The story still untold,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But without rash conjecture or suggestion
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Turn its last leaves in reverence and good heed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Until "The End" I read.
</pre>
