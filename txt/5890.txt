<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Taddeo Gaddi built me.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I am old,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Five centuries old.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I plant my foot of stone
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Upon the Arno, as St. Michael's own
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was planted on the dragon.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fold by fold
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beneath me as it struggles.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I behold
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Its glistening scales.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Twice hath it overthrown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My kindred and companions.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Me alone
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It moveth not, but is by me controlled,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I can remember when the Medici
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Were driven from Florence; longer still ago
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The final wars of Ghibelline and Guelf.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Florence adorns me with her jewelry;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And when I think that Michael Angelo
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hath leaned on me, I glory in myself.
</pre>
