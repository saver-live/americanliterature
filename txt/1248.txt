<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I would build a cloudy House
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For my thoughts to live in;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When for earth too fancy-loose
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And too low for Heaven!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hush! I talk my dream aloud,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I build it bright to see,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I build it on the moonlit cloud,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To which I looked with thee.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cloud-walls of the morning's grey,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Faced with amber column,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crowned with crimson cupola
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From a sunset solemn!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;May mists, for the casements, fetch,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pale and glimmering;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a sunbeam hid in each,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And a smell of spring.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Build the entrance high and proud,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Darkening and then brightening,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If a riven thunder-cloud,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Veined by the lightning.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Use one with an iris-stain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For the door within;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Turning to a sound like rain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As I enter in.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Build a spacious hall thereby:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Boldly, never fearing.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Use the blue place of the sky,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which the wind is clearing;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Branched with corridors sublime,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flecked with winding stairs
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Such as children wish to climb,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Following their own prayers.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the mutest of the house,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will have my chamber:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Silence at the door shall use
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Evening's light of amber,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Solemnising every mood,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Softemng in degree,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Turning sadness into good,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As I turn the key.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Be my chamber tapestried
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the showers of summer,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Close, but soundless, glorified
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When the sunbeams come here;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wandering harpers, harping on
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Waters stringed for such,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drawing colours, for a tune,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a vibrant touch.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bring a shadow green and still
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the chestnut forest,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bring a purple from the hill,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When the heat is sorest;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Spread them out from wall to wall,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Carpet-wove around,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whereupon the foot shall fall
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In light instead of sound.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bring the fantasque cloudlets home
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the noontide zenith
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ranged, for sculptures, round the room,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Named as Fancy weeneth:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some be Junos, without eyes;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Naiads, without sources
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some be birds of paradise,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Some, Olympian horses.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bring the dews the birds shake off,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Waking in the hedges,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Those too, perfumed for a proof,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the lilies' edges:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From our England's field and moor,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bring them calm and white in;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whence to form a mirror pure,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For Love's self-delighting.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bring a grey cloud from the east,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where the lark is singing;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Something of the song at least,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unlost in the bringing:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That shall be a morning chair,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Poet-dream may sit in,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When it leans out on the air,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unrhymed and unwritten.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bring the red cloud from the sun
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While he sinketh, catch it.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That shall be a couch, with one
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sidelong star to watch it,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fit for poet's finest Thought,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At the curfew-sounding;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Things unseen being nearer brought
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Than the seen, around him.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Poet's thought, not poet's sigh!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'Las, they come together!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cloudy walls divide and fly,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As in April weather!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cupola and column proud,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Structure bright to see
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gone except that moonlit cloud,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To which I looked with thee!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let them! Wipe such visionings
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the Fancy's cartel
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Love secures some fairer things
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dowered with his immortal.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sun may darken, heaven be bowed
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But still, unchanged shall be,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Here in my soul, that moonlit cloud,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To which I looked with THEE!
</pre>
