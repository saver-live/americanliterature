<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou ancient oak! whose myriad leaves are loud
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With sounds of unintelligible speech,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sounds as of surges on a shingly beach,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or multitudinous murmurs of a crowd;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With some mysterious gift of tongues endowed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thou speakest a different dialect to each;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To me a language that no man can teach,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of a lost race, long vanished like a cloud.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For underneath thy shade, in days remote,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seated like Abraham at eventide
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beneath the oaks of Mamre, the unknown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Apostle of the Indians, Eliot, wrote
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His Bible in a language that hath died
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And is forgotten, save by thee alone.
</pre>
