<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No sound of wheels or hoof-beat breaks
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The silence of the summer day,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As by the loveliest of all lakes
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I while the idle hours away.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I pace the leafy colonnade
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where level branches of the plane
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Above me weave a roof of shade
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Impervious to the sun and rain.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At times a sudden rush of air
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Flutters the lazy leaves o'erhead,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And gleams of sunshine toss and flare
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like torches down the path I tread.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By Somariva's garden gate
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I make the marble stairs my seat,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And hear the water, as I wait,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lapping the steps beneath my feet.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The undulation sinks and swells
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Along the stony parapets,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And far away the floating bells
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tinkle upon the fisher's nets.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Silent and slow, by tower and town
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The freighted barges come and go,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Their pendent shadows gliding down
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By town and tower submerged below.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The hills sweep upward from the shore,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With villas scattered one by one
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Upon their wooded spurs, and lower
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bellaggio blazing in the sun.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And dimly seen, a tangled mass
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of walls and woods, of light and shade,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stands beckoning up the Stelvio Pass
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Varenna with its white cascade.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I ask myself, Is this a dream?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Will it all vanish into air?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is there a land of such supreme
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And perfect beauty anywhere?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sweet vision!&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Do not fade away;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Linger until my heart shall take
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into itself the summer day,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And all the beauty of the lake.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Linger until upon my brain
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is stamped an image of the scene,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then fade into the air again,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And be as if thou hadst not been.
</pre>
