<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Under Mount Etna he lies,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is slumber, it is not death;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For he struggles at times to arise,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And above him the lurid skies
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are hot with his fiery breath.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The crags are piled on his breast,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The earth is heaped on his head;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the groans of his wild unrest,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Though smothered and half suppressed,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are heard, and he is not dead.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the nations far away
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Are watching with eager eyes;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They talk together and say,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"To-morrow, perhaps to-day,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Euceladus will arise!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the old gods, the austere
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Oppressors in their strength,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stand aghast and white with fear
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At the ominous sounds they hear,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And tremble, and mutter, "At length!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ah me! for the land that is sown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the harvest of despair!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where the burning cinders, blown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the lips of the overthrown
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enceladus, fill the air.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where ashes are heaped in drifts
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over vineyard and field and town,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whenever he starts and lifts
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His head through the blackened rifts
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the crags that keep him down.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;See, see! the red light shines!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'T is the glare of his awful eyes!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the storm-wind shouts through the pines
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of Alps and of Apennines,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Enceladus, arise!"
</pre>
