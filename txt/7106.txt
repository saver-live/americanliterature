<p><cite class="al-title">Henry VIII</cite>, alternately titled <cite class="al-title">All Is True</cite> was co-authored by Shakespeare and his successor, <cite class="al-author">John Fletcher</cite>. The Globe Theatre was burned down during its performance in 1613 due to a special effects cannon shot that caught the stage thatched roof ablaze. The title <cite class="al-title">The Famous History of the Life of King Henry the Eight</cite> did not appearing until the play's publication in the <cite class="al-title">First Folio</cite> of 1623. <i>[Portrait of Henry VIII with Charles Quint and Pope Leon, 1520.]</i></p><p><img alt="King Henry the Eighth, frontis" title="King Henry the Eight, frontis" itemprop="image" src="https://assets.americanliterature.com/al/images/play/henry-viii.jpg" style="max-width:350px; float:right; margin-left: 12px; margin-bottom: 12px;" data-pagespeed-url-hash="1682402576" onload="pagespeed.CriticalImages.checkImageForCriticality(this);"> </p><p><a href="prologue">Prologue</a></p><p><a href="act-one-scene-i">Act One, Scene I</a></p><p><a href="act-one-scene-ii">Act One, Scene II</a></p><p><a href="act-one-scene-iii">Act One, Scene III</a></p><p><a href="act-one-scene-iv">Act One, Scene IV</a></p><p><a href="act-two-scene-i">Act Two, Scene I</a></p><p><a href="act-two-scene-ii">Act Two, Scene II</a></p><p><a href="act-two-scene-iii">Act Two, Scene III</a></p><p><a href="act-two-scene-iv">Act Two, Scene IV</a></p><p><a href="act-three-scene-i">Act Three, Scene I</a></p><p><a href="act-three-scene-ii">Act Three, Scene II</a></p><p><a href="act-four-scene-i">Act Four, Scene I</a></p><p><a href="act-four-scene-ii">Act Four, Scene II</a></p><p><a href="act-five-scene-i">Act Five, Scene I</a></p><p><a href="act-five-scene-ii">Act Five, Scene II</a></p><p><a href="act-five-scene-iii">Act Five, Scene III</a></p><p><a href="epilogue">Epilogue</a></p><p style="margin-top: 26px;"> Return to the <a href="/author/william-shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a> library. </p>
