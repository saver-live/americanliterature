<p>Some that have deeper digg'd love's mine than I,<br> Say, where his centric happiness doth lie.<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>I have loved, and got, and told,<br> But should I love, get, tell, till I were old,<br> I should not find that hidden mystery.<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>O! 'tis imposture all;<br> And as no chemic yet th' elixir got,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>But glorifies his pregnant pot,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>If by the way to him befall<br> Some odoriferous thing, or medicinal,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>So, lovers dream a rich and long delight,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>But get a winter-seeming summer's night.<br> <br> Our ease, our thrift, our honour, and our day,<br> Shall we for this vain bubble's shadow pay?<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Ends love in this, that my man<br> Can be as happy as I can, if he can<br> Endure the short scorn of a bridegroom's play?<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>That loving wretch that swears,<br> 'Tis not the bodies marry, but the minds,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Which he in her angelic finds,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:2em;">&nbsp;</span>Would swear as justly, that he hears,<br> In that day's rude hoarse minstrelsy, the spheres.<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Hope not for mind in women; at their best,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Sweetness and wit they are, but mummy, possess'd.</p>
