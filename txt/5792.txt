<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have a vague remembrance
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of a story, that is told
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In some ancient Spanish legend
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Or chronicle of old.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was when brave King Sanchez
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Was before Zamora slain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And his great besieging army
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lay encamped upon the plain.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Don Diego de Ordonez
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sallied forth in front of all,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And shouted loud his challenge
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the warders on the wall.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All the people of Zamora,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Both the born and the unborn,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As traitors did he challenge
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With taunting words of scorn.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The living, in their houses,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And in their graves, the dead!
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the waters of their rivers,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And their wine, and oil, and bread!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is a greater army,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That besets us round with strife,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A starving, numberless army,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At all the gates of life.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The poverty-stricken millions
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who challenge our wine and bread,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And impeach us all as traitors,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Both the living and the dead.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And whenever I sit at the banquet,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where the feast and song are high,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Amid the mirth and the music
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I can hear that fearful cry.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And hollow and haggard faces
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Look into the lighted hall,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And wasted hands are extended
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To catch the crumbs that fall.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For within there is light and plenty,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And odors fill the air;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But without there is cold and darkness,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And hunger and despair.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And there in the camp of famine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In wind and cold and rain,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Christ, the great Lord of the army,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lies dead upon the plain!
</pre>
