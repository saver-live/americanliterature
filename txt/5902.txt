<pre>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In that desolate land and lone,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where the Big Horn and Yellowstone
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Roar down their mountain path,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By their fires the Sioux Chiefs
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Muttered their woes and griefs
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the menace of their wrath.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Revenge!" cried Rain-in-the-Face,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Revenue upon all the race
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the White Chief with yellow hair!"
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the mountains dark and high
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From their crags re-echoed the cry
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of his anger and despair.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the meadow, spreading wide
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By woodland and riverside
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Indian village stood;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;All was silent as a dream,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Save the rushing a of the stream
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And the blue-jay in the wood.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In his war paint and his beads,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like a bison among the reeds,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In ambush the Sitting Bull
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lay with three thousand braves
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Crouched in the clefts and caves,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Savage, unmerciful!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Into the fatal snare
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The White Chief with yellow hair
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And his three hundred men
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dashed headlong, sword in hand;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But of that gallant band
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not one returned again.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sudden darkness of death
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Overwhelmed them like the breath
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And smoke of a furnace fire:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the river's bank, and between
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The rocks of the ravine,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They lay in their bloody attire.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the foemen fled in the night,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And Rain-in-the-Face, in his flight
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Uplifted high in air
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As a ghastly trophy, bore
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The brave heart, that beat no more,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of the White Chief with yellow hair.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whose was the right and the wrong?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sing it, O funeral song,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a voice that is full of tears,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And say that our broken faith
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wrought all this ruin and scathe,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the Year of a Hundred Years.
</pre>
