<p>Follow the trials and tribulations of Henry Fleming, a recruit in the American Civil War in this impressionistic novel by American writer <a href="/author/stephen-crane/bio-books-stories">Stephen Crane</a>. Considered one of the most influential war stories every written, <b>The Red Badge of Courage</b> was published in 1895, a full thirty years after the American Civil War had ended. Although Stephen Crane was born after the war and never participated in battle himself, he was highly praised by the Civil War veterans for having capture a realistic impression of their actual battlefield experiences and emotions.</p><p><b>The Red Badge of Courage</b> treats with the meaning of courage as the young protagonist, Henry Fleming, is cast into a literal "trial by fire" that will take the full measure of his courage. Crane carefully traces the development of our young soldier, detailing the hopes and fears and rationalizations of his career.</p><p>When this novel has been completed and discussed, thoughtful teachers, students, and readers should turn their attention to Crane's "unknown sequel" to the novel; a short story centered on Henry Fleming later in life that allows Crane to get in a last word on the meaning of courage and character of Henry Fleming; <a href="/author/stephen-crane/short-story/the-veteran">The Veteran</a>.</p><p>Featured in our collection of <a href="/the-civil-war-library">Civil War Stories</a> and <a href="/twenty-five-great-american-novels">25 Great American Novels</a>.</p><p><a href="chapter-1">CHAPTER 1</a></p><p><a href="chapter-2">CHAPTER 2</a></p><p><a href="chapter-3">CHAPTER 3</a></p><p><a href="chapter-4">CHAPTER 4</a></p><p><a href="chapter-5">CHAPTER 5</a></p><p><a href="chapter-6">CHAPTER 6</a></p><p><a href="chapter-7">CHAPTER 7</a></p><p><a href="chapter-8">CHAPTER 8</a></p><p><a href="chapter-9">CHAPTER 9</a></p><p><a href="chapter-10">CHAPTER 10</a></p><p><a href="chapter-11">CHAPTER 11</a></p><p><a href="chapter-12">CHAPTER 12</a></p><p><a href="chapter-13">CHAPTER 13</a></p><p><a href="chapter-14">CHAPTER 14</a></p><p><a href="chapter-15">CHAPTER 15</a></p><p><a href="chapter-16">CHAPTER 16</a></p><p><a href="chapter-17">CHAPTER 17</a></p><p><a href="chapter-18">CHAPTER 18</a></p><p><a href="chapter-19">CHAPTER 19</a></p><p><a href="chapter-20">CHAPTER 20</a></p><p><a href="chapter-21">CHAPTER 21</a></p><p><a href="chapter-22">CHAPTER 22</a></p><p><a href="chapter-23">CHAPTER 23</a></p><p><a href="chapter-24">CHAPTER 24</a></p><p style="margin-top: 26px;"> Return to the <a href="/author/stephen-crane">Stephen Crane</a> library. </p>
