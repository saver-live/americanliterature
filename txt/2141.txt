<p><span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>UPON this Primrose hill,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Where, if heaven would distil<br> A shower of rain, each several drop might go<br> To his own primrose, and grow manna so;<br> And where their form, and their infinity<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Make a terrestrial galaxy,<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>As the small stars do in the sky;<br> I walk to find a true love; and I see<br> That 'tis not a mere woman, that is she,<br> But must or more or less than woman be.<br> <br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Yet know I not, which flower<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>I wish; a six, or four;<br> For should my true-love less than woman be,<br> She were scarce anything; and then, should she<br> Be more than woman, she would get above<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>All thought of sex, and think to move<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>My heart to study her, and not to love.<br> Both these were monsters; since there must reside<br> Falsehood in woman, I could more abide,<br> She were by art, than nature falsified.<br> <br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Live, primrose, then, and thrive<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>With thy true number five;<br> And, woman, whom this flower doth represent,<br> With this mysterious number be content;<br> Ten is the farthest number; if half ten<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Belongs to each woman, then<br> <span style="display:inline-block; width:1em;">&nbsp;</span>Each woman may take half us men;<br> Or—if this will not serve their turn—since all<br> Numbers are odd, or even, and they fall<br> First into five, women may take us all.</p>
